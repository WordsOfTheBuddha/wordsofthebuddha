### 1.82

"Bhikkhus, I do not see any other single quality that leads to such great harm as negligence (translation for pamādo: alternatives are carelessness, heedlessness.). Negligence, bhikkhus, leads to great harm."

First.

### 1.83

"Bhikkhus, I do not see any other single quality that leads to such great benefit as diligence (translation for appamādo: alternatives are alertness, care, carefulness, caution, vigilance, conscientiousness, heedfulness.). Diligence, bhikkhus, leads to great benefit."

Second.

### 1.84

"Bhikkhus, I do not see any other single quality that leads to such great harm as laziness (translation for kosajjaṁ: alternatives are inactivity, idleness.). Laziness, bhikkhus, leads to great harm."

Third.

### 1.85

"Bhikkhus, I do not see any other single quality that leads to such great benefit as the \{arousing of energy} (translation for vīriyārambho: alternative is making an effort.). The arousing of energy, bhikkhus, leads to great benefit."

Fourth.

### 1.86

"Bhikkhus, I do not see any other single quality that leads to such great harm as \{having many desires} (translation for mahicchatā: alternatives are greediness, longing and yearning.). Having many desires, bhikkhus, leads to great harm."

Fifth.

### 1.87

"Bhikkhus, I do not see any other single quality that leads to such great benefit as \{having few wishes} (translation for appicchatā: alternative is being content.). Having few wishes, bhikkhus, leads to great benefit."

Sixth.

### 1.88

"Bhikkhus, I do not see any other single quality that leads to such great harm as discontentment (translation for asantuṭṭhitā: alternative is dissatisfaction.). Discontentment, bhikkhus, leads to great harm."

Seventh.

### 1.89

"Bhikkhus, I do not see any other single quality that leads to such great benefit as contentment (translation for santuṭṭhitā: alternatives are satisfaction, having few wishes.). Contentment, bhikkhus, leads to great benefit."

Eighth.

### 1.90

"Bhikkhus, I do not see any other single quality that leads to such great harm as \{unwise attention} (translation for ayonisomanasikāro: alternatives are improper attention, imprudent use of the mind.). Unwise attention, bhikkhus, leads to great harm."

Ninth.

### 1.91

"Bhikkhus, I do not see any other single quality that leads to such great benefit as \{wise attention} (translation for yonisomanasikāro: alternatives are proper attention, prudent use of the mind, wise reflection, attention to the source.). Wise attention, bhikkhus, leads to great benefit."

Tenth.

### 1.92

"Bhikkhus, I do not see any other single quality that leads to such great harm as \{wrong view} (translation for micchādiṭṭhi). Wrong view, bhikkhus, leads to great harm."

Eleventh.

### 1.93

"Bhikkhus, I do not see any other single quality that leads to such great benefit as \{right view} (translation for sammādiṭṭhi). Right view, bhikkhus, leads to great benefit."

Twelfth.

### 1.94

"Bhikkhus, I do not see any other single quality that leads to such great harm as \{lack of full awareness} (translation for asampajaññaṁ: lack of attentiveness, lack of clear comprehension.). Lack of full awareness, bhikkhus, leads to great harm."

Thirteenth.

### 1.95

"Bhikkhus, I do not see any other single quality that leads to such great benefit as \{full awareness} (translation for sampajaññaṁ: attentiveness, clear comprehension.). Full awareness, bhikkhus, leads to great benefit."

Fourteenth.

### 1.96

"Bhikkhus, I do not see any other single quality that leads to such great harm as \{bad friendship} (translation for pāpamittatā: alternative is friendship with unwholesome persons.). Bad friendship, bhikkhus, leads to great harm."

Fifteenth.

### 1.97

"Bhikkhus, I do not see any other single quality that leads to such great benefit as \{good friendship} (translation for kalyāṇamittatā: alternative is friendship with wholesome persons.). Good friendship, bhikkhus, leads to great benefit."

Sixteenth.
