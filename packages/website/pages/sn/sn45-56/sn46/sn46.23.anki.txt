Bhikkhave; Bhikkhus (monks) <sub>Vocative plural, masculine noun</sub>; A common address used in the Pali Canon when the Buddha speaks to monks. <br> Example: "Bhikkhave, by frequently paying attention to..."
Manasikāra; Attention, mental application <sub>Nominative singular, masculine noun</sub>; Refers to the act of paying attention or mental focus, critical in both the arising of hindrances and development of awakening factors. <br> Example: "Manasikārabahulīkārā" – "By frequently paying attention..."
Anuppanno (anuppa + anno); Unarisen <sub>Nominative singular, masculine past participle</sub>; Describes a state that has not yet arisen, often used with mental states like desire, ill-will, etc. <br> Example: "Anuppanno ceva kāmacchando uppajjati" – "Unarisen sensual desire arises."
Uppajjati; Arises <sub>Present tense, 3rd person singular, verb</sub>; A common verb in Pali used to describe the arising of mental states or phenomena. <br> Example: "Anuppanno ceva kāmacchando uppajjati" – "Unarisen sensual desire arises."
Bhiyyobhāvāya (bhiyyobhāva + āya); For increase <sub>Dative singular, noun</sub>; Indicates the increase or growth of a mental state, used with both hindrances and positive factors. <br> Example: "Uppanno ca kāmacchando bhiyyobhāvāya vepullāya saṁvattati" – "Arisen sensual desire increases and expands."
Vepullāya (vepulla + āya); For abundance, expansion <sub>Dative singular, noun</sub>; Used to describe the expansion or abundance of mental states or qualities. <br> Example: "Uppanno ca kāmacchando vepullāya saṁvattati" – "Arisen sensual desire increases and expands."
Saṁvattati; Leads to, causes <sub>Present tense, 3rd person singular, verb</sub>; Describes the result of the arising and increase of mental states, leading to their expansion or development. <br> Example: "Uppanno ca kāmacchando bhiyyobhāvāya vepullāya saṁvattati" – "Arisen sensual desire leads to increase and abundance."
Satisambojjhaṅgo (sati + sambojjhaṅga); Awakening factor of mindfulness <sub>Nominative singular, masculine noun</sub>; Refers to mindfulness (*sati*) as one of the seven factors of awakening. <br> Example: "Satisambojjhaṅgo uppajjati" – "The awakening factor of mindfulness arises."
Bhāvanāpāripūriṁ (bhāvanā + pāripūri); Full development through cultivation <sub>Accusative singular, compound noun</sub>; Indicates the full development of a mental quality through meditation and practice. <br> Example: "Satisambojjhaṅgo bhāvanāpāripūriṁ gacchati" – "The awakening factor of mindfulness reaches full development through cultivation."
Gacchati; Reaches, goes to <sub>Present tense, 3rd person singular, verb</sub>; Used in the context of a mental state reaching full development or a higher level. <br> Example: "Satisambojjhaṅgo bhāvanāpāripūriṁ gacchati" – "The awakening factor of mindfulness reaches full development."
Byāpādaṭṭhāniyānaṁ (byāpāda + ṭṭhāniya); Serving as a basis for ill-will (aversion, anger, contempt, resentment) <sub>Genitive plural, compound noun, masculine</sub>; "Byāpāda" is declined in the **genitive plural**, referring to ill-will or aversion. It is part of the compound indicating the source of ill-will. <br> Example: "Byāpādaṭṭhāniyānaṁ dhammānaṁ manasikārabahulīkārā..." - "By frequently paying attention to the things serving as a basis for ill-will..."

Together, they denote the mental conditions of unease. <br> Example: "Uddhaccakukkuccaṭṭhāniyānaṁ dhammānaṁ manasikārabahulīkārā..." - "By frequently paying attention to the things serving as a basis for restlessness and worry..."
Vicikicchāṭṭhāniyānaṁ (vicikicchā + ṭṭhāniya); Serving as a basis for doubt (uncertainty, indecisiveness) <sub>Genitive plural, compound noun, feminine</sub>; "Vicikicchā" is declined in the **genitive plural** form here as part of the compound. It refers to doubt or uncertainty as a mental quality. <br> Example: "Vicikicchāṭṭhāniyānaṁ dhammānaṁ..." - "Serving as a basis for doubt..."
Satisambojjhaṅgaṭṭhāniyānaṁ (sati + sambojjhaṅga + ṭṭhāniya); Serving as a basis for the awakening factor of mindfulness <sub>Genitive plural, compound noun, masculine</sub>; "Sati" is mindfulness, and "sambojjhaṅga" refers to the factors of awakening. This compound represents the foundation of mindful awareness. <br> Example: "Satisambojjhaṅgaṭṭhāniyānaṁ dhammānaṁ manasikārabahulīkārā..." - "By frequently paying attention to the things serving as a basis for the awakening factor of mindfulness..."
Dhammavicayasambojjhaṅgaṭṭhāniyānaṁ (dhamma + vicaya + sambojjhaṅga + ṭṭhāniya); Serving as a basis for the awakening factor of investigation of mental qualities <sub>Genitive plural, compound noun, masculine</sub>; "Dhammavicaya" refers to the investigation or examination of phenomena (dhamma), and "sambojjhaṅga" represents the factors of awakening. <br> Example: "Dhammavicayasambojjhaṅgaṭṭhāniyānaṁ dhammānaṁ manasikārabahulīkārā..." - "By frequently paying attention to the things serving as a basis for the awakening factor of investigation of mental qualities..."
Vīriyasambojjhaṅgaṭṭhāniyānaṁ (vīriya + sambojjhaṅga + ṭṭhāniya); Serving as a basis for the awakening factor of energy (persistence, determination) <sub>Genitive plural, compound noun, masculine</sub>; "Vīriya" refers to energy or effort, coupled with "sambojjhaṅga," the factors of awakening. It denotes the mental effort that sustains mindfulness. <br> Example: "Vīriyasambojjhaṅgaṭṭhāniyānaṁ dhammānaṁ manasikārabahulīkārā..." - "By frequently paying attention to the things serving as a basis for the awakening factor of energy..."
Pītisambojjhaṅgaṭṭhāniyānaṁ (pīti + sambojjhaṅga + ṭṭhāniya); Serving as a basis for the awakening factor of joy (heartfelt joy) <sub>Genitive plural, compound noun, masculine</sub>; "Pīti" signifies joy or rapture, combined with "sambojjhaṅga" (factors of awakening), representing the joyous aspect of spiritual progress. <br> Example: "Pītisambojjhaṅgaṭṭhāniyānaṁ dhammānaṁ manasikārabahulīkārā..." - "By frequently paying attention to the things serving as a basis for the awakening factor of joy..."
Passaddhisambojjhaṅgaṭṭhāniyānaṁ (passaddhi + sambojjhaṅga + ṭṭhāniya); Serving as a basis for the awakening factor of tranquility (serenity) <sub>Genitive plural, compound noun, masculine</sub>; "Passaddhi" means calm or tranquility, linked with "sambojjhaṅga," indicating the foundational state for mental serenity in the path of awakening. <br> Example: "Passaddhisambojjhaṅgaṭṭhāniyānaṁ dhammānaṁ manasikārabahulīkārā..." - "By frequently paying attention to the things serving as a basis for the awakening factor of tranquility..."
Samādhisambojjhaṅgaṭṭhāniyānaṁ (samādhi + sambojjhaṅga + ṭṭhāniya); Serving as a basis for the awakening factor of collectedness (stability of mind) <sub>Genitive plural, compound noun, masculine</sub>; "Samādhi" refers to concentration or collectedness of mind, representing the foundation of mental focus on the path of awakening. <br> Example: "Samādhisambojjhaṅgaṭṭhāniyānaṁ dhammānaṁ manasikārabahulīkārā..." - "By frequently paying attention to the things serving as a basis for the awakening factor of collectedness..."
Upekkhāsambojjhaṅgaṭṭhāniyānaṁ (upekkhā + sambojjhaṅga + ṭṭhāniya); Serving as a basis for the awakening factor of equanimity <sub>Genitive plural, compound noun, feminine</sub>; "Upekkhā" is a **feminine noun**, used here in the genitive plural as part of the compound. It refers to equanimity (mental balance). While typically a noun, **upekkhā** can also function adjectivally, describing a state of equanimity. <br> Example: "Upekkhāsambojjhaṅgaṭṭhāniyānaṁ dhammānaṁ manasikārabahulīkārā..." - "By frequently paying attention to the things serving as a basis for the awakening factor of equanimity..."
Ceva; And also, indeed <sub>Indeclinable</sub>; A particle used to emphasize or indicate continuity in the Pali language, often translated as “and also” or “indeed.” <br> Example: "Anuppanno ceva kāmacchando uppajjati..." – "Unarisen sensual desire arises..."
Ca; And <sub>Indeclinable</sub>; A common conjunction used to connect words, phrases, or clauses. It is often placed after the first word of the phrase it connects. <br> Example: "Uppanno ca kāmacchando bhiyyobhāvāya..." – "And arisen sensual desire increases..."
Tīti; Thus it is <sub>Indeclinable</sub>; Used to mark the conclusion of a speech or section of text in Pali suttas. It is often found at the end of quotes or explanations. <br> Example: "Upekkhāsambojjhaṅgo bhāvanāpāripūriṁ gacchatī”ti" – "The awakening factor of equanimity reaches full development through cultivation, thus it is."
Sambojjhaṅga (samma + bodhi + aṅga); Factor of awakening <sub>Nominative singular, masculine noun</sub>; Refers to the seven factors of awakening, which include mindfulness, investigation of dhamma, energy, joy, tranquility, concentration, and equanimity. <br> Example: "Satisambojjhaṅgo uppajjati" – "The awakening factor of mindfulness arises."
Āya; For, to <sub>Dative singular, noun</sub>; Used to indicate the dative case, often in the context of expressing purpose or direction, such as "for increase" or "for abundance." <br> Example: "Bhiyyobhāvāya vepullāya saṁvattati" – "Leads to increase and abundance."
Bahulīkārā (bahu + likāra); Frequent application <sub>Nominative singular, compound noun</sub>; Refers to the frequent or abundant application of something, particularly in the context of mindfulness or attention. <br> Example: "Manasikārabahulīkārā" – "Frequent application of attention..."
Ānāpānassati (āna + apāna + sati); Mindfulness of breathing <sub>Nominative singular, compound noun</sub>; Refers to mindfulness meditation focused on the in-and-out breath, considered a key meditative practice. <br> Example: "Satisambojjhaṅgaṭṭhāniyānaṁ dhammānaṁ" – "Mindfulness as a factor of awakening arises through breathing meditation..."
Passaddhi; Calm, tranquility <sub>Nominative singular, feminine noun</sub>; Refers to tranquility or calmness of both body and mind, one of the seven factors of awakening. <br> Example: "Passaddhisambojjhaṅgo bhāvanāpāripūriṁ gacchati" – "The awakening factor of tranquility reaches full development through cultivation."
Kāmacchando (kāma + chanda); Sensual desire <sub>Nominative singular, masculine noun</sub>; Refers to desire or attachment to sensual pleasures, often identified as one of the five hindrances (*nīvaraṇāni*). <br> Example: "Kāmarāgaṭṭhāniyānaṁ dhammānaṁ..." – "Serving as a basis for sensual desire..."
Byāpāda; Ill-will <sub>Nominative singular, masculine noun</sub>; Refers to anger, aversion, or resentment, which is another of the five hindrances (*nīvaraṇāni*). <br> Example: "Byāpādaṭṭhāniyānaṁ dhammānaṁ manasikārabahulīkārā..." – "Serving as a basis for ill-will..."
Thīna; Mental sluggishness <sub>Nominative singular, neuter noun</sub>; Refers to a state of mental laziness or dullness, part of the hindrance of dullness and drowsiness (*thīna-middha*). <br> Example: "Thīnamiddhaṭṭhāniyānaṁ dhammānaṁ..." – "Serving as a basis for sluggishness..."
Middha; Drowsiness <sub>Nominative singular, neuter noun</sub>; Refers to physical lethargy or drowsiness, typically paired with mental sluggishness in the compound *thīna-middha*. <br> Example: "Thīnamiddhaṭṭhāniyānaṁ dhammānaṁ..." – "Serving as a basis for drowsiness..."
Uddhacca; Restlessness <sub>Nominative singular, neuter noun</sub>; Refers to mental agitation, one of the five hindrances. It often arises from an overactive or distracted mind. <br> Example: "Uddhaccakukkuccaṭṭhāniyānaṁ dhammānaṁ..." – "Serving as a basis for restlessness..."
Kukkucca; Worry, remorse <sub>Nominative singular, neuter noun</sub>; Refers to feelings of regret or worry, often paired with *uddhacca* to represent a combined mental hindrance of restlessness and worry. <br> Example: "Uddhaccakukkuccaṭṭhāniyānaṁ dhammānaṁ..." – "Serving as a basis for worry..."
Vicikicchā; Doubt <sub>Nominative singular, feminine noun</sub>; Refers to uncertainty or indecision, particularly about the Dhamma, one of the five hindrances (*nīvaraṇāni*). <br> Example: "Vicikicchāṭṭhāniyānaṁ dhammānaṁ..." – "Serving as a basis for doubt..."
