## Plan: SVG Diagram for MN 148 (Chachakka Sutta)

Create a dark-mode SVG diagram (920×~1500px) visualizing the core teaching of MN 148: the systematic deconstruction of sensory experience into six sets of six, demonstrating not-self, the contrast of personal existence (arising vs ending), three underlying tendencies, and the conditioned process from abandoning to liberation. Uses the established design language: dark-blue background, Georgia serif, gold accents, burgundy-vs-teal contrast, glow filters, lotus motif footer.

### Key Design Notes

1. **English always before Pali** — in all section labels, panel headers, connector phrases, and body text. Exception: the main SVG title where the Pali sutta name appears in the title line per established convention.

2. **Craving is NOT classified by sense object** — The sutta describes craving arising conditioned by felt experience. It does NOT describe "craving for forms," "craving for sounds," etc. Set 6 is displayed as the terminal outcome at each sense door, not as 6 named cravings.

3. **Internal and external bases are independent** — There is no "dependent on" relationship between Sets 1 and 2. Both feed into consciousness (Set 3). The "dependent on both, there arises" connector should only appear between Sets 1+2 and Set 3, showing that consciousness depends on *both* the internal and external base.

4. **Club of three / dependent origination** — For each sense door column, the internal base, external base, and resulting consciousness form a triad. This triad visually converges into contact (Set 4). The "meeting of the three is contact" connector makes this explicit.

5. **Consciousness and contact show English + Pali** — Not Pali-only. Each of the six items under Sets 3 and 4 shows an abbreviated English term (e.g., "eye-c.") above the Pali term.

6. **Not-self logic is based on arising/passing away, NOT affliction** — The argument: "If anyone says, 'The eye is self,' that is not tenable. The arising and passing away of the eye can be discerned. Since its arising and passing away can be discerned, one would have to conclude, 'My self arises and passes away.' Therefore, it is not tenable for anyone to say, 'The eye is self.' Thus the eye is not-self."

7. **Not-self section visually separated** — Entire section enclosed in a containing rect with subtle teal border to distinguish it from the six sets above.

8. **No glow on not-self conclusion** — The ∴ not-self · anattā text is bold teal but without the `filter="url(#glow1)"` wrapper, as the glow was too jarring.

9. **Personal existence includes samanupassati** — "One regards" (*samanupassati*) is the key verb establishing the perceptual mode. Left panel: "one regards" (identification). Right panel: "one does not regard" (dis-identification). This ties back conceptually to MN 1 (uninstructed ordinary person vs. disciple of the Noble Ones).

10. **Person icons for personal existence** — Left panel uses the puthujjana-style icon from MN 1 (head + body + grasping arms + **up/down arrows in coral #c07060**, burgundy #a06070). Right panel uses the awakened-style icon (head + body + calm posture + halo circle, teal #60a088). **The up/down arrows on the puthujjana icon are essential** — they symbolize the turmoil of identification. Left-side UP arrow, right-side DOWN arrow.

11. **Conditioned process boxes are narrower and taller** — The "abandoning ignorance, arousing true knowledge" box and subsequent boxes use narrower widths with text wrapping for more vertical rhythm, rather than stretching full width.

12. **Conditioned process diagram** — Shows the sutta's path: three tendencies abandoned in parallel → merge → abandoning ignorance / arousing true knowledge → disenchantment → dispassion → liberation.

13. **Conclusion quote** — Uses the standalone "by abandoning ignorance and arousing true knowledge, shall make an end of suffering here and now — this is possible" rather than "Birth is ended..." which is less self-contained.

14. **60 bhikkhus removed** — Anecdotal detail, not shown in diagram.

15. **Subtitle alternatives provided** — Four options (A–D) for the subtitle line. English-first options preferred.

16. **Icon placement: LEFT of text, not on top** — Following MN 1 pattern: icon sits to the left, text on the right in two rows (English bold + Pali italic). This is more compact and elegant than icon-on-top-of-text.

17. **Dependent link lines between sets** — Vertical lines per sense-door column run through all six sets, making the dependent co-arising chain explicit. Between Sets 1+2 and Set 3, two short lines per column (one from internal, one from external) converge into one, visually showing the "club of three." Between Sets 3→4, 4→5, 5→6, continuing vertical lines show the chain.

18. **Breathing room between sections** — Each set pair needs ~15–20px more vertical space. The connector text and chevrons should have enough room to breathe. Total height will increase to ~1950–2000px.

19. **Not-self redesign: two-column approach** — Instead of a single wide centered block, show TWO example sense doors (eye = 1st, mind = 6th) side by side. Each walks through the not-self argument for all 6 items in that door's chain. This ties not-self directly to the six sets above, uses width better, and implies the other four follow the same pattern with an "…and so for ear, nose, tongue, and body" note.
