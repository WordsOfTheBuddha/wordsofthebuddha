At Sāvatthi.

Then the venerable Ānanda approached the Blessed One; having approached, after paying respects to the Blessed One, he sat down at one side. Sitting at one side, the venerable Ānanda said to the Blessed One:

“Is there, venerable sir, one quality that when cultivated and frequently practiced fulfills four qualities, the four qualities when cultivated and frequently practiced fulfill seven qualities, the seven qualities when cultivated and frequently practiced fulfill two qualities?”

“There is, Ānanda, one quality that when cultivated and frequently practiced fulfills four qualities, the four qualities when cultivated and frequently practiced fulfill seven qualities, the seven qualities when cultivated and frequently practiced fulfill two qualities.”

“And what, venerable sir, is the one quality that when cultivated and frequently practiced fulfills four qualities, the four qualities when cultivated and frequently practiced fulfill seven qualities, the seven qualities when cultivated and frequently practiced fulfill two qualities?”

“Ānanda, collectedness [born from] mindfulness while breathing in and out is the one quality that when cultivated and frequently practiced fulfills the four establishments of mindfulness, the four establishments of mindfulness when cultivated and frequently practiced fulfill the seven factors of awakening, the seven factors of awakening when cultivated and frequently practiced, fulfill |true knowledge and liberation::emancipation through wisdom [vijjāvimutti]|.

#### Fulfillment of the Four Establishments of Mindfulness

How is collectedness [born from] mindfulness while breathing in and out cultivated, Ānanda, how is it frequently practiced to fulfill the four establishments of mindfulness? Here, Ānanda, a bhikkhu, having gone to the forest or to the foot of a tree or to an empty hut, sits down; having folded his legs crosswise, aligns his body upright, and establishes mindfulness |as his primary focal point::as first priority, directed towards the meditation object; lit. around the front [parimukhaṁ]|. Just mindful, he breathes in; just mindful, he breathes out. Breathing in long, he |discerns::distinguishes, understands, knows clearly [pajānāti]|, ‘I am breathing in long;’ breathing out long, he discerns, ‘I am breathing out long.’ ... He trains thus, ‘While breathing in, I shall |observe relinquishment::watch letting go of craving, attachment, identification with processes [paṭinissaggānupassī]|;’ he trains thus, ‘While breathing out, I shall observe relinquishment.’

When, Ānanda, a bhikkhu, breathing in long, discerns ‘I breathe in long’, and breathing out long, discerns ‘I breathe out long’; breathing in short, <collapse>discerns ‘I breathe in short’, and breathing out short, discerns ‘I breathe out short’; he trains in breathing in while |experiencing the whole body::be conscious of the whole body, be sensitive to the whole process [sabbakāyapaṭisaṃvedī]|, and in breathing out while experiencing the whole body;</collapse> he trains in breathing in while |settling::calming, stilling [passambhayanta]| the |bodily constructs::bodily processes associated with breathing, specifically the in-breath and out-breath. It encompasses the physical movements and sensations that arise from the act of breathing. [kāyasaṅkhārā]|, and in breathing out while settling the bodily constructs—at that time, Ānanda, observing the body in and of itself, the bhikkhu dwells with continuous effort, clearly aware and with excellent recollection, having removed |craving and distress::greediness and dissatisfaction, wanting and unhappiness, craving and aversion [abhijjhā + domanassa]| with regard to the world. For what reason? Because in-breath and out-breath is a certain aspect of the physical process, I say. Therefore, Ānanda, the bhikkhu at that time dwells observing the body in and of itself, with continuous effort, clearly aware and with excellent recollection, having removed craving and distress with regard to the world.

When, Ānanda, a bhikkhu trains in breathing in while |experiencing uplifting joy::heartfelt or intense joy, feeling of love, rapture lit. lovely feeling [pītipaṭisaṃvedī]|, <collapse>and in breathing out while experiencing uplifting joy; trains in breathing in while</collapse> |experiencing ease::contentment, happiness, pleasant abiding [sukhapaṭisaṃvedī]|, <collapse>and in breathing out while experiencing ease; he trains in breathing in while</collapse> |experiencing mental constructs::observe thought formation, be sensitive to the mental processes; (comm) conscious of feeling and perception [cittasaṅkhārapaṭisaṃvedī]|, <collapse>and in breathing out while experiencing mental constructs;</collapse> he trains in breathing in while settling the mental constructs, and in breathing out while settling the mental constructs—at that time, Ānanda, observing the felt experience in and of itself, the bhikkhu dwells with continuous effort, clearly aware and with excellent recollection, having removed craving and distress with regard to the world. For what reason? Because, Ānanda, attending thoroughly to the in-breath and out-breath is a certain aspect of felt experience, I say. Therefore, Ānanda, a bhikkhu at that time dwells observing the felt experience in and of itself, with continuous effort, clearly aware and with excellent recollection, having removed craving and distress with regard to the world.

When, Ānanda, a bhikkhu trains in breathing in while |experiencing the mind::be conscious of the psyche, be sensitive to the mind [cittapaṭisaṃvedī]|, and in breathing out while experiencing the mind; trains in breathing in while gladdening the mind<collapse>, and in breathing out while gladdening the mind; trains in breathing in while</collapse> |collecting::composing, stabilizing [samādaha]| the mind<collapse>, and in breathing out while collecting the mind; trains in breathing in while</collapse> |disengaging::detaching, releasing [vimocayanta]| the mind, and in breathing out while disengaging the mind—at that time, Ānanda, observing the mind in and of itself, the bhikkhu dwells with continuous effort, clearly aware and with excellent recollection, having removed craving and distress with regard to the world. For what reason? I do not declare the cultivation of collectedness [born from] mindfulness while breathing in and out, Ānanda, for one who is |muddle-minded::forgetful, not mindful [muṭṭhassatī]| and |lacking clear awareness::inattentive [asampajāna]|. Therefore, Ānanda, a bhikkhu at that time dwells observing the mind in and of itself, with continuous effort, clearly aware and with excellent recollection, having removed craving and distress with regard to the world.

When, Ānanda, a bhikkhu trains in breathing in while |observing impermanence::watching instability [aniccānupassī]| <collapse>, and in breathing out while observing impermanence; trains in breathing in while</collapse> |observing dispassion::watching fading of desire [virāgānupassī]| <collapse>, and trains in breathing out while observing dispassion; trains in breathing in while</collapse> |observing cessation::watching the natural ending of phenomena [nirodhānupassī]| <collapse>, and trains in breathing out while observing cessation; he trains in breathing in while</collapse> |observing relinquishment::watching letting go of craving, attachment, identification with processes [paṭinissaggānupassī]|, and trains in breathing out while observing relinquishment—at that time, Ānanda, observing the mental qualities in and of themselves, the bhikkhu dwells with continuous effort, clearly aware and with excellent recollection, having removed craving and distress with regard to the world. He, having seen with wisdom the abandoning of craving and distress, closely observes it well. Therefore, Ānanda, a bhikkhu at that time dwells observing the mental qualities in and of themselves, with continuous effort, clearly aware and with excellent recollection, having removed craving and distress with regard to the world.

Thus, Ānanda, collectedness [born from] mindfulness while breathing in and out when cultivated and frequently practiced fulfills the four establishments of mindfulness.

#### Fulfillment of the Seven Factors of Awakening

And how, Ānanda, do the four establishments of mindfulness when cultivated and frequently practiced fulfill the seven factors of awakening? Whenever, Ānanda, a bhikkhu dwells observing the body in and of itself—at that time, |unmuddled::without confusion [asammuṭṭha]| mindfulness is established in him. Whenever, Ānanda, unmuddled mindfulness is established in the bhikkhu—at that time, the awakening factor of mindfulness is aroused in the bhikkhu; he cultivates the awakening factor of mindfulness, and the awakening factor of mindfulness reaches the culmination of its cultivation in the bhikkhu.

While dwelling thus mindful, he investigates, explores, and fully examines that state with wisdom. Whenever, Ānanda, the bhikkhu, dwelling thus mindful, investigates, explores, and fully examines that state with wisdom—at that time, the |awakening factor of investigation of states::the quality of discriminative investigation into states, mental qualities and other phenomena; analyzing the teaching as a factor of enlightenment; second of the seven awakening factors [dhammavicayasambojjhaṅga]| is aroused in the bhikkhu; he cultivates the awakening factor of investigation of states, and the awakening factor of investigation of states reaches the culmination of its cultivation in the bhikkhu.

For him, investigating, exploring, and fully examining that state with wisdom, unflagging energy is roused up. Whenever, Ānanda, for the bhikkhu investigating, exploring, and fully examining that state with wisdom, unflagging energy is roused up—at that time, the |awakening factor of energy::the quality of unrelenting effort, the sustaining power of persistence as a factor of enlightenment; third of the seven awakening factors [vīriyasambojjhaṅga]| is aroused in the bhikkhu; he cultivates the awakening factor of energy, and the awakening factor of energy reaches the culmination of its cultivation in the bhikkhu.

For him, |with energy aroused::with initiative [āraddhavīriya]|, an unworldly uplifting joy arises. Whenever, Ānanda, for the bhikkhu with energy aroused, an unworldly uplifting joy arises—at that time, the |awakening factor of joy::the quality of heartfelt gladness, spiritual rapture untainted by sensuality, as a factor of enlightenment; fourth of the seven awakening factors [pītisambojjhaṅga]| is aroused in the bhikkhu; he cultivates the awakening factor of joy, and the awakening factor of joy reaches the culmination of its cultivation in the bhikkhu.

For him, uplifted by joy, the physical body relaxes and the mind relaxes. Whenever, Ānanda, for the bhikkhu uplifted by joy, the physical body relaxes and the mind relaxes—at that time, the |awakening factor of tranquility::the quality of inner stillness, bodily and mental calm, as a factor of enlightenment; fifth of the seven awakening factors [passaddhisambojjhaṅga]| is aroused in the bhikkhu; he cultivates the awakening factor of tranquility, and the awakening factor of tranquility reaches the culmination of its cultivation in the bhikkhu.

With a tranquil physical body and a mind at ease, the mind |becomes collected::is calmed, becomes composed, becomes stable [samādhiyati]|. Whenever, Ānanda, for the bhikkhu with a tranquil physical body and a mind at ease, the mind becomes collected—at that time, the |awakening factor of collectedness::the quality of unification, stability, and deep stillness of the mind, as a factor of enlightenment; sixth of the seven awakening factors [samādhisambojjhaṅga]| is aroused in the bhikkhu; he cultivates the awakening factor of collectedness, and the awakening factor of collectedness reaches the culmination of its cultivation in the bhikkhu.

He closely observes the mind thus well-collected. Whenever, Ānanda, the bhikkhu closely observes the mind thus well-collected—at that time, the |awakening factor of equanimity::the quality of balanced observation, non-reactivity, and mental equipoise as a factor of enlightenment; seventh of the seven awakening factors [upekkhāsambojjhaṅga]| is aroused in the bhikkhu; he cultivates the awakening factor of equanimity, and the awakening factor of equanimity reaches the culmination of its cultivation in the bhikkhu.

Whenever, Ānanda, a bhikkhu dwells observing the felt experience in and of itself ... the mind in and of itself ... the mental qualities in and of themselves—at that time, unmuddled mindfulness is established in him. Whenever, Ānanda, unmuddled mindfulness is established in the bhikkhu—at that time, the awakening factor of mindfulness is aroused in the bhikkhu; he cultivates the awakening factor of mindfulness, and the awakening factor of mindfulness reaches the culmination of its cultivation in the bhikkhu.

[As with the first establishment of mindfulness, so it should be expanded.]

He closely observes the mind thus well-collected. Whenever, Ānanda, the bhikkhu closely observes the mind thus well-collected—at that time, the awakening factor of equanimity is aroused in the bhikkhu; he cultivates the awakening factor of equanimity, and the awakening factor of equanimity reaches the culmination of its cultivation in the bhikkhu. Thus, Ānanda, the four establishments of mindfulness when cultivated and frequently practiced fulfill the seven factors of awakening.

And how, Ānanda, do the seven factors of awakening when cultivated and frequently practiced fulfill true knowledge and liberation? Here, Ānanda, a bhikkhu cultivates the awakening factor of mindfulness |supported by seclusion::dependent on detachment, by means of disengagement [vivekanissita]|, |supported by dispassion::based on fading of desire [virāganissita]|, |supported by cessation::based on ending [nirodhanissita]|, and |culminating in complete relinquishment::ripening in release, culminating in letting go [vossaggapariṇāmī]|; he cultivates the awakening factor of investigation of states ... he cultivates the awakening factor of equanimity supported by seclusion, supported by dispassion, supported by cessation, culminating in complete relinquishment. Thus, Ānanda, the seven factors of awakening when cultivated and frequently practiced fulfill true knowledge and liberation.”
