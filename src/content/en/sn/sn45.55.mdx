---
title: Yonisomanasikārasampadā sutta - Accomplishment in Wise Attention
description: The Buddha explains the importance of accomplishment in radical attention in the development and cultivation of the noble eightfold path.
qualities: radical attention
theme: inspiration, wisdom
slug: sn45.55
simile: dawn precedes rising of the sun
---

“|Bhikkhus,::::| just as dawn precedes and predicts the rising of the sun, so too does accomplishment in |radical attention::wise steering of attention, root-level attention, attention to the structural source, contextual reflection; lit. attention from the source [yoniso + manasikāra]| precede and predict the arising of the noble eightfold path for a bhikkhu. For a bhikkhu accomplished in radical attention, it is to be expected that he will cultivate and frequently practice the noble eightfold path.

And how|, bhikkhus,::::| does a bhikkhu accomplished in radical attention cultivate and frequently practice the noble eightfold path? Here, |bhikkhus,::::| a bhikkhu develops |right view::view that is in line with the Dhamma - teachings of the Buddha that point to the nature of reality, the ultimate truth [sammādiṭṭhi]| |dependent on seclusion::supported by detachment, by means of disengagement [vivekanissita]|, |supported by dispassion::based on fading of desire [virāganissita]|, |supported by cessation::based on ending [nirodhanissita]|, |culminating in complete relinquishment::ripening in release, culminating in letting go [vossaggapariṇāmī]|; similarly he develops |right intention::intention of renunciation, goodwill, and harmlessness; the resolve to let go of craving, ill will, and cruelty, cultivating thoughts that lead to peace and liberation [sammāsaṅkappa]|, |right speech::speech that is purified by abstaining from falsehood, divisive talk, harsh words, and idle chatter [sammāvācā]|, |right action::action that upholds ethical integrity by abstaining from killing, stealing, and sexual misconduct; bodily conduct aligned with harmlessness and honesty [sammākammanta]|, |right livelihood::means of living that does not cause harm to others or oneself; earning a living ethically without deceit, exploitation, or violence [sammāājīva]|, |right effort::energy and effort directed toward abandoning unwholesome mental states and qualities, and cultivating wholesome ones [sammāvāyāma]|, |right mindfulness::correct active presence of mind and lucid retention; accurately remembering the Dhamma to anchor attention on the body, felt experience, mind, and mental qualities [sammāsati]|, and |right collectedness::correct mental composure that stabilizes the mind and supports clarity of thoughts [sammāsamādhi]|, dependent on seclusion, supported by dispassion, supported by cessation, culminating in complete relinquishment. It is in this way|, bhikkhus,::::| that a bhikkhu accomplished in radical attention cultivates and frequently practices the noble eightfold path.”
