The venerable Gavampati confirms this by quoting the Buddha directly: whoever sees suffering, its arising, its end, or the way of practice leading to its end, simultaneously sees the other three, demonstrating the unified realization of the Dhamma."

At one time, many elder bhikkhus were dwelling among the |Cetās::people of the country of Ceta [cetā]| at |Sahañcanika::name of a Cetan town [sahañcanika]|. And on that occasion, after the meal, when those many elder bhikkhus had returned from the alms round, they gathered in the round pavilion and were seated together when this conversation arose among them:

“Friends, one who sees |suffering::discomfort, unpleasantness, discontentment, dissatisfaction, stress, pain, disease, i.e. mild or intense suffering [dukkha]| also sees the |arising of suffering::source of stress, appearance of discomfort [dukkhasamudaya]|, also sees the |end of suffering::ending of discontentment, cessation of distress [dukkhanirodha]|, and also sees the |way of practice leading to the end of suffering::i.e. the Noble Eightfold Path consisting of right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right collectedness [dukkhanirodhagāmī]|.”

When this was said, the venerable Gavampati said to the senior bhikkhus: “Friends, I have heard this directly from the Blessed One, learned this in his presence:

‘Bhikkhus, one who sees suffering also sees the arising of suffering, also sees the end of suffering, and also sees the way of practice leading to the end of suffering.

One who sees the arising of suffering also sees suffering, also sees the end of suffering, and also sees the way of practice leading to the end of suffering.

One who sees the end of suffering also sees suffering, also sees the arising of suffering, and also sees the way of practice leading to the end of suffering.

One who sees the way of practice leading to the end of suffering also sees suffering, also sees the arising of suffering, and also sees the end of suffering.’”
