title: Ekadhamma sutta - One Thing
description: Cultivating mindfulness while breathing in and out yields great fruit and benefit. This sixteen-step practice guides a meditator from foundational breath awareness to calming bodily and mental constructs, ultimately culminating in the profound observation of impermanence and complete relinquishment.
qualities: breathing, recognition of impermanence
theme: wisdom

At Sāvatthi.

There, the Blessed One said this:

“One thing, |bhikkhus,::::| when cultivated and frequently practiced, is of great fruit and great benefit. What is that one thing? Mindfulness while breathing in and out.

And how|, bhikkhus,::::| is |mindfulness while breathing in and out::mindfulness during inhalation and exhalation [ānāpānassati]| cultivated and frequently practiced so that it is of great fruit and great benefit?

Here, |bhikkhus,::::| a bhikkhu, having gone to the forest or to the foot of a tree or to an empty hut, sits down; having folded his legs crosswise, aligns his body upright, and establishes mindfulness |as his primary focal point::as first priority, directed towards the meditation object; lit. around the front [parimukhaṁ]|.

Just mindful, he breathes in; just mindful, he breathes out.

#### Mindfulness of Body

1.) Breathing in long, he |discerns::distinguishes, understands, knows clearly [pajānāti]|, ‘I am breathing in long’; breathing out long, he discerns, ‘I am breathing out long.’
2.) Breathing in short, he discerns, ‘I am breathing in short’; breathing out short, he discerns, ‘I am breathing out short.’
3.) He trains thus, ‘While breathing in, I shall |experience the whole body::be conscious of the whole body, be sensitive to the whole process [sabbakāyapaṭisaṃvedī]|’; he trains thus, ‘While breathing out, I shall experience the whole body.’
4.) He trains thus, ‘While breathing in, I shall |settle::calm, still [passambhayanta]| the |bodily constructs::bodily processes associated with breathing, specifically the in-and-out breath. It encompasses the physical movements and sensations that arise from the act of breathing. [kāyasaṅkhārā]|’; he trains thus, ‘While breathing out, I shall settle the bodily constructs.’

#### Mindfulness of Felt Experience

1.) He trains thus, ‘While breathing in, I shall |experience uplifting joy::heartfelt or intense joy, feeling of love, rapture lit. lovely feeling [pītipaṭisaṃvedī]|’; he trains thus, ‘While breathing out, I shall experience uplifting joy.’
2.) He trains thus, ‘While breathing in, I shall |experience ease::contentment, happiness, pleasant abiding [sukhapaṭisaṃvedī]|’; he trains thus, ‘While breathing out, I shall experience ease.’
3.) He trains thus, ‘While breathing in, I shall |experience mental constructs::be sensitive to the mental processes; (comm) conscious of feeling and perception [cittasaṅkhārapaṭisaṃvedī]|’; he trains thus, ‘While breathing out, I shall experience mental activity.’
4.) He trains thus, ‘While breathing in, I shall settle the mental constructs;’ he trains thus, ‘While breathing out, I shall settle the mental constructs.’

#### Mindfulness of Mind

1.) He trains thus, ‘While breathing in, I shall |experience the mind::be conscious of the psyche, be sensitive to the mind [cittapaṭisaṃvedī]|’; he trains thus, ‘While breathing out, I shall experience the mind.’
2.) He trains thus, ‘While breathing in, I shall gladden the mind;’ he trains thus, ‘While breathing out, I shall gladden the mind.’
3.) He trains thus, ‘While breathing in, I shall |collect::compose, stabilize [samādaha]| the mind’; he trains thus, ‘While breathing out, I shall collect the mind.’
4.) He trains thus, ‘While breathing in, I shall |disengage::detach, release [vimocayanta]| the mind’; he trains thus, ‘While breathing out, I shall disengage the mind.’

#### Mindfulness of Mental Qualities

1.) He trains thus, ‘While breathing in, I shall |observe impermanence::watching instability [aniccānupassī]|’; he trains thus, ‘While breathing out, I shall observe impermanence.’
2.) He trains thus, ‘While breathing in, I shall |observe fading of desire::watch dispassion [virāgānupassī]|’; he trains thus, ‘While breathing out, I shall observe fading of desire.’
3.) He trains thus, ‘While breathing in, I shall |observe cessation::watch the natural ending of phenomena [nirodhānupassī]|’; he trains thus, ‘While breathing out, I shall observe cessation.’
4.) He trains thus, ‘While breathing in, I shall |observe relinquishment::watch letting go of craving, attachment, identification with processes [paṭinissaggānupassī]|’; he trains thus, ‘While breathing out, I shall observe relinquishment.’

Mindfulness while breathing in and out, |bhikkhus,::::| when cultivated and frequently practiced in this way, is of great fruit and great benefit.”
