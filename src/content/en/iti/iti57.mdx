title: Dutiya āsava sutta - Taints (Second)
description: The Buddha describes the three taints - the taint of sensual desire, the taint of becoming, and the taint of ignorance, and the one who is free from them.
qualities: sensual desire, self-making, ignorance, ending

This was said by the Blessed One, said by the Awakened One, as I have heard:

“There are these three |taints::outflows, discharges; (comm) mental defilements [āsavā]||, bhikkhus.::::.| What are the three? The |taint of sensual desire::oozing sensual desire [kāmāsava]|, the |taint of becoming::effluent of being, taint of existence [bhavāsava]|, and the |taint of ignorance::taint of not knowing how things have come to be, illusion of knowing, distorted perception [avijjāsava]|. These are the three taints.”

The Blessed One spoke on this matter. In this regard, it is said:

“One whose taint of sensual desire is |exhausted::depleted, finished [khīṇa]|,
and ignorance has faded away;
one whose taint of becoming is completely exhausted,
is completely released and |free from attachment::free from grasping, not taking as mine, not appropriating [nirūpadhi]|;
he carries his final body,
having defeated |Māra::embodiment of all forces that keeps beings trapped in the cycle of rebirth [māra]| and his army.”

This matter too was spoken by the Blessed One, as I have heard.
