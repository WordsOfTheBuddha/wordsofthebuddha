---
title: Sutadhara sutta - What One has Learned
description: For a practitioner cultivating mindfulness while breathing in and out, the Buddha describes five qualities that lead to the unshakeable in no long time.
qualities: breathing, sense restraint, wakefulness, learning
theme: wisdom, urgency
slug: an5.96
priority: 1.2
---

“Endowed with five qualities, |bhikkhus,::::| a bhikkhu cultivating |mindfulness while breathing in and out::mindfulness during inhalation and exhalation [ānāpānassati]| in no long time penetrates to the |unshakeable::unassailable, indisputable [akuppa]|. What five?

1.) Here, |bhikkhus,::::| a bhikkhu is |with few involvements::having few activities [appaṭṭha]|, |with few duties::with little work [appakicca]|, is easy to support, and is easily satisfied with the requisites of life;

2.) He eats little food, practicing |moderation in eating::not overeating [anodarikatta]|;

3.) He is seldom drowsy, practicing |wakefulness::lucidity, being awake, being alert [jāgariya]|;

4.) He has learned much, remembers what he has learned, and consolidates what he has learned. Those teachings that are beneficial in the beginning, beneficial in the middle, and beneficial in the end, that are meaningful and well-expressed, that proclaim the perfectly complete and purified spiritual life—such teachings as these he has learned extensively, remembered, recited verbally, mentally examined, and |thoroughly penetrated::completely understood [suppaṭividdha]| |by view::in concept, in theory, in attitude, in philosophy [diṭṭhi]|.

5.) He |reviews::considers, reflects [paccavekkhati]| the extent to which his mind is |liberated::freed [vimutta]|.

Endowed with these five qualities, |bhikkhus,::::| a bhikkhu cultivating mindfulness while breathing in and out in no long time penetrates to the unshakeable.”
