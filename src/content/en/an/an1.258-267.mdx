#### 1.258

“Bhikkhus, of my female lay disciples who were the first to go for refuge, the foremost is Sujātā, the army general’s daughter.

#### 1.259

<collapse>Bhikkhus, the foremost of my female lay disciples</collapse> among donors is |Migāramātā Visākhā::one of the foremost laywomen and devoted supporters of the Buddha, also known as Migāramātā (Migāra’s mother) from a significant event in her life when she skillfully guided her father-in-law, Migāra, towards the Dhamma [visākha + migāramātar]|.

#### 1.260

<collapse>Bhikkhus, the foremost of my female lay disciples</collapse> among those who are very learned is Khujjuttarā.

#### 1.261

<collapse>Bhikkhus, the foremost of my female lay disciples</collapse> among those who dwell in  |loving-kindness::goodwill, friendliness, benevolence [metta]| is Sāmāvatī.

#### 1.262

<collapse>Bhikkhus, the foremost of my female lay disciples</collapse> among those who practice meditation is Uttarā Nandamātā.

#### 1.263

<collapse>Bhikkhus, the foremost of my female lay disciples</collapse> among those who give what is excellent is Suppavāsā, the Koliyan princess.

#### 1.264

<collapse>Bhikkhus, the foremost of my female lay disciples</collapse> among those who attend to the sick is the laywoman Suppiyā.

#### 1.265

<collapse>Bhikkhus, the foremost of my female lay disciples</collapse> among those with unwavering confidence is Kātiyānī.

#### 1.266

<collapse>Bhikkhus, the foremost of my female lay disciples</collapse> among those who are trustworthy is the householder Nakulamātā.

#### 1.267

<collapse>Bhikkhus, the foremost of my female lay disciples</collapse> among those whose faith is based on hearing is the laywoman Kālī from Kuraraghara.”
