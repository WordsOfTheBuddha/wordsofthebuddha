---
title: Ārakkha sutta - Guarding
description: By actively protecting the mind from enticing, irritating, deceptive, and intoxicating phenomena, a practitioner attains freedom from fear, wavering, and they do not go by the mere word of an ascetic.
qualities: passion, aversion, delusion, vanity, diligence, mindfulness, sense restraint
theme: wisdom
slug: an4.117
priority: 1.2
---

“|Bhikkhus,::::| in four situations |diligence::quality of wishing to do one’s work or duty well, with alertness, carefulness and care [appamāda]|, |mindfulness::active presence of mind and lucid retention, recollection, keeping in mind [sati]|, and guarding of the mind should be observed by a sensible person. What four?

Thinking, ‘Let my mind not become impassioned by |enticing::arousing, tantalizing [rajanīya]| phenomena,’ diligence, mindfulness, and guarding of the mind should be observed by a sensible person.

Thinking, ‘Let my mind not become averse towards |irritating::hatred-inducing; lit. cause to be hated [dosanīya]| phenomena,’ diligence, mindfulness, and guarding of the mind should be observed by a sensible person.

Thinking, ‘Let my mind not become |deluded::dull, forgetful [muyhi]| by |deceptive::illusory, delusion-inducing [mohanīya]| phenomena,’ diligence, mindfulness, and guarding of the mind should be observed by a sensible person.

Thinking, ‘Let my mind not |become intoxicated::become arrogant, be with self-importance [majja]| by |intoxicating::befuddling, stupefying [madanīya]| phenomena,’ diligence, mindfulness, and guarding of the mind should be observed by a sensible person.

When|, bhikkhus,::::| a bhikkhu’s mind does not become impassioned by enticing phenomena through being |free from passion::free from intense desire, strong emotion, infatuation, obsession, lust [vītarāga]|; when his mind does not become averse towards irritating phenomena through being |free from aversion::free from hatred, free from ill will [vītadosa]|; when his mind does not become deluded by deceptive phenomena through being |free from delusion::free from confusion [vītamoha]|; and when his mind does not become intoxicated by intoxicating phenomena through being free from intoxication—he does not fear, does not tremble, does not waver, and does not fall into terror; nor does he go by the mere word of an ascetic.”
