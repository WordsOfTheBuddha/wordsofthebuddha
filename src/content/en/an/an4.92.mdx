---
title: Paṭhama samādhi sutta - Collectedness (First)
description: The Buddha outlines four kinds of persons based on whether they possess internal tranquility of mind, the penetrative vision of the Dhammas through higher wisdom, neither, or both of these attainments.
qualities: tranquility, penetrative vision, wisdom
theme: wisdom
slug: an4.92
---

“Bhikkhus, there are these four kinds of persons found existing in the world. What four?

Here, bhikkhus, a certain person gains internal tranquility of mind but does not gain the |penetrative vision of the Dhammas::distinct seeing of the Dhammas, insight into phenomena [dhammavipassanā]| through |higher wisdom::deeper understanding, insight [adhipaññā]|.

But here, bhikkhus, a certain person gains the penetrative vision of the Dhammas through higher wisdom but does not gain internal tranquility of mind.

But here, bhikkhus, a certain person gains neither internal tranquility of mind nor the penetrative vision of the Dhammas through higher wisdom.

But here, bhikkhus, a certain person gains both internal tranquility of mind and the penetrative vision of the Dhammas through higher wisdom.

These, bhikkhus, are the four kinds of persons found existing in the world.”
