    - "[1] Per the commentary, venerable Subhūti was Anāthapiṇḍika’s younger brother and went forth on the day Jetavana Monastery was dedicated to the Buddha. He attained arahantship through developing insight grounded in loving-kindness. Before giving Dhamma teachings or receiving alms, he would first enter collectedness through loving-kindness and then emerge. The Buddha praised him for his peaceful conduct and for living “without contention toward anyone.”"
    - "[2] Per the commentary, venerable Revata of the Acacia forest is the youngest brother of the venerable Sāriputta. Compelled by his mother to marry when still young, he ran away and received ordination."

#### 1.198

“Bhikkhus, the |foremost::chief, best [agga]| of my bhikkhu disciples among those |creating::fashioning, forming [abhinimminanta]| a mind-made body is |Cūḷapanthaka::foremost disciple of the Buddha in creating a mind-made body [cūḷapanthaka]|.

#### 1.199

<collapse>Bhikkhus, the foremost of my bhikkhu disciples</collapse> among those |skilled in mental transformation::skilled in the evolution (lit. turning apart [vivaṭṭa]) of mind. According to the commentary (Mp-ṭ), this means mastery in directing and refining collectedness (samādhi) — the capacity to move the mind from a lesser calm to a greater calm, from a coarse collectedness to one more refined. [cetovivaṭṭakusala]| is Cūḷapanthaka.

#### 1.200

<collapse>Bhikkhus, the foremost of my bhikkhu disciples</collapse> among those |skilled in the transformation of perception::skilled in the evolution (lit. turning apart [vivaṭṭa]) of perception. The commentary (Mp-ṭ) explains this as the ability to refine or shift perception, to go beyond coarse perceptions of form and multiplicity toward subtler perceptions leading to peace. [saññāvivaṭṭakusala]| is |Mahāpanthaka::foremost disciple of the Buddha among those skilled in the transformation of perception [mahāpanthaka]|.

#### 1.201

<collapse>Bhikkhus, the foremost of my bhikkhu disciples</collapse> among those who |dwell without conflict::dwell harmlessly [araṇavihārī]| is |Subhūti::foremost disciple of the Buddha in dwelling without conflict and worthy of offerings [subhūti]|.^[1]^

#### 1.202

<collapse>Bhikkhus, the foremost of my bhikkhu disciples</collapse> among those who is worthy of offerings is Subhūti.

#### 1.203

<collapse>Bhikkhus, the foremost of my bhikkhu disciples</collapse> among forest dwellers is Revata of the Acacia forest.^[2]^

#### 1.204

<collapse>Bhikkhus, the foremost of my bhikkhu disciples</collapse> among |meditators::yogis [jhāyi]| is Revata the doubter.

#### 1.205

<collapse>Bhikkhus, the foremost of my bhikkhu disciples</collapse> among those |with energy aroused::with initiative [āraddhavīriya]| is Soṇa Koḷivisa.^[3]^

#### 1.206

<collapse>Bhikkhus, the foremost of my bhikkhu disciples</collapse> among those who speak eloquently is Soṇa Kuṭikaṇṇa.^[4]^

#### 1.207

<collapse>Bhikkhus, the foremost of my bhikkhu disciples</collapse> among those who make gains is Sīvali.

#### 1.208

<collapse>Bhikkhus, the foremost of my bhikkhu disciples</collapse> among those unwavering in confidence is Vakkali.^[5]^
