<!-- @segment sn22.51:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn22.51:1.2 -->

“Mendicants, form really is impermanent. A mendicant sees that it is impermanent: that’s their right view.

<!-- @segment sn22.51:1.3 -->

Seeing rightly, they grow disillusioned.

<!-- @segment sn22.51:1.4 -->

When relishing ends, greed ends. When greed ends, relishing ends.

<!-- @segment sn22.51:1.5 -->

When relishing and greed end, the mind is freed, and is said to be well freed.

<!-- @segment sn22.51:1.6 -->

Feeling …

<!-- @segment sn22.51:1.7 -->



<!-- @segment sn22.51:1.8 -->



<!-- @segment sn22.51:1.9 -->



<!-- @segment sn22.51:1.10 -->

Perception …

<!-- @segment sn22.51:1.11 -->

Choices …

<!-- @segment sn22.51:1.12 -->



<!-- @segment sn22.51:1.13 -->



<!-- @segment sn22.51:1.14 -->



<!-- @segment sn22.51:1.15 -->

Consciousness really is impermanent. A mendicant sees that it is impermanent: that’s their right view.

<!-- @segment sn22.51:1.16 -->

Seeing rightly, they grow disillusioned.

<!-- @segment sn22.51:1.17 -->

When relishing ends, greed ends. When greed ends, relishing ends.

<!-- @segment sn22.51:1.18 -->

When relishing and greed end, the mind is freed, and is said to be well freed.”
