<!-- @segment sn22.35:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn22.35:1.2 -->

Then a mendicant went up to the Buddha, bowed, sat down to one side, and said to him,

<!-- @segment sn22.35:1.3 -->

“Sir, may the Buddha please teach me Dhamma in brief. When I’ve heard it, I’ll live alone, withdrawn, diligent, keen, and resolute.”

<!-- @segment sn22.35:1.4 -->



<!-- @segment sn22.35:1.5 -->

“Mendicant, you’re reckoned by what you have an underlying tendency for.

<!-- @segment sn22.35:1.6 -->

You’re not reckoned by what you have no underlying tendency for.”

<!-- @segment sn22.35:1.7 -->

“Understood, Blessed One! Understood, Holy One!”

<!-- @segment sn22.35:2.1 -->

“But how do you see the detailed meaning of my brief statement?”

<!-- @segment sn22.35:2.2 -->

“If you have an underlying tendency for form, you’re reckoned by that.

<!-- @segment sn22.35:2.3 -->

If you have an underlying tendency for feeling …

<!-- @segment sn22.35:2.4 -->

perception …

<!-- @segment sn22.35:2.5 -->

choices …

<!-- @segment sn22.35:2.6 -->

consciousness, you’re reckoned by that.

<!-- @segment sn22.35:2.7 -->

If you have no underlying tendency for form, you’re not reckoned by that.

<!-- @segment sn22.35:2.8 -->

If you have no underlying tendency for feeling …

<!-- @segment sn22.35:2.9 -->

perception …

<!-- @segment sn22.35:2.10 -->

choices …

<!-- @segment sn22.35:2.11 -->

consciousness, you’re not reckoned by that.

<!-- @segment sn22.35:2.12 -->

That’s how I understand the detailed meaning of the Buddha’s brief statement.”

<!-- @segment sn22.35:3.1 -->

“Good, good, mendicant!

<!-- @segment sn22.35:3.2 -->

It’s good that you understand the detailed meaning of what I’ve said in brief like this.

<!-- @segment sn22.35:3.3 -->

If you have an underlying tendency for form, you’re reckoned by that.

<!-- @segment sn22.35:3.4 -->

If you have an underlying tendency for feeling …

<!-- @segment sn22.35:3.5 -->

perception …

<!-- @segment sn22.35:3.6 -->

choices …

<!-- @segment sn22.35:3.7 -->

consciousness, you’re reckoned by that.

<!-- @segment sn22.35:3.8 -->

If you have no underlying tendency for form, you’re not reckoned by that.

<!-- @segment sn22.35:3.9 -->

If you have no underlying tendency for feeling …

<!-- @segment sn22.35:3.10 -->

perception …

<!-- @segment sn22.35:3.11 -->

choices …

<!-- @segment sn22.35:3.12 -->

consciousness, you’re not reckoned by that.

<!-- @segment sn22.35:3.13 -->

This is how to understand the detailed meaning of what I said in brief.”

<!-- @segment sn22.35:4.1 -->

And then that mendicant approved and agreed with what the Buddha said. He rose from his seat, bowed, and respectfully circled the Buddha, keeping him on his right, before leaving.

<!-- @segment sn22.35:5.1 -->

Then that mendicant, living alone, withdrawn, diligent, keen, and resolute, soon realized the supreme end of the spiritual path in this very life. He lived having achieved with his own insight the goal for which gentlemen rightly go forth from the lay life to homelessness.

<!-- @segment sn22.35:5.2 -->

He understood: “Rebirth is ended; the spiritual journey has been completed; what had to be done has been done; there is nothing further for this place.”

<!-- @segment sn22.35:5.3 -->

And that mendicant became one of the perfected.
