<!-- @segment sn1.74:1.1 -->

“What’s the best of things that rise?

<!-- @segment sn1.74:1.2 -->

And what’s the finest thing to fall?

<!-- @segment sn1.74:1.3 -->

And what of the things that go forth?

<!-- @segment sn1.74:1.4 -->

And who’s the finest speaker?”

<!-- @segment sn1.74:2.1 -->

“A seed’s the best of things that rise.

<!-- @segment sn1.74:2.2 -->

Rain’s the finest thing to fall.

<!-- @segment sn1.74:2.3 -->

Cattle, of things that go forth.

<!-- @segment sn1.74:2.4 -->

And a child is the finest speaker.”

<!-- @segment sn1.74:3.1 -->

“Knowledge is best of things that rise.

<!-- @segment sn1.74:3.2 -->

Ignorance the finest thing to fall.

<!-- @segment sn1.74:3.3 -->

The Saṅgha, of things that go forth.

<!-- @segment sn1.74:3.4 -->

And the Buddha is the finest speaker.”
