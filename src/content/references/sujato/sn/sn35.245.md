<!-- @segment sn35.245:1.1 -->

Then one mendicant went up to another mendicant and asked,

<!-- @segment sn35.245:1.2 -->

“Reverend, at what point is a mendicant’s vision well purified?”

<!-- @segment sn35.245:1.3 -->

“When a mendicant truly understands the origin and disappearance of the six fields of contact, at that point their vision is well purified.”

<!-- @segment sn35.245:2.1 -->

Not content with that answer, that mendicant went up to a series of other mendicants and received the following answers:

<!-- @segment sn35.245:2.2 -->



<!-- @segment sn35.245:2.3 -->

“When a mendicant truly understands the origin and disappearance of the five grasping aggregates, at that point their vision is well purified.”

<!-- @segment sn35.245:3.1 -->



<!-- @segment sn35.245:3.2 -->



<!-- @segment sn35.245:3.3 -->

“When a mendicant truly understands the origin and disappearance of the four principal states, at that point their vision is well purified.”

<!-- @segment sn35.245:4.1 -->



<!-- @segment sn35.245:4.2 -->



<!-- @segment sn35.245:4.3 -->

“When a mendicant truly understands that everything that is liable to arise is liable to cease, at that point their vision is well purified.”

<!-- @segment sn35.245:5.1 -->

Not content with any of those answers, that mendicant went up to the Buddha and told him what had happened. Then he asked,

<!-- @segment sn35.245:5.2 -->



<!-- @segment sn35.245:5.3 -->



<!-- @segment sn35.245:5.4 -->



<!-- @segment sn35.245:5.5 -->



<!-- @segment sn35.245:5.6 -->



<!-- @segment sn35.245:5.7 -->



<!-- @segment sn35.245:5.8 -->



<!-- @segment sn35.245:5.9 -->



<!-- @segment sn35.245:5.10 -->



<!-- @segment sn35.245:5.11 -->



<!-- @segment sn35.245:5.12 -->

“Sir, at what point is a mendicant’s vision well purified?”

<!-- @segment sn35.245:6.1 -->

“Mendicant, suppose a person had never seen a parrot tree before.

<!-- @segment sn35.245:6.2 -->

They’d go up to someone who had seen a parrot tree and ask them,

<!-- @segment sn35.245:6.3 -->

‘My man, what’s a parrot tree like?’

<!-- @segment sn35.245:6.4 -->

They’d say,

<!-- @segment sn35.245:6.5 -->

‘A parrot tree is blackish, like a charred stump.’

<!-- @segment sn35.245:6.6 -->

Now, at that time a parrot tree may well have been just as that person saw it.

<!-- @segment sn35.245:7.1 -->

Not content with that answer, that person would go up to a series of other people and receive the following answers:

<!-- @segment sn35.245:7.2 -->



<!-- @segment sn35.245:7.3 -->



<!-- @segment sn35.245:7.4 -->

‘A parrot tree is reddish, like a scrap of meat.’

<!-- @segment sn35.245:7.5 -->



<!-- @segment sn35.245:7.6 -->



<!-- @segment sn35.245:7.7 -->



<!-- @segment sn35.245:7.8 -->



<!-- @segment sn35.245:7.9 -->

‘A parrot tree has flaking bark and burst pods, like a lebbeck tree.’

<!-- @segment sn35.245:7.10 -->



<!-- @segment sn35.245:7.11 -->



<!-- @segment sn35.245:7.12 -->



<!-- @segment sn35.245:7.13 -->



<!-- @segment sn35.245:7.14 -->

‘A parrot tree has luxuriant, shady foliage, like a banyan.’

<!-- @segment sn35.245:7.15 -->

Now, at each of those times a parrot tree may well have been just as those people saw them.

<!-- @segment sn35.245:7.16 -->

In the same way, those true persons each answered according to what they were focused on when their vision was well purified.

<!-- @segment sn35.245:8.1 -->

Suppose there was a king’s frontier citadel with fortified embankments, ramparts, and arches, and six gates.

<!-- @segment sn35.245:8.2 -->

And it has a gatekeeper who is astute, competent, and clever. He keeps strangers out and lets known people in.

<!-- @segment sn35.245:8.3 -->

A swift pair of messengers would arrive from the east and say to the gatekeeper,

<!-- @segment sn35.245:8.4 -->

‘Worthy man, where is the lord of the city?’

<!-- @segment sn35.245:8.5 -->

They’d say,

<!-- @segment sn35.245:8.6 -->

‘There he is, sirs, seated at the central square.’

<!-- @segment sn35.245:8.7 -->

Then that swift pair of messengers would deliver a message of truth to the lord of the city and depart the way they came.

<!-- @segment sn35.245:8.8 -->

A swift pair of messengers would come from the west …

<!-- @segment sn35.245:8.9 -->

north …

<!-- @segment sn35.245:8.10 -->

south …

<!-- @segment sn35.245:8.11 -->



<!-- @segment sn35.245:8.12 -->



<!-- @segment sn35.245:8.13 -->



<!-- @segment sn35.245:8.14 -->

deliver a message of truth to the lord of the city and depart the way they came.

<!-- @segment sn35.245:9.1 -->

I’ve made up this simile to make a point.

<!-- @segment sn35.245:9.2 -->

And this is the point.

<!-- @segment sn35.245:9.3 -->

‘City’ is a term for this body made up of the four principal states, produced by mother and father, built up from rice and porridge, liable to impermanence, to wearing away and erosion, to breaking up and destruction.

<!-- @segment sn35.245:9.4 -->

‘Six gates’ is a term for the six interior sense fields.

<!-- @segment sn35.245:9.5 -->

‘Gatekeeper’ is a term for mindfulness.

<!-- @segment sn35.245:9.6 -->

‘A swift pair of messengers’ is a term for serenity and discernment.

<!-- @segment sn35.245:9.7 -->

‘The lord of the city’ is a term for consciousness.

<!-- @segment sn35.245:9.8 -->

‘The central square’ is a term for the four principal states:

<!-- @segment sn35.245:9.9 -->

the elements of earth, water, fire, and air.

<!-- @segment sn35.245:9.10 -->

‘A message of truth’ is a term for extinguishment.

<!-- @segment sn35.245:9.11 -->

‘The way they came’ is a term for the noble eightfold path, that is,

<!-- @segment sn35.245:9.12 -->

right view, right purpose, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right immersion.”
