<!-- @segment sn22.106:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn22.106:1.2 -->

“Mendicants, I will teach you the things that should be completely understood, complete understanding, and the individual who has completely understood.

<!-- @segment sn22.106:1.3 -->

Listen …

<!-- @segment sn22.106:1.4 -->

And what things should be completely understood?

<!-- @segment sn22.106:1.5 -->

Form,

<!-- @segment sn22.106:1.6 -->

feeling,

<!-- @segment sn22.106:1.7 -->

perception,

<!-- @segment sn22.106:1.8 -->

choices,

<!-- @segment sn22.106:1.9 -->

and consciousness.

<!-- @segment sn22.106:1.10 -->

These are called the things that should be completely understood.

<!-- @segment sn22.106:1.11 -->

And what is complete understanding?

<!-- @segment sn22.106:1.12 -->

The ending of greed, hate, and delusion.

<!-- @segment sn22.106:1.13 -->

This is called complete understanding.

<!-- @segment sn22.106:1.14 -->

And who is the individual who has completely understood?

<!-- @segment sn22.106:1.15 -->

It should be said: a perfected one,

<!-- @segment sn22.106:1.16 -->

the venerable of such and such name and clan.

<!-- @segment sn22.106:1.17 -->

This is called the individual who has completely understood.”
