<!-- @segment sn4.8:1.1 -->

So I have heard.

<!-- @segment sn4.8:1.2 -->

At one time the Buddha was staying near Sāvatthī in Jeta’s Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s monastery.

<!-- @segment sn4.8:1.3 -->

Then Māra the Wicked went up to the Buddha and spoke this verse in the Buddha’s presence:

<!-- @segment sn4.8:2.1 -->

“Children bring you delight!

<!-- @segment sn4.8:2.2 -->

Cattle also bring you delight!

<!-- @segment sn4.8:2.3 -->

For attachments are a man’s delight;

<!-- @segment sn4.8:2.4 -->

without attachments there’s no delight.”

<!-- @segment sn4.8:3.1 -->

“Your children bring you sorrow.

<!-- @segment sn4.8:3.2 -->

Your cattle also bring you sorrow.

<!-- @segment sn4.8:3.3 -->

For attachments are a man’s sorrow;

<!-- @segment sn4.8:3.4 -->

without attachments there are no sorrows.”

<!-- @segment sn4.8:4.1 -->

Then Māra the Wicked, thinking, “The Buddha knows me! The Holy One knows me!” miserable and sad, vanished right there.
