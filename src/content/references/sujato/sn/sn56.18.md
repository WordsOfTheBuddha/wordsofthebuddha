<!-- @segment sn56.18:1.1 -->

Then a mendicant went up to the Buddha, bowed, sat down to one side, and said to him:

<!-- @segment sn56.18:1.2 -->

“Sir, they speak of this thing called ‘knowledge’.

<!-- @segment sn56.18:1.3 -->

What is knowledge?

<!-- @segment sn56.18:1.4 -->

And how is a knowing one defined?”

<!-- @segment sn56.18:1.5 -->

“Knowing about suffering, the origin of suffering, the cessation of suffering, and the practice that leads to the cessation of suffering.

<!-- @segment sn56.18:1.6 -->

This is called knowledge.

<!-- @segment sn56.18:1.7 -->

And this is how a knowing one is defined.

<!-- @segment sn56.18:2.1 -->

That’s why you should practice meditation …”
