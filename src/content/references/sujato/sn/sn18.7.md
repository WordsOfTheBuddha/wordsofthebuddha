title: Intention
description: The Buddha teaches Rāhula to contemplate intentions regarding sights, sounds, smells, tastes, touches, and ideas as impermanent, suffering, and not-self—not fit to be regarded as “mine, I am this, my self”—until disenchantment, fading of desire, and liberation arise.
qualities: desire, learned
theme: wisdom, training guideline
---

<!-- @segment sn18.7:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn18.7:1.2 -->

“What do you think, Rāhula?

<!-- @segment sn18.7:1.3 -->

Is intention regarding sights permanent or impermanent?”

<!-- @segment sn18.7:1.4 -->

“Impermanent, sir.” …

<!-- @segment sn18.7:1.5 -->

“… intention regarding sounds …

<!-- @segment sn18.7:1.6 -->

intention regarding smells …

<!-- @segment sn18.7:1.7 -->

intention regarding tastes …

<!-- @segment sn18.7:1.8 -->

intention regarding touches …

<!-- @segment sn18.7:1.9 -->

Is intention regarding ideas permanent or impermanent?”

<!-- @segment sn18.7:1.10 -->

“Impermanent, sir.” …

<!-- @segment sn18.7:1.11 -->

“Seeing this, a learned noble disciple grows disillusioned with intention regarding sights, sounds, smells, tastes, touches, and ideas. …”

<!-- @segment sn18.7:1.12 -->
