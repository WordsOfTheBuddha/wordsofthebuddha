<!-- @segment sn1.31:1.1 -->

So I have heard.

<!-- @segment sn1.31:1.2 -->

At one time the Buddha was staying near Sāvatthī in Jeta’s Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery.

<!-- @segment sn1.31:1.3 -->

Then, late at night, several glorious deities of the host of a hundred felicities, lighting up the entire Jeta’s Grove, went up to the Buddha, bowed, and stood to one side.

<!-- @segment sn1.31:1.4 -->

Standing to one side, one deity spoke this verse in the Buddha’s presence:

<!-- @segment sn1.31:2.1 -->

“Associate only with the virtuous!

<!-- @segment sn1.31:2.2 -->

Try to get close to the virtuous!

<!-- @segment sn1.31:2.3 -->

Understanding the true teaching of the good,

<!-- @segment sn1.31:2.4 -->

things get better, not worse.”

<!-- @segment sn1.31:3.1 -->

Then another deity spoke this verse in the Buddha’s presence:

<!-- @segment sn1.31:4.1 -->

“Associate only with the virtuous!

<!-- @segment sn1.31:4.2 -->

Try to get close to the virtuous!

<!-- @segment sn1.31:4.3 -->

Understanding the true teaching of the good,

<!-- @segment sn1.31:4.4 -->

wisdom is gained—but not from anyone else.”

<!-- @segment sn1.31:5.1 -->

Then another deity spoke this verse in the Buddha’s presence:

<!-- @segment sn1.31:6.1 -->

“Associate only with the virtuous!

<!-- @segment sn1.31:6.2 -->

Try to get close to the virtuous!

<!-- @segment sn1.31:6.3 -->

Understanding the true teaching of the good,

<!-- @segment sn1.31:6.4 -->

you grieve not among the grieving.”

<!-- @segment sn1.31:7.1 -->

Then another deity spoke this verse in the Buddha’s presence:

<!-- @segment sn1.31:8.1 -->

“Associate only with the virtuous!

<!-- @segment sn1.31:8.2 -->

Try to get close to the virtuous!

<!-- @segment sn1.31:8.3 -->

Understanding the true teaching of the good,

<!-- @segment sn1.31:8.4 -->

you shine among your relatives.”

<!-- @segment sn1.31:9.1 -->

Then another deity spoke this verse in the Buddha’s presence:

<!-- @segment sn1.31:10.1 -->

“Associate only with the virtuous!

<!-- @segment sn1.31:10.2 -->

Try to get close to the virtuous!

<!-- @segment sn1.31:10.3 -->

Understanding the true teaching of the good,

<!-- @segment sn1.31:10.4 -->

sentient beings go to a good place.”

<!-- @segment sn1.31:11.1 -->

Then another deity spoke this verse in the Buddha’s presence:

<!-- @segment sn1.31:12.1 -->

“Associate only with the virtuous!

<!-- @segment sn1.31:12.2 -->

Try to get close to the virtuous!

<!-- @segment sn1.31:12.3 -->

Understanding the true teaching of the good,

<!-- @segment sn1.31:12.4 -->

sentient beings live happily.”

<!-- @segment sn1.31:13.1 -->

Then another deity said to the Buddha,

<!-- @segment sn1.31:13.2 -->

“Sir, who has spoken well?”

<!-- @segment sn1.31:14.1 -->

“You’ve all spoken well in your own way. However, listen to me also:

<!-- @segment sn1.31:15.1 -->

Associate only with the virtuous!

<!-- @segment sn1.31:15.2 -->

Try to get close to the virtuous!

<!-- @segment sn1.31:15.3 -->

Understanding the true teaching of the good,

<!-- @segment sn1.31:15.4 -->

you’re released from all suffering.”

<!-- @segment sn1.31:16.1 -->

That is what the Buddha said.

<!-- @segment sn1.31:16.2 -->

Then those deities, knowing that the teacher approved, bowed and respectfully circled the Buddha, keeping him on their right, before vanishing right there.
