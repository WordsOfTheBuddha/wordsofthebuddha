<!-- @segment sn12.59:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn12.59:1.2 -->

“There are things that tighten the fetters. When you concentrate on the gratification provided by these things, consciousness is conceived.

<!-- @segment sn12.59:1.3 -->

Consciousness is a requirement for name and form. …

<!-- @segment sn12.59:1.4 -->

That is how this entire mass of suffering originates.

<!-- @segment sn12.59:2.1 -->

Suppose there was a great tree.

<!-- @segment sn12.59:2.2 -->

And its roots going downwards and across all draw the sap upwards. …

<!-- @segment sn12.59:2.3 -->

In the same way, there are things that tighten the fetters. When you concentrate on the gratification provided by these things, consciousness is conceived. …

<!-- @segment sn12.59:3.1 -->

There are things that tighten the fetters. When you concentrate on the drawbacks of these things, consciousness is not conceived.

<!-- @segment sn12.59:3.2 -->

When consciousness ceases, name and form cease. …

<!-- @segment sn12.59:3.3 -->

That is how this entire mass of suffering ceases.

<!-- @segment sn12.59:4.1 -->

Suppose there was a great tree.

<!-- @segment sn12.59:4.2 -->

Then a person comes along with a spade and basket. …

<!-- @segment sn12.59:4.3 -->

In this way the great tree is cut off at the root, made like a palm stump, obliterated, and unable to arise in the future.

<!-- @segment sn12.59:4.4 -->

In the same way, there are things that tighten the fetters. When you concentrate on the drawbacks of these things, consciousness is not conceived.

<!-- @segment sn12.59:4.5 -->

When consciousness ceases, name and form cease. …

<!-- @segment sn12.59:4.6 -->

That is how this entire mass of suffering ceases.”
