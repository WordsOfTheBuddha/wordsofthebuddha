<!-- @segment sn23.35:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn23.35:1.2 -->

When Venerable Rādha was seated to one side, the Buddha said to him:

<!-- @segment sn23.35:1.3 -->

“Rādha, you should give up any desire, any greed, any desire and greed for whatever is Māra.

<!-- @segment sn23.35:1.4 -->

And what is Māra?

<!-- @segment sn23.35:1.5 -->

Form is Māra. You should give up any desire, any greed, any desire and greed for it. …

<!-- @segment sn23.35:1.6 -->

Consciousness is Māra. You should give up any desire, any greed, any desire and greed for it.

<!-- @segment sn23.35:1.7 -->

You should give up any desire, any greed, any desire and greed for whatever is Māra.”

<!-- @segment sn23.36:1.1 -->

“You should give up any desire, any greed, any desire and greed for whatever is subject to Māra …”

<!-- @segment sn23.37:1.1 -->

“… impermanent …”

<!-- @segment sn23.38:1.1 -->

“… liable to impermanence …”

<!-- @segment sn23.39:1.1 -->

“… suffering …”

<!-- @segment sn23.40:1.1 -->

“… liable to suffering …”

<!-- @segment sn23.41:1.1 -->

“… not-self …”

<!-- @segment sn23.42:1.1 -->

“… liable to not-self …”

<!-- @segment sn23.43:1.1 -->

“… liable to end …”

<!-- @segment sn23.44:1.1 -->

“… liable to fall …”

<!-- @segment sn23.45:1.1 -->

“… liable to originate …”
