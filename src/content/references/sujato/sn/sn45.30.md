<!-- @segment sn45.30:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn45.30:1.2 -->

Then Venerable Uttiya went up to the Buddha … and asked him,

<!-- @segment sn45.30:1.3 -->

“Just now, sir, as I was in private retreat this thought came to mind.

<!-- @segment sn45.30:1.4 -->

‘The Buddha has spoken of the five kinds of sensual stimulation.

<!-- @segment sn45.30:1.5 -->

What are they?’”

<!-- @segment sn45.30:1.6 -->

“Good, good, Uttiya!

<!-- @segment sn45.30:1.7 -->

I have spoken of these five kinds of sensual stimulation.

<!-- @segment sn45.30:1.8 -->

What five?

<!-- @segment sn45.30:1.9 -->

Sights known by the eye, which are likable, desirable, agreeable, pleasant, sensual, and arousing.

<!-- @segment sn45.30:1.10 -->

Sounds known by the ear …

<!-- @segment sn45.30:1.11 -->

Smells known by the nose …

<!-- @segment sn45.30:1.12 -->

Tastes known by the tongue …

<!-- @segment sn45.30:1.13 -->

Touches known by the body, which are likable, desirable, agreeable, pleasant, sensual, and arousing.

<!-- @segment sn45.30:1.14 -->

These are the five kinds of sensual stimulation that I’ve spoken of.

<!-- @segment sn45.30:1.15 -->

The noble eightfold path should be developed to give up these five kinds of sensual stimulation.

<!-- @segment sn45.30:1.16 -->

What is the noble eightfold path?

<!-- @segment sn45.30:1.17 -->

It is right view, right purpose, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right immersion.

<!-- @segment sn45.30:1.18 -->

This is the noble eightfold path that should be developed to give up these five kinds of sensual stimulation.”
