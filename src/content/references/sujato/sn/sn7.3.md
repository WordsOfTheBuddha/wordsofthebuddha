<!-- @segment sn7.3:1.1 -->

At one time the Buddha was staying near Rājagaha, in the Bamboo Grove, the squirrels’ feeding ground.

<!-- @segment sn7.3:1.2 -->

The brahmin Bhāradvāja the Fiend heard a rumor to the effect that

<!-- @segment sn7.3:1.3 -->

a brahmin of the Bhāradvāja clan had gone forth from the lay life to homelessness in the presence of the ascetic Gotama. Angry and displeased he went to the Buddha and abused and insulted him with rude, harsh words.

<!-- @segment sn7.3:1.4 -->

But when he said this, the Buddha kept silent.

<!-- @segment sn7.3:1.5 -->

Then Bhāradvāja the Fiend said to the Buddha,

<!-- @segment sn7.3:1.6 -->

“You’re beaten, ascetic, you’re beaten!”

<!-- @segment sn7.3:2.1 -->

“‘Ha! I won!’ thinks the fool,

<!-- @segment sn7.3:2.2 -->

when speaking with harsh words.

<!-- @segment sn7.3:2.3 -->

The victory for one who understands

<!-- @segment sn7.3:2.4 -->

is that very endurance.

<!-- @segment sn7.3:3.1 -->

When you get angry at an angry person

<!-- @segment sn7.3:3.2 -->

you just make things worse for yourself.

<!-- @segment sn7.3:3.3 -->

When you don’t get angry at an angry person

<!-- @segment sn7.3:3.4 -->

you win a battle hard to win.

<!-- @segment sn7.3:4.1 -->

When you know that the other is angry,

<!-- @segment sn7.3:4.2 -->

you act for the good of both

<!-- @segment sn7.3:4.3 -->

yourself and the other

<!-- @segment sn7.3:4.4 -->

if you’re mindful and stay calm.

<!-- @segment sn7.3:5.1 -->

People inexpert in Dhamma

<!-- @segment sn7.3:5.2 -->

consider one who heals both

<!-- @segment sn7.3:5.3 -->

oneself and the other

<!-- @segment sn7.3:5.4 -->

to be a fool.”

<!-- @segment sn7.3:6.1 -->

When he had spoken, Bhāradvāja the Fiend said to the Buddha,

<!-- @segment sn7.3:6.2 -->

“Excellent, worthy Gotama! …” …

<!-- @segment sn7.3:6.3 -->



<!-- @segment sn7.3:6.4 -->

And Venerable Bhāradvāja became one of the perfected.
