<!-- @segment sn21.12:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn21.12:1.2 -->

Then two mendicants who were companions, protégés of Venerable Mahākappina, went to see the Buddha.

<!-- @segment sn21.12:1.3 -->

The Buddha saw them coming off in the distance,

<!-- @segment sn21.12:1.4 -->

and addressed the mendicants:

<!-- @segment sn21.12:1.5 -->

“Mendicants, do you see those monks coming who are companions, protégés of Venerable Mahākappina?”

<!-- @segment sn21.12:1.6 -->

“Yes, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn21.12:1.7 -->

“Those mendicants are very mighty and powerful.

<!-- @segment sn21.12:1.8 -->

It’s not easy to find an attainment that they have not already attained.

<!-- @segment sn21.12:1.9 -->

And they’ve realized the supreme end of the spiritual path in this very life. They live having achieved with their own insight the goal for which gentlemen rightly go forth from the lay life to homelessness.”

<!-- @segment sn21.12:2.1 -->

That is what the Buddha said.

<!-- @segment sn21.12:2.2 -->

Then the Holy One, the Teacher, went on to say:

<!-- @segment sn21.12:3.1 -->

“These companion mendicants

<!-- @segment sn21.12:3.2 -->

have been together for a long time.

<!-- @segment sn21.12:3.3 -->

The true teaching has brought them together,

<!-- @segment sn21.12:3.4 -->

the teaching proclaimed by the Buddha.

<!-- @segment sn21.12:4.1 -->

They’ve been well trained by Kappina

<!-- @segment sn21.12:4.2 -->

in the teaching proclaimed by the Noble One.

<!-- @segment sn21.12:4.3 -->

They bear their final body,

<!-- @segment sn21.12:4.4 -->

having vanquished Māra with his legions.”
