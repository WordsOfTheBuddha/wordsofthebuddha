<!-- @segment sn1.23:1.1 -->

“Tangled within, tangled without:

<!-- @segment sn1.23:1.2 -->

these people are tangled in tangles.

<!-- @segment sn1.23:1.3 -->

I ask you this, Gotama:

<!-- @segment sn1.23:1.4 -->

who can untangle this tangle?”

<!-- @segment sn1.23:2.1 -->

“A wise person grounded in ethics,

<!-- @segment sn1.23:2.2 -->

developing the mind and wisdom,

<!-- @segment sn1.23:2.3 -->

a keen and alert mendicant—

<!-- @segment sn1.23:2.4 -->

they can untangle this tangle.

<!-- @segment sn1.23:3.1 -->

Those in whom greed, hate, and ignorance

<!-- @segment sn1.23:3.2 -->

have faded away;

<!-- @segment sn1.23:3.3 -->

the perfected ones with defilements ended—

<!-- @segment sn1.23:3.4 -->

they have untangled the tangle.

<!-- @segment sn1.23:4.1 -->

And where name and form

<!-- @segment sn1.23:4.2 -->

cease with no residue left behind;

<!-- @segment sn1.23:4.3 -->

and impingement and perception of form:

<!-- @segment sn1.23:4.4 -->

it’s here that the tangle is cut.”
