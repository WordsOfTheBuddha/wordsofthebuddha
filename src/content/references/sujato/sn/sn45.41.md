<!-- @segment sn45.41:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn45.41:1.2 -->

“Mendicants, if wanderers who follow another religion were to ask you:

<!-- @segment sn45.41:1.3 -->

‘Reverends, what’s the goal of leading the spiritual life under the ascetic Gotama?’ You should answer them like this:

<!-- @segment sn45.41:1.4 -->

‘The goal of leading the spiritual life under the Buddha is the fading away of greed.’

<!-- @segment sn45.41:1.5 -->

If wanderers of other religions were to ask you:

<!-- @segment sn45.41:1.6 -->

‘Is there a path and a practice for the fading away of greed?’ You should answer them like this:

<!-- @segment sn45.41:1.7 -->

‘There is a path and a practice for the fading away of greed.’

<!-- @segment sn45.41:1.8 -->

And what is that path, what is that practice for the fading away of greed?

<!-- @segment sn45.41:1.9 -->

It is simply this noble eightfold path, that is:

<!-- @segment sn45.41:1.10 -->

right view, right purpose, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right immersion.

<!-- @segment sn45.41:1.11 -->

This is the path, this is the practice for the fading away of greed.

<!-- @segment sn45.41:1.12 -->

When questioned by wanderers of other religions, that’s how you should answer them.”
