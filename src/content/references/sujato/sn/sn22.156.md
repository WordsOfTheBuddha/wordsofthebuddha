<!-- @segment sn22.156:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn22.156:1.2 -->

“Mendicants, when what exists, because of grasping what and insisting on what, does theory of self arise?”

<!-- @segment sn22.156:1.3 -->

“Our teachings are rooted in the Buddha. …”

<!-- @segment sn22.156:1.4 -->

“When form exists, because of grasping form and insisting on form, theory of self arises.

<!-- @segment sn22.156:1.5 -->

When feeling …

<!-- @segment sn22.156:1.6 -->

perception …

<!-- @segment sn22.156:1.7 -->

choices …

<!-- @segment sn22.156:1.8 -->

consciousness exists, because of grasping consciousness and insisting on consciousness, theory of self arises.

<!-- @segment sn22.156:1.9 -->

What do you think, mendicants?

<!-- @segment sn22.156:1.10 -->

Is form permanent or impermanent?”

<!-- @segment sn22.156:1.11 -->

“Impermanent, sir.” …

<!-- @segment sn22.156:1.12 -->



<!-- @segment sn22.156:1.13 -->

“But by not grasping what’s impermanent, suffering, and perishable, would theory of self arise?”

<!-- @segment sn22.156:1.14 -->

“No, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn22.156:1.15 -->

“Is feeling …

<!-- @segment sn22.156:1.16 -->

perception …

<!-- @segment sn22.156:1.17 -->

choices …

<!-- @segment sn22.156:1.18 -->

consciousness permanent or impermanent?”

<!-- @segment sn22.156:1.19 -->

“Impermanent, sir.” …

<!-- @segment sn22.156:1.20 -->



<!-- @segment sn22.156:1.21 -->

“But by not grasping what’s impermanent, suffering, and perishable, would theory of self arise?”

<!-- @segment sn22.156:1.22 -->

“No, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn22.156:1.23 -->

“Seeing this …

<!-- @segment sn22.156:1.24 -->
