<!-- @segment sn22.152:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn22.152:1.2 -->

“Mendicants, when what exists, because of grasping what and insisting on what, does the view arise:

<!-- @segment sn22.152:1.3 -->

‘The self and the cosmos are one and the same. After death I will be that, permanent, everlasting, eternal, and imperishable’?”

<!-- @segment sn22.152:1.4 -->

“Our teachings are rooted in the Buddha. …”

<!-- @segment sn22.152:1.5 -->

“When form exists, because of grasping form and insisting on form, the view arises:

<!-- @segment sn22.152:1.6 -->

‘The self and the cosmos are one and the same. After death I will be that, permanent, everlasting, eternal, and imperishable.’

<!-- @segment sn22.152:1.7 -->

When feeling …

<!-- @segment sn22.152:1.8 -->

perception …

<!-- @segment sn22.152:1.9 -->

choices …

<!-- @segment sn22.152:1.10 -->

consciousness exists, because of grasping consciousness and insisting on consciousness, the view arises:

<!-- @segment sn22.152:1.11 -->

‘The self and the cosmos are one and the same. After death I will be that, permanent, everlasting, eternal, and imperishable.’

<!-- @segment sn22.152:2.1 -->

What do you think, mendicants?

<!-- @segment sn22.152:2.2 -->

Is form permanent or impermanent?”

<!-- @segment sn22.152:2.3 -->

“Impermanent, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn22.152:2.4 -->

“But if it’s impermanent, is it suffering or happiness?”

<!-- @segment sn22.152:2.5 -->

“Suffering, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn22.152:2.6 -->

“But by not grasping what’s impermanent, suffering, and perishable, would the view arise:

<!-- @segment sn22.152:2.7 -->

‘The self and the cosmos are one and the same. After death I will be that, permanent, everlasting, eternal, and imperishable’?”

<!-- @segment sn22.152:2.8 -->

“No, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn22.152:2.9 -->

“Is feeling …

<!-- @segment sn22.152:2.10 -->

perception …

<!-- @segment sn22.152:2.11 -->

choices …

<!-- @segment sn22.152:2.12 -->

consciousness permanent or impermanent?”

<!-- @segment sn22.152:2.13 -->

“Impermanent, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn22.152:2.14 -->

“But if it’s impermanent, is it suffering or happiness?”

<!-- @segment sn22.152:2.15 -->

“Suffering, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn22.152:2.16 -->

“But by not grasping what’s impermanent, suffering, and perishable, would the view arise:

<!-- @segment sn22.152:2.17 -->

‘The self and the cosmos are one and the same. After death I will be that, permanent, everlasting, eternal, and imperishable’?”

<!-- @segment sn22.152:2.18 -->

“No, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn22.152:2.19 -->

“Seeing this …

<!-- @segment sn22.152:2.20 -->

They understand: ‘… there is nothing further for this place.’”
