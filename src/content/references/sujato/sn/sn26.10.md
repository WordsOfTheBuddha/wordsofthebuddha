<!-- @segment sn26.10:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn26.10:1.2 -->

“Mendicants, the arising, continuation, and manifestation and regeneration of form is the arising of suffering, the continuation of diseases, and the manifestation of old age and death.

<!-- @segment sn26.10:1.3 -->

The arising, continuation, and manifestation and regeneration of feeling,

<!-- @segment sn26.10:1.4 -->

perception,

<!-- @segment sn26.10:1.5 -->

choices,

<!-- @segment sn26.10:1.6 -->

and consciousness is the arising of suffering, the continuation of diseases, and the manifestation of old age and death.

<!-- @segment sn26.10:1.7 -->

The cessation, settling, and disappearance of form is the cessation of suffering, the settling of diseases, and the disappearance of old age and death.

<!-- @segment sn26.10:1.8 -->

The cessation, settling, and ending of feeling,

<!-- @segment sn26.10:1.9 -->

perception,

<!-- @segment sn26.10:1.10 -->

choices,

<!-- @segment sn26.10:1.11 -->

and consciousness is the cessation of suffering, the settling of diseases, and the disappearance of old age and death.”
