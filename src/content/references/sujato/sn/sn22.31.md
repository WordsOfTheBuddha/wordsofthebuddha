<!-- @segment sn22.31:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn22.31:1.2 -->

“Mendicants, I will teach you gloom and the root of gloom.

<!-- @segment sn22.31:1.3 -->

Listen …

<!-- @segment sn22.31:1.4 -->

And what is gloom?

<!-- @segment sn22.31:1.5 -->

Form, feeling, perception, choices, and consciousness are gloom.

<!-- @segment sn22.31:1.6 -->

This is called gloom.

<!-- @segment sn22.31:1.7 -->

And what is the root of gloom?

<!-- @segment sn22.31:1.8 -->

It’s the craving that leads to future lives, mixed up with relishing and greed, taking pleasure there wherever it alights. That is,

<!-- @segment sn22.31:1.9 -->

craving for sensual pleasures, craving for existence, and craving for nonexistence.

<!-- @segment sn22.31:1.10 -->

This is called the root of gloom.”
