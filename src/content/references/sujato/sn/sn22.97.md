<!-- @segment sn22.97:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn22.97:1.2 -->

Seated to one side, that mendicant said to the Buddha:

<!-- @segment sn22.97:1.3 -->

“Sir, is there any form at all that’s permanent, everlasting, eternal, imperishable, and will last forever and ever?

<!-- @segment sn22.97:1.4 -->

Is there any feeling …

<!-- @segment sn22.97:1.5 -->

perception …

<!-- @segment sn22.97:1.6 -->

choices …

<!-- @segment sn22.97:1.7 -->

consciousness at all that’s permanent, everlasting, eternal, imperishable, and will last forever and ever?”

<!-- @segment sn22.97:1.8 -->

“Mendicant, there is no form at all that’s permanent, everlasting, eternal, imperishable, and will last forever and ever.

<!-- @segment sn22.97:1.9 -->

There’s no feeling …

<!-- @segment sn22.97:1.10 -->

perception …

<!-- @segment sn22.97:1.11 -->

choices …

<!-- @segment sn22.97:1.12 -->

consciousness at all that’s permanent, everlasting, eternal, imperishable, and will last forever and ever.”

<!-- @segment sn22.97:2.1 -->

Then the Buddha, picking up a little bit of dirt on his fingernail, addressed that mendicant:

<!-- @segment sn22.97:2.2 -->

“There’s not even this much of any form that’s permanent, everlasting, eternal, imperishable, and will last forever and ever.

<!-- @segment sn22.97:2.3 -->

If there were, this living of the spiritual life for the complete ending of suffering would not be found.

<!-- @segment sn22.97:2.4 -->

But since there isn’t, this living of the spiritual life for the complete ending of suffering is found.

<!-- @segment sn22.97:3.1 -->

There’s not even this much of any feeling …

<!-- @segment sn22.97:3.2 -->



<!-- @segment sn22.97:3.3 -->



<!-- @segment sn22.97:4.1 -->

perception …

<!-- @segment sn22.97:4.2 -->

choices …

<!-- @segment sn22.97:4.3 -->



<!-- @segment sn22.97:4.4 -->



<!-- @segment sn22.97:5.1 -->

consciousness that’s permanent, everlasting, eternal, imperishable, and will last forever and ever.

<!-- @segment sn22.97:5.2 -->

If there were, this living of the spiritual life for the complete ending of suffering would not be found.

<!-- @segment sn22.97:5.3 -->

But since there isn’t, this living of the spiritual life for the complete ending of suffering is found.

<!-- @segment sn22.97:6.1 -->

What do you think, mendicant?

<!-- @segment sn22.97:6.2 -->

Is form permanent or impermanent?”

<!-- @segment sn22.97:6.3 -->

“Impermanent, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn22.97:6.4 -->

“Is feeling …

<!-- @segment sn22.97:6.5 -->

perception …

<!-- @segment sn22.97:6.6 -->

choices …

<!-- @segment sn22.97:6.7 -->

consciousness permanent or impermanent?”

<!-- @segment sn22.97:6.8 -->

“Impermanent, sir.” …

<!-- @segment sn22.97:6.9 -->

“So you should truly see …

<!-- @segment sn22.97:6.10 -->

Seeing this …

<!-- @segment sn22.97:6.11 -->

They understand: ‘… there is nothing further for this place.’”
