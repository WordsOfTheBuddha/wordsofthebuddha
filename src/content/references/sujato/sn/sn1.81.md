<!-- @segment sn1.81:1.1 -->

“Who in the world is free of strife?

<!-- @segment sn1.81:1.2 -->

Whose life is not wasted?

<!-- @segment sn1.81:1.3 -->

Who here completely understands desire?

<!-- @segment sn1.81:1.4 -->

Who always lives as their own master?

<!-- @segment sn1.81:2.1 -->

To whom do mother, father, and brothers

<!-- @segment sn1.81:2.2 -->

bow when they’re established?

<!-- @segment sn1.81:2.3 -->

Who here, though low born,

<!-- @segment sn1.81:2.4 -->

is bowed to even by aristocrats?”

<!-- @segment sn1.81:3.1 -->

“Ascetics are free of strife in the world.

<!-- @segment sn1.81:3.2 -->

The life of ascetics is not wasted.

<!-- @segment sn1.81:3.3 -->

Ascetics completely understand desire.

<!-- @segment sn1.81:3.4 -->

Ascetics always live as their own master.

<!-- @segment sn1.81:4.1 -->

Mother, father, and brothers

<!-- @segment sn1.81:4.2 -->

bow to ascetics when they’re established.

<!-- @segment sn1.81:4.3 -->

Even though an ascetic is low born,

<!-- @segment sn1.81:4.4 -->

they’re bowed to even by aristocrats.”
