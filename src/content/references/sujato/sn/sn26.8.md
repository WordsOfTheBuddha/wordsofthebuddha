title: Craving
description: The Buddha teaches that the arising, continuation, and manifestation of craving for sights, sounds, smells, tastes, touches, and ideas is the arising of suffering, the continuation of disease, and the manifestation of old age and death—and that their cessation is the cessation of suffering.
qualities: craving, ending
theme: principle, urgency
---

<!-- @segment sn26.8:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn26.8:1.2 -->

“Mendicants, the arising of craving for sights …

<!-- @segment sn26.8:1.3 -->



<!-- @segment sn26.8:1.4 -->

craving for ideas … is the manifestation of old age and death.

<!-- @segment sn26.8:1.5 -->

The cessation of craving for sights …

<!-- @segment sn26.8:1.6 -->



<!-- @segment sn26.8:1.7 -->

craving for ideas … is the disappearance of old age and death.”
