<!-- @segment sn1.19:1.1 -->

“Don’t you have a little hut?

<!-- @segment sn1.19:1.2 -->

Don’t you have a little nest?

<!-- @segment sn1.19:1.3 -->

Don’t you have any networks?

<!-- @segment sn1.19:1.4 -->

Aren’t you free of shackles?”

<!-- @segment sn1.19:2.1 -->

“Indeed I have no little hut.

<!-- @segment sn1.19:2.2 -->

Indeed I have no little nest.

<!-- @segment sn1.19:2.3 -->

Indeed I have no networks.

<!-- @segment sn1.19:2.4 -->

Indeed I’m free from shackles.”

<!-- @segment sn1.19:3.1 -->

“What do you think I call your little hut?

<!-- @segment sn1.19:3.2 -->

What do I call your little nest?

<!-- @segment sn1.19:3.3 -->

What do I call your network?

<!-- @segment sn1.19:3.4 -->

And what do I call your shackle?”

<!-- @segment sn1.19:4.1 -->

“You call mother a little hut;

<!-- @segment sn1.19:4.2 -->

and wife a little nest.

<!-- @segment sn1.19:4.3 -->

You call children a network,

<!-- @segment sn1.19:4.4 -->

and you call my craving a shackle.”

<!-- @segment sn1.19:5.1 -->

“It’s good you have no little hut!

<!-- @segment sn1.19:5.2 -->

It’s good you have no little nest!

<!-- @segment sn1.19:5.3 -->

It’s good you have no networks!

<!-- @segment sn1.19:5.4 -->

And good that you’re free from shackles.”
