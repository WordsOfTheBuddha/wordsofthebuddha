title: Consciousness
description: The Buddha teaches that the arising, continuation, and manifestation of eye, ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind consciousness is the arising of suffering, the continuation of disease, and the manifestation of old age and death—and that their cessation is the cessation of suffering.
qualities: ending, wisdom
theme: principle, urgency
---

<!-- @segment sn26.3:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn26.3:1.2 -->

“Mendicants, the arising of eye consciousness …

<!-- @segment sn26.3:1.3 -->



<!-- @segment sn26.3:1.4 -->

mind consciousness …

<!-- @segment sn26.3:1.5 -->

is the manifestation of old age and death.

<!-- @segment sn26.3:1.6 -->

The cessation of eye consciousness …

<!-- @segment sn26.3:1.7 -->



<!-- @segment sn26.3:1.8 -->

mind consciousness …

<!-- @segment sn26.3:1.9 -->

is the disappearance of old age and death.”
