<!-- @segment sn22.130:1.1 -->

At Varanasi.

<!-- @segment sn22.130:1.2 -->

“Reverend Sāriputta, they speak of this thing called ‘knowledge’.

<!-- @segment sn22.130:1.3 -->

What is knowledge?

<!-- @segment sn22.130:1.4 -->

And how is a knowing one defined?”

<!-- @segment sn22.130:2.1 -->

“Reverend, a learned noble disciple truly understands the gratification, the drawback, and the escape when it comes to form,

<!-- @segment sn22.130:2.2 -->

feeling,

<!-- @segment sn22.130:2.3 -->

perception,

<!-- @segment sn22.130:2.4 -->

choices,

<!-- @segment sn22.130:2.5 -->

and consciousness.

<!-- @segment sn22.130:2.6 -->

This is called knowledge.

<!-- @segment sn22.130:2.7 -->

And this is how a knowing one is defined.”
