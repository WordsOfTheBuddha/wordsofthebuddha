<!-- @segment sn22.133:1.1 -->

At Varanasi.

<!-- @segment sn22.133:1.2 -->



<!-- @segment sn22.133:1.3 -->

Sāriputta said to Mahākoṭṭhita:

<!-- @segment sn22.133:1.4 -->

“Reverend Koṭṭhita, they speak of this thing called ‘ignorance’.

<!-- @segment sn22.133:1.5 -->

What is ignorance?

<!-- @segment sn22.133:1.6 -->

And how is an ignoramus defined?”

<!-- @segment sn22.133:2.1 -->

“Reverend, an unlearned ordinary person doesn’t truly understand the gratification, the drawback, and the escape when it comes to form,

<!-- @segment sn22.133:2.2 -->

feeling,

<!-- @segment sn22.133:2.3 -->

perception,

<!-- @segment sn22.133:2.4 -->

choices,

<!-- @segment sn22.133:2.5 -->

and consciousness.

<!-- @segment sn22.133:2.6 -->

This is called ignorance.

<!-- @segment sn22.133:2.7 -->

And this is how an ignoramus is defined.”

<!-- @segment sn22.133:3.1 -->

When he said this, Venerable Sāriputta said to him:

<!-- @segment sn22.133:3.2 -->

“Reverend Koṭṭhita, they speak of this thing called ‘knowledge’.

<!-- @segment sn22.133:3.3 -->

What is knowledge?

<!-- @segment sn22.133:3.4 -->

And how is a knowing one defined?”

<!-- @segment sn22.133:4.1 -->

“Reverend, a learned noble disciple truly understands the gratification, the drawback, and the escape when it comes to form,

<!-- @segment sn22.133:4.2 -->

feeling,

<!-- @segment sn22.133:4.3 -->

perception,

<!-- @segment sn22.133:4.4 -->

choices,

<!-- @segment sn22.133:4.5 -->

and consciousness.

<!-- @segment sn22.133:4.6 -->

This is called knowledge.

<!-- @segment sn22.133:4.7 -->

And this is how a knowing one is defined.”
