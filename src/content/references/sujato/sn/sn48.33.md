<!-- @segment sn48.33:1.1 -->

“Mendicants, there are these five faculties.

<!-- @segment sn48.33:1.2 -->

What five?

<!-- @segment sn48.33:1.3 -->

The faculties of pleasure, pain, happiness, sadness, and equanimity.

<!-- @segment sn48.33:1.4 -->

A mendicant comes to be freed by not grasping after truly understanding these five faculties’ origin, disappearance, gratification, drawback, and escape.

<!-- @segment sn48.33:1.5 -->

Such a mendicant is called a perfected one, with defilements ended, who has completed the spiritual journey, done what had to be done, laid down the burden, achieved their heart’s goal, utterly ended the fetter of continued existence, and is rightly freed through enlightenment.”
