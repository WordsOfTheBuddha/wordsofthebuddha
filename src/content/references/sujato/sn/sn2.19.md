<!-- @segment sn2.19:1.1 -->

At Rājagaha.

<!-- @segment sn2.19:1.2 -->

Standing to one side, the godling Uttara spoke this verse in the Buddha’s presence:

<!-- @segment sn2.19:2.1 -->

“This life, so very short, is led onward.

<!-- @segment sn2.19:2.2 -->

One led on to old age has no shelter.

<!-- @segment sn2.19:2.3 -->

Seeing this peril in death,

<!-- @segment sn2.19:2.4 -->

do good deeds that bring you joy.”

<!-- @segment sn2.19:3.1 -->

“This life, so very short, is led onward.

<!-- @segment sn2.19:3.2 -->

One led on to old age has no shelter.

<!-- @segment sn2.19:3.3 -->

Seeing this peril in death,

<!-- @segment sn2.19:3.4 -->

a seeker of peace would drop the world’s bait.”
