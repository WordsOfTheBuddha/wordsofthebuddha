title: The Explanation of the Hundred and Eight
description: The Buddha explains that he has taught feelings in many ways—two, three, five, six, eighteen, thirty-six, or a hundred and eight—and that those who accept the well-spoken teaching live in harmony rather than dispute.
qualities: equanimity, happiness, displeasure, suffering

<!-- @segment sn36.22:1.1 -->

“Mendicants, I will teach you an exposition of the teaching on the hundred and eight.

<!-- @segment sn36.22:1.2 -->

Listen …

<!-- @segment sn36.22:1.3 -->

And what is the exposition of the teaching on the hundred and eight?

<!-- @segment sn36.22:1.4 -->

Mendicants, in one explanation I’ve spoken of two feelings. In another explanation I’ve spoken of three feelings, or five, six, eighteen, thirty-six, or a hundred and eight feelings.

<!-- @segment sn36.22:1.5 -->



<!-- @segment sn36.22:1.6 -->



<!-- @segment sn36.22:1.7 -->



<!-- @segment sn36.22:1.8 -->



<!-- @segment sn36.22:1.9 -->



<!-- @segment sn36.22:1.10 -->



<!-- @segment sn36.22:2.1 -->

And what are the two feelings?

<!-- @segment sn36.22:2.2 -->

Physical and mental.

<!-- @segment sn36.22:2.3 -->

These are called the two feelings.

<!-- @segment sn36.22:2.4 -->

And what are the three feelings?

<!-- @segment sn36.22:2.5 -->

Pleasant, painful, and neutral feelings. …

<!-- @segment sn36.22:2.6 -->



<!-- @segment sn36.22:2.7 -->

And what are the five feelings?

<!-- @segment sn36.22:2.8 -->

The faculties of pleasure, pain, happiness, sadness, and equanimity. …

<!-- @segment sn36.22:2.9 -->



<!-- @segment sn36.22:2.10 -->

And what are the six feelings?

<!-- @segment sn36.22:2.11 -->

Feeling born of eye contact … ear contact … nose contact … tongue contact … body contact …

<!-- @segment sn36.22:2.12 -->

mind contact. …

<!-- @segment sn36.22:2.13 -->



<!-- @segment sn36.22:2.14 -->

And what are the eighteen feelings?

<!-- @segment sn36.22:2.15 -->

There are six preoccupations with happiness, six preoccupations with sadness, and six preoccupations with equanimity. …

<!-- @segment sn36.22:2.16 -->



<!-- @segment sn36.22:2.17 -->

And what are the thirty-six feelings?

<!-- @segment sn36.22:2.18 -->

Six kinds of domestic happiness and six kinds of renunciate happiness. Six kinds of domestic sadness and six kinds of renunciate sadness. Six kinds of domestic equanimity and six kinds of renunciate equanimity. …

<!-- @segment sn36.22:2.19 -->



<!-- @segment sn36.22:2.20 -->

And what are the hundred and eight feelings?

<!-- @segment sn36.22:2.21 -->

Thirty six feelings in the past, future, and present.

<!-- @segment sn36.22:2.22 -->

These are called the hundred and eight feelings.

<!-- @segment sn36.22:2.23 -->

This is the exposition of the teaching on the hundred and eight.”
