<!-- @segment sn22.83:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn22.83:1.2 -->

There Ānanda addressed the mendicants:

<!-- @segment sn22.83:1.3 -->

“Reverends, mendicants!”

<!-- @segment sn22.83:1.4 -->

“Reverend,” they replied.

<!-- @segment sn22.83:1.5 -->

Ānanda said this:

<!-- @segment sn22.83:2.1 -->

“Reverends, the venerable named Puṇṇa son of Mantāṇī was very helpful to me when I was just ordained.

<!-- @segment sn22.83:2.2 -->

He gave me this advice:

<!-- @segment sn22.83:2.3 -->

‘Reverend Ānanda, the thought “I am” occurs because of grasping, not without grasping.

<!-- @segment sn22.83:2.4 -->

Grasping what?

<!-- @segment sn22.83:2.5 -->

The thought “I am” occurs because of grasping form,

<!-- @segment sn22.83:2.6 -->

feeling,

<!-- @segment sn22.83:2.7 -->

perception,

<!-- @segment sn22.83:2.8 -->

choices,

<!-- @segment sn22.83:2.9 -->

and consciousness, not without grasping.

<!-- @segment sn22.83:3.1 -->

Suppose there was a woman or man who was young, youthful, and fond of adornments, and they check their own reflection in a clean bright mirror or a clear bowl of water. They’d look because of grasping, not without grasping.

<!-- @segment sn22.83:3.2 -->

In the same way, the thought “I am” occurs because of grasping form,

<!-- @segment sn22.83:3.3 -->

feeling,

<!-- @segment sn22.83:3.4 -->

perception,

<!-- @segment sn22.83:3.5 -->

choices,

<!-- @segment sn22.83:3.6 -->

and consciousness, not without grasping.

<!-- @segment sn22.83:4.1 -->

What do you think, Reverend Ānanda?

<!-- @segment sn22.83:4.2 -->

Is form permanent or impermanent?’

<!-- @segment sn22.83:4.3 -->

‘Impermanent, reverend.’

<!-- @segment sn22.83:4.4 -->

‘Is feeling …

<!-- @segment sn22.83:4.5 -->

perception …

<!-- @segment sn22.83:4.6 -->

choices …

<!-- @segment sn22.83:4.7 -->

consciousness permanent or impermanent?’

<!-- @segment sn22.83:4.8 -->

‘Impermanent, reverend.’ …

<!-- @segment sn22.83:4.9 -->

‘So you should truly see …

<!-- @segment sn22.83:4.10 -->

Seeing this …

<!-- @segment sn22.83:4.11 -->

They understand: “… there is nothing further for this place.”’

<!-- @segment sn22.83:4.12 -->

Reverends, the venerable named Puṇṇa son of Mantāṇī was very helpful to me when I was just ordained.

<!-- @segment sn22.83:4.13 -->

He gave me this advice.

<!-- @segment sn22.83:4.14 -->

And when I heard this teaching from Venerable Puṇṇa son of Mantāṇī, I comprehended the teaching.”
