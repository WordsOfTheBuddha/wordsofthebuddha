<!-- @segment sn4.3:1.1 -->

So I have heard.

<!-- @segment sn4.3:1.2 -->

At one time, when he was first awakened, the Buddha was staying in Uruvelā at the root of the goatherd’s banyan tree on the bank of the Nerañjarā River.

<!-- @segment sn4.3:1.3 -->

Now at that time the Buddha was meditating in the open during the dark of night, while a gentle rain drizzled down.

<!-- @segment sn4.3:1.4 -->

Then Māra the Wicked, wanting to make the Buddha feel fear, terror, and goosebumps, approached him, and while not far away, displayed a rainbow of bright colors, both beautiful and ugly.

<!-- @segment sn4.3:1.5 -->

Then the Buddha, knowing that this was Māra the Wicked, replied to him in verse:

<!-- @segment sn4.3:2.1 -->

“Transmigrating for such a long time,

<!-- @segment sn4.3:2.2 -->

you’ve made forms beautiful and ugly.

<!-- @segment sn4.3:2.3 -->

Enough of this, Wicked One!

<!-- @segment sn4.3:2.4 -->

You’re beaten, terminator.

<!-- @segment sn4.3:3.1 -->

Those who are well restrained

<!-- @segment sn4.3:3.2 -->

in body, speech, and mind

<!-- @segment sn4.3:3.3 -->

don’t fall under Māra’s sway,

<!-- @segment sn4.3:3.4 -->

nor are they your footmen.”

<!-- @segment sn4.3:4.1 -->

Then Māra … vanished right there.
