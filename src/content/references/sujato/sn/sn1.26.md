<!-- @segment sn1.26:1.1 -->

“How many lamps are there

<!-- @segment sn1.26:1.2 -->

that light up the world?

<!-- @segment sn1.26:1.3 -->

We’ve come to ask you, worthy sir:

<!-- @segment sn1.26:1.4 -->

how are we to understand this?”

<!-- @segment sn1.26:2.1 -->

“There are four lamps in the world,

<!-- @segment sn1.26:2.2 -->

a fifth is not found.

<!-- @segment sn1.26:2.3 -->

The sun blazes by day,

<!-- @segment sn1.26:2.4 -->

the moon radiates at night,

<!-- @segment sn1.26:3.1 -->

while a fire illuminates both

<!-- @segment sn1.26:3.2 -->

by day and by night.

<!-- @segment sn1.26:3.3 -->

But a Buddha is the best of blazes:

<!-- @segment sn1.26:3.4 -->

this is the supreme radiance.”
