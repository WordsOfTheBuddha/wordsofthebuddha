<!-- @segment sn45.26:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn45.26:1.2 -->

“Mendicants, I will teach you an untrue person and an even more untrue person,

<!-- @segment sn45.26:1.3 -->

a true person and an even truer person.

<!-- @segment sn45.26:1.4 -->

Listen …

<!-- @segment sn45.26:1.5 -->

And what is an untrue person?

<!-- @segment sn45.26:1.6 -->

It’s someone who has wrong view, wrong purpose, wrong speech, wrong action, wrong livelihood, wrong effort, wrong mindfulness, and wrong immersion.

<!-- @segment sn45.26:1.7 -->

This is called an untrue person.

<!-- @segment sn45.26:2.1 -->

And what is an even more untrue person?

<!-- @segment sn45.26:2.2 -->

It’s someone who has wrong view, wrong purpose, wrong speech, wrong action, wrong livelihood, wrong effort, wrong mindfulness, wrong immersion, wrong knowledge, and wrong freedom.

<!-- @segment sn45.26:2.3 -->

This is called an even more untrue person.

<!-- @segment sn45.26:3.1 -->

And what is a true person?

<!-- @segment sn45.26:3.2 -->

It’s someone who has right view, right purpose, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right immersion.

<!-- @segment sn45.26:3.3 -->

This is called a true person.

<!-- @segment sn45.26:4.1 -->

And what is an even truer person?

<!-- @segment sn45.26:4.2 -->

It’s someone who has right view, right purpose, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, right immersion, right knowledge, and right freedom.

<!-- @segment sn45.26:4.3 -->

This is called an even truer person.”
