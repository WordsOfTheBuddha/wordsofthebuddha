title: Contact
description: The Buddha teaches Rāhula to contemplate eye, ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind contact as impermanent, suffering, and not-self—not fit to be regarded as “mine, I am this, my self”—until disenchantment, fading of desire, and liberation arise.
qualities: learning, desire
theme: wisdom, training guideline
---

<!-- @segment sn18.4:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn18.4:1.2 -->

“What do you think, Rāhula?

<!-- @segment sn18.4:1.3 -->

Is eye contact permanent or impermanent?”

<!-- @segment sn18.4:1.4 -->

“Impermanent, sir.” …

<!-- @segment sn18.4:1.5 -->

“… ear contact …

<!-- @segment sn18.4:1.6 -->

nose contact …

<!-- @segment sn18.4:1.7 -->

tongue contact …

<!-- @segment sn18.4:1.8 -->

body contact …

<!-- @segment sn18.4:1.9 -->

Is mind contact permanent or impermanent?”

<!-- @segment sn18.4:1.10 -->

“Impermanent, sir.” …

<!-- @segment sn18.4:1.11 -->

“Seeing this, a learned noble disciple grows disillusioned with eye contact, ear contact, nose contact, tongue contact, body contact, and mind contact.

<!-- @segment sn18.4:1.12 -->

Being disillusioned, desire fades away. …”
