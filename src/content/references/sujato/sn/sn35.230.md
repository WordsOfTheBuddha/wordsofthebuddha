title: The Simile of the Angler

<!-- @segment sn35.230:1.1 -->

“Mendicants, suppose an angler was to cast a baited hook into a deep lake.

<!-- @segment sn35.230:1.2 -->

Seeing the bait, a fish would swallow it.

<!-- @segment sn35.230:1.3 -->

And so the fish that swallowed the hook would meet with tragedy and disaster, and the angler can do what he wants with it.

<!-- @segment sn35.230:2.1 -->

In the same way, there are these six hooks in the world that mean tragedy and slaughter for living creatures.

<!-- @segment sn35.230:2.2 -->

What six?

<!-- @segment sn35.230:2.3 -->

There are sights known by the eye, which are likable, desirable, agreeable, pleasant, sensual, and arousing.

<!-- @segment sn35.230:2.4 -->

If a mendicant approves, welcomes, and keeps clinging to them,

<!-- @segment sn35.230:2.5 -->

they’re called a mendicant who has swallowed Māra’s hook. They’ve met with tragedy and disaster, and the Wicked One can do with them what he wants.

<!-- @segment sn35.230:2.6 -->

There are sounds … smells … tastes … touches …

<!-- @segment sn35.230:3.1 -->

ideas known by the mind, which are likable, desirable, agreeable, pleasant, sensual, and arousing.

<!-- @segment sn35.230:3.2 -->

If a mendicant approves, welcomes, and keeps clinging to them,

<!-- @segment sn35.230:3.3 -->

they’re called a mendicant who has swallowed Māra’s hook. They’ve met with tragedy and disaster, and the Wicked One can do with them what he wants.

<!-- @segment sn35.230:4.1 -->

There are sights known by the eye, which are likable, desirable, agreeable, pleasant, sensual, and arousing.

<!-- @segment sn35.230:4.2 -->

If a mendicant doesn’t approve, welcome, and keep clinging to them, they’re called a mendicant who hasn’t swallowed Māra’s hook. They’ve broken the hook, destroyed it. They haven’t met with tragedy and disaster, and the Wicked One cannot do with them what he wants.

<!-- @segment sn35.230:5.1 -->

There are sounds … smells … tastes … touches …

<!-- @segment sn35.230:5.2 -->

ideas known by the mind, which are likable, desirable, agreeable, pleasant, sensual, and arousing.

<!-- @segment sn35.230:5.3 -->

If a mendicant doesn’t approve, welcome, and keep clinging to them, they’re called a mendicant who hasn’t swallowed Māra’s hook. They’ve broken the hook, destroyed it. They haven’t met with tragedy and disaster, and the Wicked One cannot do with them what he wants.”
