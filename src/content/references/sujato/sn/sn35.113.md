<!-- @segment sn35.113:1.1 -->

At one time the Buddha was staying at Ñātika in the brick house.

<!-- @segment sn35.113:1.2 -->

Then while the Buddha was in private retreat he spoke this exposition of the teaching:

<!-- @segment sn35.113:1.3 -->

“Eye consciousness arises dependent on the eye and sights. The meeting of the three is contact.

<!-- @segment sn35.113:1.4 -->

Contact is a requirement for feeling.

<!-- @segment sn35.113:1.5 -->

Feeling is a requirement for craving.

<!-- @segment sn35.113:1.6 -->

Craving is a requirement for grasping.

<!-- @segment sn35.113:1.7 -->

Grasping is a requirement for continued existence.

<!-- @segment sn35.113:1.8 -->

Continued existence is a requirement for rebirth.

<!-- @segment sn35.113:1.9 -->

Rebirth is a requirement for old age and death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, sadness, and distress to come to be.

<!-- @segment sn35.113:1.10 -->

That is how this entire mass of suffering originates.

<!-- @segment sn35.113:1.11 -->

Ear … nose … tongue … body …

<!-- @segment sn35.113:1.12 -->

Mind consciousness arises dependent on the mind and ideas. The meeting of the three is contact.

<!-- @segment sn35.113:1.13 -->

Contact is a requirement for feeling.

<!-- @segment sn35.113:1.14 -->

Feeling is a requirement for craving.

<!-- @segment sn35.113:1.15 -->

Craving is a requirement for grasping.

<!-- @segment sn35.113:1.16 -->

Grasping is a requirement for continued existence.

<!-- @segment sn35.113:1.17 -->

Continued existence is a requirement for rebirth.

<!-- @segment sn35.113:1.18 -->

Rebirth is a requirement for old age and death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, sadness, and distress to come to be.

<!-- @segment sn35.113:1.19 -->

That is how this entire mass of suffering originates.

<!-- @segment sn35.113:2.1 -->

Eye consciousness arises dependent on the eye and sights. The meeting of the three is contact.

<!-- @segment sn35.113:2.2 -->

Contact is a requirement for feeling.

<!-- @segment sn35.113:2.3 -->

Feeling is a requirement for craving.

<!-- @segment sn35.113:2.4 -->

When that craving fades away and ceases no residue left behind, grasping ceases.

<!-- @segment sn35.113:2.5 -->

When grasping ceases, continued existence ceases.

<!-- @segment sn35.113:2.6 -->

When continued existence ceases, rebirth ceases.

<!-- @segment sn35.113:2.7 -->

When rebirth ceases, old age and death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, sadness, and distress cease.

<!-- @segment sn35.113:2.8 -->

That is how this entire mass of suffering ceases.

<!-- @segment sn35.113:2.9 -->

Ear … nose … tongue … body …

<!-- @segment sn35.113:2.10 -->

Mind consciousness arises dependent on the mind and ideas. The meeting of the three is contact.

<!-- @segment sn35.113:2.11 -->

Contact is a requirement for feeling.

<!-- @segment sn35.113:2.12 -->

Feeling is a requirement for craving.

<!-- @segment sn35.113:2.13 -->

When that craving fades away and ceases no residue left behind, grasping ceases.

<!-- @segment sn35.113:2.14 -->

When grasping ceases …

<!-- @segment sn35.113:2.15 -->

That is how this entire mass of suffering ceases.”

<!-- @segment sn35.113:3.1 -->

Now at that time a certain monk was standing listening in on the Buddha.

<!-- @segment sn35.113:3.2 -->

The Buddha saw him

<!-- @segment sn35.113:3.3 -->

and said,

<!-- @segment sn35.113:3.4 -->

“Monk, did you hear that exposition of the teaching?”

<!-- @segment sn35.113:3.5 -->

“Yes, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn35.113:3.6 -->

“Learn that exposition of the teaching,

<!-- @segment sn35.113:3.7 -->

memorize it,

<!-- @segment sn35.113:3.8 -->

and remember it.

<!-- @segment sn35.113:3.9 -->

That exposition of the teaching is beneficial and relevant to the fundamentals of the spiritual life.”
