<!-- @segment sn12.23:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:1.2 -->

“Mendicants, I say that the ending of defilements is for one who knows and sees, not for one who does not know or see.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:1.3 -->

For one who knows and sees what?

<!-- @segment sn12.23:1.4 -->

‘Such is form, such is the origin of form, such is the ending of form.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:1.5 -->

Such is feeling …

<!-- @segment sn12.23:1.6 -->

Such is perception …

<!-- @segment sn12.23:1.7 -->

Such are choices …

<!-- @segment sn12.23:1.8 -->

Such is consciousness, such is the origin of consciousness, such is the ending of consciousness.’

<!-- @segment sn12.23:1.9 -->

The ending of the defilements is for one who knows and sees this.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:2.1 -->

I say that this knowledge of ending has a vital condition, it doesn’t lack a vital condition.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:2.2 -->

And what is it?

<!-- @segment sn12.23:2.3 -->

You should say: ‘Freedom.’

<!-- @segment sn12.23:2.4 -->

I say that freedom has a vital condition, it doesn’t lack a vital condition.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:2.5 -->

And what is it?

<!-- @segment sn12.23:2.6 -->

You should say: ‘Dispassion.’

<!-- @segment sn12.23:2.7 -->

I say that dispassion has a vital condition.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:2.8 -->

And what is it?

<!-- @segment sn12.23:2.9 -->

You should say: ‘Disillusionment.’

<!-- @segment sn12.23:2.10 -->

I say that disillusionment has a vital condition.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:2.11 -->

And what is it?

<!-- @segment sn12.23:2.12 -->

You should say: ‘Truly knowing and seeing.’

<!-- @segment sn12.23:2.13 -->

I say that truly knowing and seeing has a vital condition.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:2.14 -->

And what is it?

<!-- @segment sn12.23:2.15 -->

You should say: ‘Immersion.’

<!-- @segment sn12.23:2.16 -->

I say that immersion has a vital condition.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:3.1 -->

And what is it?

<!-- @segment sn12.23:3.2 -->

You should say: ‘Bliss.’

<!-- @segment sn12.23:3.3 -->

I say that bliss has a vital condition.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:3.4 -->

And what is it?

<!-- @segment sn12.23:3.5 -->

You should say: ‘Tranquility.’

<!-- @segment sn12.23:3.6 -->

I say that tranquility has a vital condition.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:3.7 -->

And what is it?

<!-- @segment sn12.23:3.8 -->

You should say: ‘Rapture.’

<!-- @segment sn12.23:3.9 -->

I say that rapture has a vital condition.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:3.10 -->

And what is it?

<!-- @segment sn12.23:3.11 -->

You should say: ‘Joy.’

<!-- @segment sn12.23:3.12 -->

I say that joy has a vital condition.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:3.13 -->

And what is it?

<!-- @segment sn12.23:3.14 -->

You should say: ‘Faith.’

<!-- @segment sn12.23:3.15 -->

I say that faith has a vital condition.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:4.1 -->

And what is it?

<!-- @segment sn12.23:4.2 -->

You should say: ‘Suffering.’

<!-- @segment sn12.23:4.3 -->

I say that suffering has a vital condition.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:4.4 -->

And what is it?

<!-- @segment sn12.23:4.5 -->

You should say: ‘Rebirth.’

<!-- @segment sn12.23:4.6 -->

I say that rebirth has a vital condition.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:4.7 -->

And what is it?

<!-- @segment sn12.23:4.8 -->

You should say: ‘Continued existence.’

<!-- @segment sn12.23:4.9 -->

I say that continued existence has a vital condition.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:4.10 -->

And what is it?

<!-- @segment sn12.23:4.11 -->

You should say: ‘Grasping.’

<!-- @segment sn12.23:4.12 -->

I say that grasping has a vital condition.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:4.13 -->

And what is it?

<!-- @segment sn12.23:4.14 -->

You should say: ‘Craving.’

<!-- @segment sn12.23:4.15 -->

I say that craving has a vital condition.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:5.1 -->

And what is it?

<!-- @segment sn12.23:5.2 -->

You should say: ‘Feeling.’ …

<!-- @segment sn12.23:5.3 -->

You should say: ‘Contact.’ …

<!-- @segment sn12.23:5.4 -->

You should say: ‘The six sense fields.’ …

<!-- @segment sn12.23:5.5 -->

You should say: ‘Name and form.’ …

<!-- @segment sn12.23:5.6 -->

You should say: ‘Consciousness.’ …

<!-- @segment sn12.23:5.7 -->

You should say: ‘Choices.’ …

<!-- @segment sn12.23:5.8 -->

I say that choices have a vital condition, they don’t lack a vital condition.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:5.9 -->

And what is the vital condition for choices?

<!-- @segment sn12.23:5.10 -->

You should say: ‘Ignorance.’

<!-- @segment sn12.23:6.1 -->

So, mendicants: ignorance is the vital condition for choices.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:6.2 -->

Choices are the vital condition for consciousness.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:6.3 -->

Consciousness is the vital condition for name and form.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:6.4 -->

Name and form are the vital conditions for the six sense fields.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:6.5 -->

The six sense fields are the vital conditions for contact.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:6.6 -->

Contact is the vital condition for feeling.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:6.7 -->

Feeling is the vital condition for craving.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:6.8 -->

Craving is the vital condition for grasping.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:6.9 -->

Grasping is the vital condition for continued existence.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:6.10 -->

Continued existence is the vital condition for rebirth.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:6.11 -->

Rebirth is the vital condition for suffering.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:6.12 -->

Suffering is the vital condition for faith.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:6.13 -->

Faith is the vital condition for joy.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:6.14 -->

Joy is the vital condition for rapture.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:6.15 -->

Rapture is the vital condition for tranquility.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:6.16 -->

Tranquility is the vital condition for bliss.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:6.17 -->

Bliss is the vital condition for immersion.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:6.18 -->

Immersion is the vital condition for truly knowing and seeing.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:6.19 -->

Truly knowing and seeing is the vital condition for disillusionment.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:6.20 -->

Disillusionment is the vital condition for dispassion.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:6.21 -->

Dispassion is the vital condition for freedom.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:6.22 -->

Freedom is the vital condition for the knowledge of ending.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:7.1 -->

It’s like when the heavens rain heavily on a mountain top, and the water flows downhill to fill the hollows, crevices, and creeks. As they become full, they fill up the pools. The pools fill up the lakes, the lakes fill up the streams, and the streams fill up the rivers. And as the rivers become full, they fill up the ocean.

<!-- @segment sn12.23:8.1 -->

In the same way, ignorance is the vital condition for choices. … Freedom is the vital condition for the knowledge of ending.”
