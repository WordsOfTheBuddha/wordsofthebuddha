title: Elements
description: The Buddha teaches Rāhula to contemplate the earth, water, fire, air, space, and consciousness elements as impermanent, suffering, and not-self—not fit to be regarded as “mine, I am this, my self”—until disenchantment, fading of desire, and liberation arise.
qualities: learning, wisdom
theme: wisdom, training guideline
---

<!-- @segment sn18.9:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn18.9:1.2 -->

“What do you think, Rāhula?

<!-- @segment sn18.9:1.3 -->

Is the earth element permanent or impermanent?”

<!-- @segment sn18.9:1.4 -->

“Impermanent, sir.” …

<!-- @segment sn18.9:1.5 -->

“… the water element …

<!-- @segment sn18.9:1.6 -->

the fire element …

<!-- @segment sn18.9:1.7 -->

the air element …

<!-- @segment sn18.9:1.8 -->

the space element …

<!-- @segment sn18.9:1.9 -->

Is the consciousness element permanent or impermanent?”

<!-- @segment sn18.9:1.10 -->

“Impermanent, sir.” …

<!-- @segment sn18.9:1.11 -->

“Seeing this, a learned noble disciple grows disillusioned with the earth element, water element, fire element, air element, space element, and consciousness element …”
