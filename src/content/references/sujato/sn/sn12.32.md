<!-- @segment sn12.32:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn12.32:1.2 -->

Then the mendicant Kaḷāra the Aristocrat went up to Venerable Sāriputta and exchanged greetings with him.

<!-- @segment sn12.32:1.3 -->

When the greetings and polite conversation were over, he sat down to one side and said to him,

<!-- @segment sn12.32:1.4 -->

“Reverend Sāriputta, the mendicant Phagguna of the Top-Knot has resigned the training and returned to a lesser life.”

<!-- @segment sn12.32:1.5 -->

“That venerable mustn’t have gained any solace in this teaching and training.”

<!-- @segment sn12.32:2.1 -->

“Well then, has Venerable Sāriputta found solace in this teaching and training?”

<!-- @segment sn12.32:3.1 -->

“Reverend, I have no uncertainty.”

<!-- @segment sn12.32:3.2 -->

“But what of the future?”

<!-- @segment sn12.32:4.1 -->

“I have no doubt.”

<!-- @segment sn12.32:5.1 -->

Then Kaḷāra the Aristocrat went up to the Buddha, bowed, sat down to one side, and said to him,

<!-- @segment sn12.32:5.2 -->

“Sir, Venerable Sāriputta has declared enlightenment:

<!-- @segment sn12.32:5.3 -->

‘I understand: “Rebirth is ended, the spiritual journey has been completed, what had to be done has been done, there is nothing further for this place.”’”

<!-- @segment sn12.32:6.1 -->

So the Buddha addressed one of the monks,

<!-- @segment sn12.32:6.2 -->

“Please, monk, in my name tell Sāriputta that

<!-- @segment sn12.32:6.3 -->

the teacher summons him.”

<!-- @segment sn12.32:6.4 -->

“Yes, sir,” that monk replied. He went to Sāriputta and said to him,

<!-- @segment sn12.32:6.5 -->

“Reverend Sāriputta, the teacher summons you.”

<!-- @segment sn12.32:6.6 -->

“Yes, reverend,” replied Sāriputta. He went to the Buddha, bowed, and sat down to one side. The Buddha said to him,

<!-- @segment sn12.32:6.7 -->

“Sāriputta, is it really true that you have declared enlightenment:

<!-- @segment sn12.32:6.8 -->

‘I understand: “Rebirth is ended, the spiritual journey has been completed, what had to be done has been done, there is nothing further for this place”’?”

<!-- @segment sn12.32:6.9 -->

“Sir, I did not state the matter in these words and phrases.”

<!-- @segment sn12.32:6.10 -->

“Sāriputta, no matter how a gentleman declares enlightenment, what he has declared should be regarded as such.”

<!-- @segment sn12.32:6.11 -->

“Sir, did I not also say that

<!-- @segment sn12.32:6.12 -->

I did not state the matter in these words and phrases?”

<!-- @segment sn12.32:7.1 -->

“Sāriputta, suppose they were to ask you:

<!-- @segment sn12.32:7.2 -->

‘But Reverend Sāriputta, how have you known and seen so that you’ve declared enlightenment:

<!-- @segment sn12.32:7.3 -->

“I understand: ‘Rebirth is ended, the spiritual journey has been completed, what had to be done has been done, there is nothing further for this place.’”’

<!-- @segment sn12.32:7.4 -->

How would you answer?”

<!-- @segment sn12.32:8.1 -->

“Sir, if they were to ask me this,

<!-- @segment sn12.32:8.2 -->



<!-- @segment sn12.32:8.3 -->



<!-- @segment sn12.32:8.4 -->

I would answer:

<!-- @segment sn12.32:8.5 -->

‘Reverends, when the source of rebirth ended, I knew “it is ended”.

<!-- @segment sn12.32:8.6 -->

Knowing it is ended,

<!-- @segment sn12.32:8.7 -->

I understand: “Rebirth is ended, the spiritual journey has been completed, what had to be done has been done, there is nothing further for this place.”’

<!-- @segment sn12.32:8.8 -->

That’s how I’d answer such a question.”

<!-- @segment sn12.32:9.1 -->

“But Sāriputta, suppose they were to ask you:

<!-- @segment sn12.32:9.2 -->

‘But what is the source, origin, birthplace, and inception of rebirth?’

<!-- @segment sn12.32:9.3 -->

How would you answer?”

<!-- @segment sn12.32:9.4 -->

“Sir, if they were to ask me this,

<!-- @segment sn12.32:9.5 -->



<!-- @segment sn12.32:9.6 -->

I would answer:

<!-- @segment sn12.32:9.7 -->

‘Continued existence is the source, origin, birthplace, and inception of rebirth.’

<!-- @segment sn12.32:9.8 -->

That’s how I’d answer such a question.”

<!-- @segment sn12.32:10.1 -->

“But Sāriputta, suppose they were to ask you:

<!-- @segment sn12.32:10.2 -->

‘What is the source of continued existence?’

<!-- @segment sn12.32:10.3 -->

How would you answer?”

<!-- @segment sn12.32:10.4 -->

“Sir, if they were to ask me this,

<!-- @segment sn12.32:10.5 -->



<!-- @segment sn12.32:10.6 -->

I’d answer:

<!-- @segment sn12.32:10.7 -->

‘Grasping is the source of continued existence.’

<!-- @segment sn12.32:10.8 -->

That’s how I’d answer such a question.”

<!-- @segment sn12.32:11.1 -->

“But Sāriputta, suppose they were to ask you:

<!-- @segment sn12.32:11.2 -->

‘What is the source of grasping?’ …

<!-- @segment sn12.32:11.3 -->

But Sāriputta, suppose they were to ask you:

<!-- @segment sn12.32:11.4 -->

‘What is the source of craving?’

<!-- @segment sn12.32:11.5 -->

How would you answer?”

<!-- @segment sn12.32:11.6 -->

“Sir, if they were to ask me this,

<!-- @segment sn12.32:11.7 -->



<!-- @segment sn12.32:11.8 -->

I’d answer:

<!-- @segment sn12.32:11.9 -->

‘Feeling is the source of craving.’

<!-- @segment sn12.32:11.10 -->

That’s how I’d answer such a question.”

<!-- @segment sn12.32:12.1 -->

“But Sāriputta, suppose they were to ask you:

<!-- @segment sn12.32:12.2 -->

‘But how have you known and seen so that the relishing of feelings is no longer present?’

<!-- @segment sn12.32:12.3 -->

How would you answer?”

<!-- @segment sn12.32:12.4 -->

“Sir, if they were to ask me this,

<!-- @segment sn12.32:12.5 -->

I’d answer:

<!-- @segment sn12.32:12.6 -->

‘Reverends, there are three feelings.

<!-- @segment sn12.32:12.7 -->

What three?

<!-- @segment sn12.32:12.8 -->

Pleasant, painful, and neutral feeling.

<!-- @segment sn12.32:12.9 -->

These three feelings are impermanent,

<!-- @segment sn12.32:12.10 -->

and what’s impermanent is suffering.

<!-- @segment sn12.32:12.11 -->

When I understood this, the relishing of feelings was no longer present.’

<!-- @segment sn12.32:12.12 -->

That’s how I’d answer such a question.”

<!-- @segment sn12.32:13.1 -->

“Good, good, Sāriputta!

<!-- @segment sn12.32:13.2 -->

The same point may also be briefly explained in this way:

<!-- @segment sn12.32:13.3 -->

‘Suffering includes whatever is felt.’

<!-- @segment sn12.32:14.1 -->

But Sāriputta, suppose they were to ask you:

<!-- @segment sn12.32:14.2 -->

‘But Reverend, how have you been liberated that you declare enlightenment:

<!-- @segment sn12.32:14.3 -->

“I understand: ‘Rebirth is ended, the spiritual journey has been completed, what had to be done has been done, there is nothing further for this place.’”?’

<!-- @segment sn12.32:14.4 -->

How would you answer?”

<!-- @segment sn12.32:14.5 -->

“Sir, if they were to ask me this,

<!-- @segment sn12.32:14.6 -->



<!-- @segment sn12.32:14.7 -->



<!-- @segment sn12.32:14.8 -->

I’d answer:

<!-- @segment sn12.32:14.9 -->

‘Because of an inner release with the ending of all grasping, I live mindfully so that defilements don’t defile me and I don’t look down on myself.’

<!-- @segment sn12.32:14.10 -->

That’s how I’d answer such a question.”

<!-- @segment sn12.32:15.1 -->

“Good, good, Sāriputta!

<!-- @segment sn12.32:15.2 -->

The same point may also be briefly explained in this way:

<!-- @segment sn12.32:15.3 -->

‘I have no uncertainty regarding the defilements spoken of by the ascetic. I have no doubt that I’ve given them up.’”

<!-- @segment sn12.32:15.4 -->

That is what the Buddha said.

<!-- @segment sn12.32:15.5 -->

When he had spoken, the Holy One rose from his seat and entered his dwelling.

<!-- @segment sn12.32:16.1 -->

Then soon after the Buddha left, Venerable Sāriputta said to the mendicants,

<!-- @segment sn12.32:16.2 -->

“Reverends, the Buddha asked me the first question without letting me know beforehand, so I hesitated.

<!-- @segment sn12.32:16.3 -->

But when the Buddha agreed with my answer, I thought:

<!-- @segment sn12.32:16.4 -->

‘If the Buddha were to question me all day on this matter in different words and ways, I could answer all day with different words and ways.

<!-- @segment sn12.32:16.5 -->

If he were to question me all night,

<!-- @segment sn12.32:16.6 -->

all day and night,

<!-- @segment sn12.32:16.7 -->

for two days and nights,

<!-- @segment sn12.32:16.8 -->



<!-- @segment sn12.32:16.9 -->

for three,

<!-- @segment sn12.32:16.10 -->



<!-- @segment sn12.32:16.11 -->

four,

<!-- @segment sn12.32:16.12 -->



<!-- @segment sn12.32:16.13 -->

five,

<!-- @segment sn12.32:16.14 -->



<!-- @segment sn12.32:16.15 -->

six,

<!-- @segment sn12.32:16.16 -->



<!-- @segment sn12.32:16.17 -->

or seven days and nights, I could answer in different words and ways for seven days and nights.’”

<!-- @segment sn12.32:17.1 -->

Then Kaḷāra the Aristocrat went up to the Buddha, bowed, sat down to one side, and said to him,

<!-- @segment sn12.32:17.2 -->

“Sir, Venerable Sāriputta has roared his lion’s roar!”

<!-- @segment sn12.32:17.3 -->

(And he told the Buddha all that Sāriputta had said.)

<!-- @segment sn12.32:17.4 -->



<!-- @segment sn12.32:17.5 -->



<!-- @segment sn12.32:17.6 -->



<!-- @segment sn12.32:17.7 -->



<!-- @segment sn12.32:17.8 -->



<!-- @segment sn12.32:17.9 -->



<!-- @segment sn12.32:17.10 -->



<!-- @segment sn12.32:17.11 -->



<!-- @segment sn12.32:17.12 -->



<!-- @segment sn12.32:17.13 -->



<!-- @segment sn12.32:18.1 -->

“Mendicant, Sāriputta has clearly penetrated the principle of the teachings, so that he could answer any questions I might ask him in different words and ways up to the seventh day and night.”
