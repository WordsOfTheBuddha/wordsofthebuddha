title: Going to the Far Shore

<!-- @segment sn45.34:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn45.34:1.2 -->

“Mendicants, when these eight things are developed and cultivated they lead to going from the near shore to the far shore.

<!-- @segment sn45.34:1.3 -->

What eight?

<!-- @segment sn45.34:1.4 -->

They are right view, right purpose, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right immersion.

<!-- @segment sn45.34:1.5 -->

When these eight things are developed and cultivated they lead to going from the near shore to the far shore.”

<!-- @segment sn45.34:2.1 -->

That is what the Buddha said.

<!-- @segment sn45.34:2.2 -->

Then the Holy One, the Teacher, went on to say:

<!-- @segment sn45.34:3.1 -->

“Few are those among humans

<!-- @segment sn45.34:3.2 -->

who cross to the far shore.

<!-- @segment sn45.34:3.3 -->

The rest just run

<!-- @segment sn45.34:3.4 -->

around on the near shore.

<!-- @segment sn45.34:4.1 -->

When the teaching is well explained,

<!-- @segment sn45.34:4.2 -->

those who practice accordingly

<!-- @segment sn45.34:4.3 -->

are the ones who will cross over

<!-- @segment sn45.34:4.4 -->

Death’s dominion so hard to pass.

<!-- @segment sn45.34:5.1 -->

Rid of dark qualities,

<!-- @segment sn45.34:5.2 -->

an astute person should develop the bright.

<!-- @segment sn45.34:5.3 -->

Leaving home behind

<!-- @segment sn45.34:5.4 -->

for the seclusion so hard to enjoy,

<!-- @segment sn45.34:6.1 -->

try to find satisfaction there,

<!-- @segment sn45.34:6.2 -->

having left behind sensual pleasures.

<!-- @segment sn45.34:6.3 -->

Owning nothing, an astute person

<!-- @segment sn45.34:6.4 -->

should cleanse themselves of mental corruptions.

<!-- @segment sn45.34:7.1 -->

And those whose minds are rightly developed

<!-- @segment sn45.34:7.2 -->

in the awakening factors;

<!-- @segment sn45.34:7.3 -->

letting go of attachments,

<!-- @segment sn45.34:7.4 -->

they delight in not grasping.

<!-- @segment sn45.34:7.5 -->

With defilements ended, brilliant,

<!-- @segment sn45.34:7.6 -->

they are quenched in this world.”
