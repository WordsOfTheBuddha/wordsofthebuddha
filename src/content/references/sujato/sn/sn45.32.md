<!-- @segment sn45.32:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn45.32:1.2 -->

“Mendicants, I will teach you one practicing wrongly and one practicing rightly.

<!-- @segment sn45.32:1.3 -->

Listen …

<!-- @segment sn45.32:1.4 -->

And who is practicing wrongly?

<!-- @segment sn45.32:1.5 -->

It’s someone who has wrong view, wrong purpose, wrong speech, wrong action, wrong livelihood, wrong effort, wrong mindfulness, and wrong immersion.

<!-- @segment sn45.32:1.6 -->

This is called one practicing wrongly.

<!-- @segment sn45.32:1.7 -->

And who is practicing rightly?

<!-- @segment sn45.32:1.8 -->

It’s someone who has right view, right purpose, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right immersion.

<!-- @segment sn45.32:1.9 -->

This is called one practicing rightly.”
