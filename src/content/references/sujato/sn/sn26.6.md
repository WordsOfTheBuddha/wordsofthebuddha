title: Perception
description: The Buddha teaches that the arising, continuation, and manifestation of perceptions of sights, sounds, smells, tastes, touches, and ideas is the arising of suffering, the continuation of disease, and the manifestation of old age and death—and that their cessation is the cessation of suffering.
qualities: ending, wisdom
theme: principle, urgency
---

<!-- @segment sn26.6:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn26.6:1.2 -->

“Mendicants, the arising of perception of sights …

<!-- @segment sn26.6:1.3 -->



<!-- @segment sn26.6:1.4 -->

perception of ideas … is the manifestation of old age and death.

<!-- @segment sn26.6:1.5 -->

The cessation of perception of sights …

<!-- @segment sn26.6:1.6 -->



<!-- @segment sn26.6:1.7 -->

perception of ideas … is the disappearance of old age and death.”
