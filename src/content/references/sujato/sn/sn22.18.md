title: Impermanence With Its Cause
---

<!-- @segment sn22.18:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn22.18:1.2 -->

“Mendicants, form is impermanent.

<!-- @segment sn22.18:1.3 -->

The cause and reason that gives rise to form is also impermanent.

<!-- @segment sn22.18:1.4 -->

Since form is produced by what is impermanent, how could it be permanent?

<!-- @segment sn22.18:1.5 -->

Feeling is impermanent …

<!-- @segment sn22.18:1.6 -->



<!-- @segment sn22.18:1.7 -->



<!-- @segment sn22.18:1.8 -->

Perception is impermanent …

<!-- @segment sn22.18:1.9 -->

Choices are impermanent …

<!-- @segment sn22.18:1.10 -->



<!-- @segment sn22.18:1.11 -->



<!-- @segment sn22.18:1.12 -->

Consciousness is impermanent.

<!-- @segment sn22.18:1.13 -->

The cause and reason that gives rise to consciousness is also impermanent.

<!-- @segment sn22.18:1.14 -->

Since consciousness is produced by what is impermanent, how could it be permanent?

<!-- @segment sn22.18:1.15 -->

Seeing this …

<!-- @segment sn22.18:1.16 -->

They understand: ‘… there is nothing further for this place.’”
