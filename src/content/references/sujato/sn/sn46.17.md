<!-- @segment sn46.17:1.1 -->

“Mendicants, when these seven awakening factors are developed and cultivated they lead to going from the near shore to the far shore.

<!-- @segment sn46.17:1.2 -->

What seven?

<!-- @segment sn46.17:1.3 -->

The awakening factors of mindfulness, investigation of principles, energy, rapture, tranquility, immersion, and equanimity.

<!-- @segment sn46.17:1.4 -->

These seven awakening factors, when developed and cultivated, lead to going from the near shore to the far shore.

<!-- @segment sn46.17:2.1 -->

Few are those among humans

<!-- @segment sn46.17:2.2 -->

who cross to the far shore.

<!-- @segment sn46.17:2.3 -->

The rest just run

<!-- @segment sn46.17:2.4 -->

around on the near shore.

<!-- @segment sn46.17:3.1 -->

When the teaching is well explained,

<!-- @segment sn46.17:3.2 -->

those who practice accordingly

<!-- @segment sn46.17:3.3 -->

are the ones who will cross over

<!-- @segment sn46.17:3.4 -->

Death’s dominion so hard to pass.

<!-- @segment sn46.17:4.1 -->

Rid of dark qualities,

<!-- @segment sn46.17:4.2 -->

an astute person should develop the bright.

<!-- @segment sn46.17:4.3 -->

Leaving home behind

<!-- @segment sn46.17:4.4 -->

for the seclusion so hard to enjoy,

<!-- @segment sn46.17:5.1 -->

try to find satisfaction there,

<!-- @segment sn46.17:5.2 -->

having left behind sensual pleasures.

<!-- @segment sn46.17:5.3 -->

Owning nothing, an astute person

<!-- @segment sn46.17:5.4 -->

should cleanse themselves of mental corruptions.

<!-- @segment sn46.17:6.1 -->

And those whose minds are rightly developed

<!-- @segment sn46.17:6.2 -->

in the awakening factors;

<!-- @segment sn46.17:6.3 -->

letting go of attachments,

<!-- @segment sn46.17:6.4 -->

they delight in not grasping.

<!-- @segment sn46.17:6.5 -->

With defilements ended, brilliant,

<!-- @segment sn46.17:6.6 -->

they are quenched in this world.”
