title: Feeling
description: The Buddha teaches that the arising, continuation, and manifestation of feelings born of eye, ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind contact is the arising of suffering, the continuation of disease, and the manifestation of old age and death—and that their cessation is the cessation of suffering.
qualities: ending, wisdom
theme: principle, urgency
---

<!-- @segment sn26.5:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn26.5:1.2 -->

“Mendicants, the arising of feeling born of eye contact …

<!-- @segment sn26.5:2.1 -->

the arising of feeling born of mind contact …

<!-- @segment sn26.5:2.2 -->

is the manifestation of old age and death.

<!-- @segment sn26.5:2.3 -->

The cessation of feeling born of eye contact …

<!-- @segment sn26.5:2.4 -->



<!-- @segment sn26.5:2.5 -->

the cessation of feeling born of mind contact … is the disappearance of old age and death.”
