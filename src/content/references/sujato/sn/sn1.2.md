<!-- @segment sn1.2:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn1.2:1.2 -->

Then, late at night, a glorious deity, lighting up the entire Jeta’s Grove, went up to the Buddha, bowed, stood to one side, and said to him,

<!-- @segment sn1.2:2.1 -->

“Good fellow, do you know the liberation, emancipation, and seclusion of sentient beings?”

<!-- @segment sn1.2:3.1 -->

“I do, good sir.”

<!-- @segment sn1.2:4.1 -->

“But how is it that you know the liberation, emancipation, and seclusion of sentient beings?”

<!-- @segment sn1.2:5.1 -->

“Due to the ending of relish for rebirth;

<!-- @segment sn1.2:5.2 -->

due to the finishing of perception and consciousness;

<!-- @segment sn1.2:5.3 -->

due to the cessation and stilling of feelings—

<!-- @segment sn1.2:5.4 -->

that, respectable sir, is how I know the liberation,

<!-- @segment sn1.2:5.5 -->

emancipation, and seclusion of sentient beings.”
