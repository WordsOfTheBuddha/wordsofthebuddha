<!-- @segment sn22.150:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn22.150:1.2 -->

“Mendicants, when what exists, because of grasping what, do pleasure and pain arise in oneself?”

<!-- @segment sn22.150:1.3 -->

“Our teachings are rooted in the Buddha. …”

<!-- @segment sn22.150:1.4 -->

“When form exists, because of grasping form, pleasure and pain arise in oneself.

<!-- @segment sn22.150:1.5 -->

When feeling …

<!-- @segment sn22.150:1.6 -->

perception …

<!-- @segment sn22.150:1.7 -->

choices …

<!-- @segment sn22.150:1.8 -->

consciousness exists, because of grasping consciousness, pleasure and pain arise in oneself.

<!-- @segment sn22.150:1.9 -->

What do you think, mendicants?

<!-- @segment sn22.150:1.10 -->

Is form permanent or impermanent?”

<!-- @segment sn22.150:1.11 -->

“Impermanent, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn22.150:1.12 -->

“But if it’s impermanent, is it suffering or happiness?”

<!-- @segment sn22.150:1.13 -->

“Suffering, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn22.150:1.14 -->

“But by not grasping what’s impermanent, suffering, and perishable, would pleasure and pain arise in oneself?”

<!-- @segment sn22.150:1.15 -->

“No, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn22.150:1.16 -->

“Is feeling …

<!-- @segment sn22.150:1.17 -->

perception …

<!-- @segment sn22.150:1.18 -->

choices …

<!-- @segment sn22.150:1.19 -->

consciousness permanent or impermanent?”

<!-- @segment sn22.150:1.20 -->

“Impermanent, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn22.150:1.21 -->

“But if it’s impermanent, is it suffering or happiness?”

<!-- @segment sn22.150:1.22 -->

“Suffering, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn22.150:1.23 -->

“But by not grasping what’s impermanent, suffering, and perishable, would pleasure and pain arise in oneself?”

<!-- @segment sn22.150:1.24 -->

“No, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn22.150:1.25 -->

“Seeing this …

<!-- @segment sn22.150:1.26 -->

They understand: ‘… there is nothing further for this place.’”
