<!-- @segment sn52.6:1.1 -->

At Sāketa.

<!-- @segment sn52.6:1.2 -->

Sāriputta said to Anuruddha:

<!-- @segment sn52.6:1.3 -->

“What things has Venerable Anuruddha developed and cultivated to attain great direct knowledge?”

<!-- @segment sn52.6:1.4 -->

“Reverend, I attained great direct knowledge by developing and cultivating the four kinds of mindfulness meditation.

<!-- @segment sn52.6:1.5 -->

What four?

<!-- @segment sn52.6:1.6 -->

I meditate observing an aspect of the body—keen, aware, and mindful, rid of covetousness and displeasure for the world.

<!-- @segment sn52.6:1.7 -->

I meditate observing an aspect of feelings …

<!-- @segment sn52.6:1.8 -->

mind …

<!-- @segment sn52.6:1.9 -->

principles—keen, aware, and mindful, rid of covetousness and displeasure for the world.

<!-- @segment sn52.6:1.10 -->

I attained great direct knowledge by developing and cultivating these four kinds of mindfulness meditation.

<!-- @segment sn52.6:1.11 -->

And it’s because of developing and cultivating these four kinds of mindfulness meditation that I directly know the thousandfold galaxy.”
