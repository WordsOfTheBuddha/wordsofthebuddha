<!-- @segment sn56.51:1.1 -->

Then the Buddha, picking up a little bit of dirt on his fingernail, addressed the mendicants:

<!-- @segment sn56.51:1.2 -->

“What do you think, mendicants?

<!-- @segment sn56.51:1.3 -->

Which is more: the little bit of dirt on my fingernail, or this great earth?”

<!-- @segment sn56.51:1.4 -->

“Sir, the great earth is certainly more. The little bit of dirt on your fingernail is tiny.

<!-- @segment sn56.51:1.5 -->

Compared to the great earth, it doesn’t count, there’s no comparison, it’s not worth a fraction.”

<!-- @segment sn56.51:1.6 -->

“In the same way, for an individual with comprehension, a noble disciple accomplished in view, the suffering that’s over and done with is more, what’s left is tiny.

<!-- @segment sn56.51:1.7 -->

Compared to the mass of suffering in the past that’s over and done with, it doesn’t count, there’s no comparison, it’s not worth a fraction, since there are at most seven more lives.

<!-- @segment sn56.51:1.8 -->

Such an individual truly understands about suffering, its origin, its cessation, and the path.

<!-- @segment sn56.51:2.1 -->

That’s why you should practice meditation …”
