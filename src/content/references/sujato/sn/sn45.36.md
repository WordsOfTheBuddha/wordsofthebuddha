<!-- @segment sn45.36:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn45.36:1.2 -->

“Mendicants, I will teach you the ascetic life and the goal of the ascetic life.

<!-- @segment sn45.36:1.3 -->

Listen …

<!-- @segment sn45.36:1.4 -->

And what is the ascetic life?

<!-- @segment sn45.36:1.5 -->

It is simply this noble eightfold path, that is:

<!-- @segment sn45.36:1.6 -->

right view, right purpose, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right immersion.

<!-- @segment sn45.36:1.7 -->

This is called the ascetic life.

<!-- @segment sn45.36:1.8 -->

And what is the goal of the ascetic life?

<!-- @segment sn45.36:1.9 -->

The ending of greed, hate, and delusion.

<!-- @segment sn45.36:1.10 -->

This is called the goal of the ascetic life.”
