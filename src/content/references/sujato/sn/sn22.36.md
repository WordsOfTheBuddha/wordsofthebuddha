<!-- @segment sn22.36:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn22.36:1.2 -->

Then a mendicant went up to the Buddha … and asked him,

<!-- @segment sn22.36:1.3 -->

“Sir, may the Buddha please teach me Dhamma in brief. When I’ve heard it, I’ll live alone, withdrawn, diligent, keen, and resolute.”

<!-- @segment sn22.36:1.4 -->

“Mendicant, you’re measured against what you have an underlying tendency for,

<!-- @segment sn22.36:1.5 -->

and you’re reckoned by what you’re measured against.

<!-- @segment sn22.36:1.6 -->

You’re not measured against what you have no underlying tendency for,

<!-- @segment sn22.36:1.7 -->

and you’re not reckoned by what you’re not measured against.”

<!-- @segment sn22.36:1.8 -->

“Understood, Blessed One! Understood, Holy One!”

<!-- @segment sn22.36:2.1 -->

“But how do you see the detailed meaning of my brief statement?”

<!-- @segment sn22.36:2.2 -->

“If you have an underlying tendency for form, you’re measured against that,

<!-- @segment sn22.36:2.3 -->

and you’re reckoned by what you’re measured against.

<!-- @segment sn22.36:2.4 -->

If you have an underlying tendency for feeling …

<!-- @segment sn22.36:2.5 -->

perception …

<!-- @segment sn22.36:2.6 -->

choices …

<!-- @segment sn22.36:2.7 -->

consciousness, you’re measured against that,

<!-- @segment sn22.36:2.8 -->

and you’re reckoned by what you’re measured against.

<!-- @segment sn22.36:2.9 -->

If you have no underlying tendency for form, you’re not measured against that,

<!-- @segment sn22.36:2.10 -->

and you’re not reckoned by what you’re not measured against.

<!-- @segment sn22.36:2.11 -->

If you have no underlying tendency for feeling …

<!-- @segment sn22.36:2.12 -->

perception …

<!-- @segment sn22.36:2.13 -->

choices …

<!-- @segment sn22.36:2.14 -->

consciousness, you’re not measured against that,

<!-- @segment sn22.36:2.15 -->

and you’re not reckoned by what you’re not measured against.

<!-- @segment sn22.36:2.16 -->

That’s how I understand the detailed meaning of the Buddha’s brief statement.”

<!-- @segment sn22.36:3.1 -->

“Good, good, mendicant!

<!-- @segment sn22.36:3.2 -->

It’s good that you understand the detailed meaning of what I’ve said in brief like this.

<!-- @segment sn22.36:3.3 -->

If you have an underlying tendency for form, you’re measured against that,

<!-- @segment sn22.36:3.4 -->

and you’re reckoned by what you’re measured against.

<!-- @segment sn22.36:3.5 -->

If you have an underlying tendency for feeling …

<!-- @segment sn22.36:3.6 -->

perception …

<!-- @segment sn22.36:3.7 -->

choices …

<!-- @segment sn22.36:3.8 -->

consciousness, you’re measured against that,

<!-- @segment sn22.36:3.9 -->

and you’re reckoned by what you’re measured against.

<!-- @segment sn22.36:3.10 -->

If you have no underlying tendency for form, you’re not measured against that,

<!-- @segment sn22.36:3.11 -->

and you’re not reckoned by what you’re not measured against.

<!-- @segment sn22.36:3.12 -->

If you have no underlying tendency for feeling …

<!-- @segment sn22.36:3.13 -->

perception …

<!-- @segment sn22.36:3.14 -->

choices …

<!-- @segment sn22.36:3.15 -->

consciousness, you’re not measured against that,

<!-- @segment sn22.36:3.16 -->

and you’re not reckoned by what you’re not measured against.

<!-- @segment sn22.36:3.17 -->

This is how to understand the detailed meaning of what I said in brief.” …

<!-- @segment sn22.36:3.18 -->

And that mendicant became one of the perfected.
