<!-- @segment sn22.76:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn22.76:1.2 -->

“Mendicants, form is impermanent.

<!-- @segment sn22.76:1.3 -->

What’s impermanent is suffering.

<!-- @segment sn22.76:1.4 -->

What’s suffering is not-self.

<!-- @segment sn22.76:1.5 -->

And what’s not-self should be truly seen with right understanding like this: ‘This is not mine, I am not this, this is not my self.’

<!-- @segment sn22.76:1.6 -->

Feeling …

<!-- @segment sn22.76:1.7 -->

Perception …

<!-- @segment sn22.76:1.8 -->

Choices …

<!-- @segment sn22.76:1.9 -->

Consciousness is impermanent.

<!-- @segment sn22.76:1.10 -->

What’s impermanent is suffering.

<!-- @segment sn22.76:1.11 -->

What’s suffering is not-self.

<!-- @segment sn22.76:1.12 -->

And what’s not-self should be truly seen with right understanding like this: ‘This is not mine, I am not this, this is not my self.’

<!-- @segment sn22.76:2.1 -->

Seeing this, a learned noble disciple grows disillusioned with form, feeling, perception, choices, and consciousness.

<!-- @segment sn22.76:2.2 -->

Being disillusioned, desire fades away. When desire fades away they’re freed. When they’re freed, they know they’re freed.

<!-- @segment sn22.76:2.3 -->

They understand: ‘Rebirth is ended, the spiritual journey has been completed, what had to be done has been done, there is nothing further for this place.’

<!-- @segment sn22.76:2.4 -->

As far as there are abodes of sentient beings, even up until the pinnacle of existence, the perfected ones are the foremost and the best.”

<!-- @segment sn22.76:3.1 -->

That is what the Buddha said.

<!-- @segment sn22.76:3.2 -->

Then the Holy One, the Teacher, went on to say:

<!-- @segment sn22.76:4.1 -->

“Oh! How happy are the perfected ones!

<!-- @segment sn22.76:4.2 -->

Craving is not found in them,

<!-- @segment sn22.76:4.3 -->

the conceit ‘I am’ is cut off,

<!-- @segment sn22.76:4.4 -->

and the net of delusion is shattered.

<!-- @segment sn22.76:5.1 -->

They’ve attained imperturbability,

<!-- @segment sn22.76:5.2 -->

their minds are unclouded,

<!-- @segment sn22.76:5.3 -->

nothing in the world clings to them,

<!-- @segment sn22.76:5.4 -->

manifesting divinity, undefiled.

<!-- @segment sn22.76:6.1 -->

Completely understanding the five aggregates,

<!-- @segment sn22.76:6.2 -->

their domain is the seven good qualities.

<!-- @segment sn22.76:6.3 -->

Those true persons are praiseworthy,

<!-- @segment sn22.76:6.4 -->

the Buddha’s true-born sons.

<!-- @segment sn22.76:7.1 -->

Endowed with the seven gems,

<!-- @segment sn22.76:7.2 -->

and trained in the three trainings,

<!-- @segment sn22.76:7.3 -->

the great heroes live on,

<!-- @segment sn22.76:7.4 -->

with fear and dread given up.

<!-- @segment sn22.76:8.1 -->

Endowed with ten factors,

<!-- @segment sn22.76:8.2 -->

those giants are immersed in samādhi.

<!-- @segment sn22.76:8.3 -->

They are the best in the world,

<!-- @segment sn22.76:8.4 -->

craving is not found in them.

<!-- @segment sn22.76:9.1 -->

The adept’s knowledge has arisen:

<!-- @segment sn22.76:9.2 -->

‘This bag of bones is my last.’

<!-- @segment sn22.76:9.3 -->

They are independent of others

<!-- @segment sn22.76:9.4 -->

in the core of the spiritual path.

<!-- @segment sn22.76:10.1 -->

They do not waver due to the discriminations,

<!-- @segment sn22.76:10.2 -->

they’re freed from future lives.

<!-- @segment sn22.76:10.3 -->

They’ve reached the level of the tamed,

<!-- @segment sn22.76:10.4 -->

in the world, they’re the winners.

<!-- @segment sn22.76:11.1 -->

Above, below, all round,

<!-- @segment sn22.76:11.2 -->

relishing is not found in them.

<!-- @segment sn22.76:11.3 -->

They roar their lion’s roar:

<!-- @segment sn22.76:11.4 -->

‘The awakened are supreme in the world!’”
