title: A Mendicant With Defilements Ended

<!-- @segment sn17.30:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn17.30:1.2 -->

“Mendicants, possessions, honor, and popularity are an obstacle even for a mendicant who is perfected, with defilements ended.”

<!-- @segment sn17.30:1.3 -->

When he said this, Venerable Ānanda said to the Buddha,

<!-- @segment sn17.30:1.4 -->

“Sir, what do possessions, honor, and popularity obstruct for a mendicant with defilements ended?”

<!-- @segment sn17.30:1.5 -->

“Ānanda, I don’t say that possessions, honor, and popularity obstruct the unshakable freedom of heart.

<!-- @segment sn17.30:1.6 -->

But I do say that possessions, honor, and popularity obstruct the achievement of blissful meditations in this life for a meditator who is diligent, keen, and resolute.

<!-- @segment sn17.30:1.7 -->

So grim are possessions, honor, and popularity—bitter and harsh, an obstacle to reaching the supreme sanctuary from the yoke.

<!-- @segment sn17.30:1.8 -->

So you should train like this:

<!-- @segment sn17.30:1.9 -->

‘We will give up arisen possessions, honor, and popularity, and we won’t let them occupy our minds.’

<!-- @segment sn17.30:1.10 -->

That’s how you should train.”
