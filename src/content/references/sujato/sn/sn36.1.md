<!-- @segment sn36.1:1.1 -->

“Mendicants, there are these three feelings.

<!-- @segment sn36.1:1.2 -->

What three?

<!-- @segment sn36.1:1.3 -->

Pleasant, painful, and neutral feeling.

<!-- @segment sn36.1:1.4 -->

These are the three feelings.

<!-- @segment sn36.1:2.1 -->

Stilled, aware,

<!-- @segment sn36.1:2.2 -->

a mindful disciple of the Buddha

<!-- @segment sn36.1:2.3 -->

understands feelings,

<!-- @segment sn36.1:2.4 -->

the cause of feelings,

<!-- @segment sn36.1:3.1 -->

where they cease,

<!-- @segment sn36.1:3.2 -->

and the path that leads to their ending.

<!-- @segment sn36.1:3.3 -->

With the ending of feelings, a mendicant

<!-- @segment sn36.1:3.4 -->

is hungerless, quenched.”
