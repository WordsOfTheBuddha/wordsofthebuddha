<!-- @segment sn22.49:1.1 -->

So I have heard.

<!-- @segment sn22.49:1.2 -->

At one time the Buddha was staying near Rājagaha, in the Bamboo Grove, the squirrels’ feeding ground.

<!-- @segment sn22.49:1.3 -->

Then the householder Soṇa went up to the Buddha …

<!-- @segment sn22.49:1.4 -->

The Buddha said to him:

<!-- @segment sn22.49:2.1 -->

“Soṇa, there are ascetics and brahmins who—based on form, which is impermanent, suffering, and perishable—regard themselves thus: ‘I’m better’,

<!-- @segment sn22.49:2.2 -->

or ‘I’m equal’,

<!-- @segment sn22.49:2.3 -->

or ‘I’m worse’.

<!-- @segment sn22.49:2.4 -->

What is that but a failure to see truly?

<!-- @segment sn22.49:2.5 -->

Based on feeling …

<!-- @segment sn22.49:2.6 -->



<!-- @segment sn22.49:2.7 -->



<!-- @segment sn22.49:2.8 -->



<!-- @segment sn22.49:2.9 -->

perception …

<!-- @segment sn22.49:2.10 -->

choices …

<!-- @segment sn22.49:2.11 -->



<!-- @segment sn22.49:2.12 -->



<!-- @segment sn22.49:2.13 -->



<!-- @segment sn22.49:2.14 -->

consciousness, which is impermanent, suffering, and perishable, they regard themselves thus: ‘I’m better’,

<!-- @segment sn22.49:2.15 -->

or ‘I’m equal’,

<!-- @segment sn22.49:2.16 -->

or ‘I’m worse’.

<!-- @segment sn22.49:2.17 -->

What is that but a failure to see truly?

<!-- @segment sn22.49:3.1 -->

There are ascetics and brahmins who—based on form, which is impermanent, suffering, and perishable—don’t regard themselves thus: ‘I’m better’,

<!-- @segment sn22.49:3.2 -->

or ‘I’m equal’,

<!-- @segment sn22.49:3.3 -->

or ‘I’m worse’.

<!-- @segment sn22.49:3.4 -->

What is that but seeing truly?

<!-- @segment sn22.49:3.5 -->

Based on feeling …

<!-- @segment sn22.49:3.6 -->

perception …

<!-- @segment sn22.49:3.7 -->

choices …

<!-- @segment sn22.49:3.8 -->

consciousness, which is impermanent, suffering, and perishable, they don’t regard themselves thus: ‘I’m better’,

<!-- @segment sn22.49:3.9 -->

or ‘I’m equal’,

<!-- @segment sn22.49:3.10 -->

or ‘I’m worse’.

<!-- @segment sn22.49:3.11 -->

What is that but seeing truly?

<!-- @segment sn22.49:4.1 -->

What do you think, Soṇa?

<!-- @segment sn22.49:4.2 -->

Is form permanent or impermanent?”

<!-- @segment sn22.49:4.3 -->

“Impermanent, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn22.49:4.4 -->

“But if it’s impermanent, is it suffering or happiness?”

<!-- @segment sn22.49:4.5 -->

“Suffering, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn22.49:4.6 -->

“But if it’s impermanent, suffering, and perishable, is it fit to be regarded thus:

<!-- @segment sn22.49:4.7 -->

‘This is mine, I am this, this is my self’?”

<!-- @segment sn22.49:4.8 -->

“No, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn22.49:4.9 -->

“Is feeling …

<!-- @segment sn22.49:4.10 -->



<!-- @segment sn22.49:4.11 -->

perception …

<!-- @segment sn22.49:4.12 -->

choices …

<!-- @segment sn22.49:4.13 -->

consciousness permanent or impermanent?”

<!-- @segment sn22.49:4.14 -->

“Impermanent, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn22.49:4.15 -->

“But if it’s impermanent, is it suffering or happiness?”

<!-- @segment sn22.49:4.16 -->

“Suffering, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn22.49:4.17 -->

“But if it’s impermanent, suffering, and perishable, is it fit to be regarded thus:

<!-- @segment sn22.49:4.18 -->

‘This is mine, I am this, this is my self’?”

<!-- @segment sn22.49:4.19 -->

“No, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn22.49:5.1 -->

“So, Soṇa, you should truly see any kind of form at all—past, future, or present; internal or external; solid or subtle; inferior or superior; far or near: <em>all</em> form—with right understanding: ‘This is not mine, I am not this, this is not my self.’

<!-- @segment sn22.49:6.1 -->

You should truly see any kind of feeling …

<!-- @segment sn22.49:6.2 -->

perception …

<!-- @segment sn22.49:6.3 -->

choices …

<!-- @segment sn22.49:6.4 -->

consciousness at all—past, future, or present; internal or external; solid or subtle; inferior or superior; far or near: <em>all</em> consciousness—with right understanding: ‘This is not mine, I am not this, this is not my self.’

<!-- @segment sn22.49:7.1 -->

Seeing this, a learned noble disciple grows disillusioned with form, feeling, perception, choices, and consciousness.

<!-- @segment sn22.49:7.2 -->

Being disillusioned, desire fades away. When desire fades away they’re freed. When they’re freed, they know they’re freed.

<!-- @segment sn22.49:7.3 -->

They understand: ‘Rebirth is ended, the spiritual journey has been completed, what had to be done has been done, there is nothing further for this place.’”
