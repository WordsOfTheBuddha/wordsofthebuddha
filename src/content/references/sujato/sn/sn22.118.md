<!-- @segment sn22.118:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn22.118:1.2 -->

“What do you think, mendicants?

<!-- @segment sn22.118:1.3 -->

Do you regard form like this: ‘This is mine, I am this, this is my self’?”

<!-- @segment sn22.118:1.4 -->

“No, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn22.118:1.5 -->

“Good, mendicants!

<!-- @segment sn22.118:1.6 -->

Form should be truly seen with right understanding like this: ‘This is not mine, I am not this, this is not my self.’

<!-- @segment sn22.118:1.7 -->

Do you regard feeling …

<!-- @segment sn22.118:1.8 -->

perception …

<!-- @segment sn22.118:1.9 -->

choices …

<!-- @segment sn22.118:1.10 -->

consciousness like this: ‘This is mine, I am this, this is my self’?”

<!-- @segment sn22.118:1.11 -->

“No, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn22.118:1.12 -->

“Good, mendicants!

<!-- @segment sn22.118:1.13 -->

Consciousness should be truly seen with right understanding like this: ‘This is not mine, I am not this, this is not my self.’

<!-- @segment sn22.118:1.14 -->

Seeing this …

<!-- @segment sn22.118:1.15 -->

They understand: ‘… there is nothing further for this place.’”
