<!-- @segment sn1.13:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn1.13:1.2 -->

Standing to one side, that deity spoke this verse in the Buddha’s presence:

<!-- @segment sn1.13:2.1 -->

“There’s no fondness like that for a child,

<!-- @segment sn1.13:2.2 -->

no wealth equal to cattle,

<!-- @segment sn1.13:2.3 -->

no light like that of the sun,

<!-- @segment sn1.13:2.4 -->

and of lakes the ocean is paramount.”

<!-- @segment sn1.13:3.1 -->

“There’s no fondness like that for oneself,

<!-- @segment sn1.13:3.2 -->

no wealth equal to grain,

<!-- @segment sn1.13:3.3 -->

no light like that of wisdom,

<!-- @segment sn1.13:3.4 -->

and of waters the rain is paramount.”
