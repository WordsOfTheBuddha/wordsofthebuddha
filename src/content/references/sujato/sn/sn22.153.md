<!-- @segment sn22.153:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn22.153:1.2 -->

“Mendicants, when what exists, because of grasping what and insisting on what, does the view arise:

<!-- @segment sn22.153:1.3 -->

‘I might not be, and it might not be mine. I will not be, and it will not be mine’?”

<!-- @segment sn22.153:1.4 -->

“Our teachings are rooted in the Buddha. …”

<!-- @segment sn22.153:1.5 -->

“When form exists, because of grasping form and insisting on form, the view arises:

<!-- @segment sn22.153:1.6 -->

‘I might not be, and it might not be mine. I will not be, and it will not be mine.’

<!-- @segment sn22.153:1.7 -->

When feeling …

<!-- @segment sn22.153:1.8 -->

perception …

<!-- @segment sn22.153:1.9 -->

choices …

<!-- @segment sn22.153:1.10 -->

consciousness exists, because of grasping consciousness and insisting on consciousness, the view arises:

<!-- @segment sn22.153:1.11 -->

‘I might not be, and it might not be mine. I will not be, and it will not be mine.’

<!-- @segment sn22.153:1.12 -->

What do you think, mendicants?

<!-- @segment sn22.153:1.13 -->

Is form permanent or impermanent?”

<!-- @segment sn22.153:1.14 -->

“Impermanent, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn22.153:1.15 -->

“But if it’s impermanent, is it suffering or happiness?”

<!-- @segment sn22.153:1.16 -->

“Suffering, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn22.153:1.17 -->

“But by not grasping what’s impermanent, suffering, and perishable, would the view arise:

<!-- @segment sn22.153:1.18 -->

‘I might not be, and it might not be mine. I will not be, and it will not be mine’?”

<!-- @segment sn22.153:1.19 -->

“No, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn22.153:1.20 -->

“Is feeling …

<!-- @segment sn22.153:1.21 -->

perception …

<!-- @segment sn22.153:1.22 -->

choices …

<!-- @segment sn22.153:1.23 -->

consciousness permanent or impermanent?”

<!-- @segment sn22.153:1.24 -->

“Impermanent, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn22.153:1.25 -->

“But if it’s impermanent, is it suffering or happiness?”

<!-- @segment sn22.153:1.26 -->

“Suffering, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn22.153:1.27 -->

“But by not grasping what’s impermanent, suffering, and perishable, would the view arise:

<!-- @segment sn22.153:1.28 -->

‘I might not be, and it might not be mine. I will not be, and it will not be mine’?”

<!-- @segment sn22.153:1.29 -->

“No, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn22.153:1.30 -->

“Seeing this …

<!-- @segment sn22.153:1.31 -->

They understand: ‘… there is nothing further for this place.’”
