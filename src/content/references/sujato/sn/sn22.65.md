<!-- @segment sn22.65:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn22.65:1.2 -->

Then a mendicant went up to the Buddha … and asked him,

<!-- @segment sn22.65:1.3 -->

“Sir, may the Buddha please teach me Dhamma in brief. When I’ve heard it, I’ll live alone, withdrawn, diligent, keen, and resolute.”

<!-- @segment sn22.65:1.4 -->

“When you take pleasure, mendicant, you’re bound by Māra.

<!-- @segment sn22.65:1.5 -->

Not taking pleasure, you’re free from the Wicked One.”

<!-- @segment sn22.65:1.6 -->

“Understood, Blessed One! Understood, Holy One!”

<!-- @segment sn22.65:2.1 -->

“But how do you see the detailed meaning of my brief statement?”

<!-- @segment sn22.65:2.2 -->

“Sir, when you take pleasure in form you’re bound by Māra.

<!-- @segment sn22.65:2.3 -->

Not taking pleasure, you’re free from the Wicked One.

<!-- @segment sn22.65:2.4 -->

When you take pleasure in feeling …

<!-- @segment sn22.65:2.5 -->

perception …

<!-- @segment sn22.65:2.6 -->

choices …

<!-- @segment sn22.65:2.7 -->

consciousness you’re bound by Māra.

<!-- @segment sn22.65:2.8 -->

Not taking pleasure, you’re free from the Wicked One.

<!-- @segment sn22.65:2.9 -->

That’s how I understand the detailed meaning of the Buddha’s brief statement.”

<!-- @segment sn22.65:3.1 -->

“Good, good, mendicant!

<!-- @segment sn22.65:3.2 -->

It’s good that you understand the detailed meaning of what I’ve said in brief like this.

<!-- @segment sn22.65:3.3 -->

When you take pleasure in form you’re bound by Māra.

<!-- @segment sn22.65:3.4 -->

Not taking pleasure, you’re free from the Wicked One.

<!-- @segment sn22.65:3.5 -->

When you take pleasure in feeling …

<!-- @segment sn22.65:3.6 -->

perception …

<!-- @segment sn22.65:3.7 -->

choices …

<!-- @segment sn22.65:3.8 -->

consciousness you’re bound by Māra.

<!-- @segment sn22.65:3.9 -->

Not taking pleasure, you’re free from the Wicked One.

<!-- @segment sn22.65:3.10 -->

This is how to understand the detailed meaning of what I said in brief.” …

<!-- @segment sn22.65:3.11 -->

And that mendicant became one of the perfected.
