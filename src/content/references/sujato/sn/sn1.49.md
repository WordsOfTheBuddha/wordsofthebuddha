<!-- @segment sn1.49:1.1 -->

“Those folk in the world who are stingy,

<!-- @segment sn1.49:1.2 -->

miserly and abusive,

<!-- @segment sn1.49:1.3 -->

setting up obstacles

<!-- @segment sn1.49:1.4 -->

for others who give.

<!-- @segment sn1.49:2.1 -->

What kind of result do they reap?

<!-- @segment sn1.49:2.2 -->

What kind of future life?

<!-- @segment sn1.49:2.3 -->

We’ve come to ask you, worthy sir:

<!-- @segment sn1.49:2.4 -->

how are we to understand this?”

<!-- @segment sn1.49:3.1 -->

“Those folk in the world who are stingy,

<!-- @segment sn1.49:3.2 -->

miserly and abusive,

<!-- @segment sn1.49:3.3 -->

setting up obstacles

<!-- @segment sn1.49:3.4 -->

for others who give:

<!-- @segment sn1.49:4.1 -->

they’re reborn in hell,

<!-- @segment sn1.49:4.2 -->

the animal realm, or Yama’s world.

<!-- @segment sn1.49:4.3 -->

If they return to the human state,

<!-- @segment sn1.49:4.4 -->

they’re born in a poor family,

<!-- @segment sn1.49:5.1 -->

where clothes, food, pleasure, and play

<!-- @segment sn1.49:5.2 -->

are hard to find.

<!-- @segment sn1.49:5.3 -->

They don’t even get

<!-- @segment sn1.49:5.4 -->

what they hope for from others.

<!-- @segment sn1.49:5.5 -->

This is the result in the present life,

<!-- @segment sn1.49:5.6 -->

and in the next, a bad destination.”

<!-- @segment sn1.49:6.1 -->

“We understand what you’re saying,

<!-- @segment sn1.49:6.2 -->

and ask another question, Gotama.

<!-- @segment sn1.49:6.3 -->

What about those who’ve gained the human state,

<!-- @segment sn1.49:6.4 -->

who are bountiful and rid of stinginess,

<!-- @segment sn1.49:7.1 -->

confident in the Buddha and the teaching,

<!-- @segment sn1.49:7.2 -->

with keen respect for the Saṅgha?

<!-- @segment sn1.49:7.3 -->

What kind of result do they reap?

<!-- @segment sn1.49:7.4 -->

What kind of future life?

<!-- @segment sn1.49:7.5 -->

We’ve come to ask you, worthy sir:

<!-- @segment sn1.49:7.6 -->

how are we to understand this?”

<!-- @segment sn1.49:8.1 -->

“Those who’ve gained the human state

<!-- @segment sn1.49:8.2 -->

who are bountiful and rid of stinginess,

<!-- @segment sn1.49:8.3 -->

confident in the Buddha and the teaching,

<!-- @segment sn1.49:8.4 -->

with keen respect for the Saṅgha:

<!-- @segment sn1.49:8.5 -->

they illuminate the heavens

<!-- @segment sn1.49:8.6 -->

wherever they’re reborn.

<!-- @segment sn1.49:9.1 -->

If they return to the human state,

<!-- @segment sn1.49:9.2 -->

they’re reborn in a rich family,

<!-- @segment sn1.49:9.3 -->

where clothes, food, pleasure, and play

<!-- @segment sn1.49:9.4 -->

are easy to find.

<!-- @segment sn1.49:10.1 -->

They rejoice like those

<!-- @segment sn1.49:10.2 -->

who control the possessions of others.

<!-- @segment sn1.49:10.3 -->

This is the result in the present life,

<!-- @segment sn1.49:10.4 -->

and in the next, a good destination.”
