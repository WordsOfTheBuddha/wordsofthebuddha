<!-- @segment sn45.22:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn45.22:1.2 -->

“Mendicants, I will teach you skillful and unskillful qualities.

<!-- @segment sn45.22:1.3 -->

Listen …

<!-- @segment sn45.22:1.4 -->

And what are unskillful qualities?

<!-- @segment sn45.22:1.5 -->

They are wrong view, wrong purpose, wrong speech, wrong action, wrong livelihood, wrong effort, wrong mindfulness, and wrong immersion.

<!-- @segment sn45.22:1.6 -->

These are called unskillful qualities.

<!-- @segment sn45.22:1.7 -->

And what are skillful qualities?

<!-- @segment sn45.22:1.8 -->

They are right view, right purpose, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right immersion.

<!-- @segment sn45.22:1.9 -->

These are called skillful qualities.”
