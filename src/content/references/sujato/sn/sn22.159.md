<!-- @segment sn22.159:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn22.159:1.2 -->

Then Venerable Ānanda went up to the Buddha, bowed, sat down to one side, and said to him:

<!-- @segment sn22.159:1.3 -->

“Sir, may the Buddha please teach me Dhamma in brief. When I’ve heard it, I’ll live alone, withdrawn, diligent, keen, and resolute.”

<!-- @segment sn22.159:2.1 -->

“What do you think, Ānanda?

<!-- @segment sn22.159:2.2 -->

Is form permanent or impermanent?”

<!-- @segment sn22.159:2.3 -->

“Impermanent, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn22.159:2.4 -->

“But if it’s impermanent, is it suffering or happiness?”

<!-- @segment sn22.159:2.5 -->

“Suffering, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn22.159:2.6 -->

“But if it’s impermanent, suffering, and perishable, is it fit to be regarded thus:

<!-- @segment sn22.159:2.7 -->

‘This is mine, I am this, this is my self’?”

<!-- @segment sn22.159:2.8 -->

“No, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn22.159:2.9 -->

“Is feeling …

<!-- @segment sn22.159:2.10 -->

perception …

<!-- @segment sn22.159:2.11 -->

choices …

<!-- @segment sn22.159:2.12 -->

consciousness permanent or impermanent?”

<!-- @segment sn22.159:2.13 -->

“Impermanent, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn22.159:2.14 -->

“But if it’s impermanent, is it suffering or happiness?”

<!-- @segment sn22.159:2.15 -->

“Suffering, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn22.159:2.16 -->

“But if it’s impermanent, suffering, and perishable, is it fit to be regarded thus:

<!-- @segment sn22.159:2.17 -->

‘This is mine, I am this, this is my self’?”

<!-- @segment sn22.159:2.18 -->

“No, sir.” …

<!-- @segment sn22.159:2.19 -->

“Seeing this …

<!-- @segment sn22.159:2.20 -->

They understand: ‘… there is nothing further for this place.’”
