<!-- @segment sn12.48:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn12.48:1.2 -->

Then a brahmin cosmologist went up to the Buddha …

<!-- @segment sn12.48:1.3 -->

Seated to one side he said to the Buddha:

<!-- @segment sn12.48:2.1 -->

“Worthy Gotama, does all exist?”

<!-- @segment sn12.48:2.2 -->

“‘All exists’: this is the oldest cosmology, brahmin.”

<!-- @segment sn12.48:3.1 -->

“Then does all not exist?”

<!-- @segment sn12.48:3.2 -->

“‘All does not exist’: this is the second cosmology.”

<!-- @segment sn12.48:4.1 -->

“Well, is all oneness?”

<!-- @segment sn12.48:4.2 -->

“‘All is oneness’: this is the third cosmology.”

<!-- @segment sn12.48:5.1 -->

“Then is all expansiveness?”

<!-- @segment sn12.48:5.2 -->

“‘All is expansiveness’: this is the fourth cosmology.

<!-- @segment sn12.48:6.1 -->

Avoiding these two extremes, the Realized One teaches by the middle way:

<!-- @segment sn12.48:6.2 -->

‘Ignorance is a requirement for choices.

<!-- @segment sn12.48:6.3 -->

Choices are a requirement for consciousness. …

<!-- @segment sn12.48:6.4 -->

That is how this entire mass of suffering originates.

<!-- @segment sn12.48:6.5 -->

When ignorance fades away and ceases no residue left behind, choices cease.

<!-- @segment sn12.48:6.6 -->

When choices cease, consciousness ceases. …

<!-- @segment sn12.48:6.7 -->

That is how this entire mass of suffering ceases.’”

<!-- @segment sn12.48:7.1 -->

When he said this, the brahmin cosmologist said to the Buddha,

<!-- @segment sn12.48:7.2 -->

“Excellent, worthy Gotama! Excellent! …

<!-- @segment sn12.48:7.3 -->

From this day forth, may the worthy Gotama remember me as a lay follower who has gone for refuge for life.”
