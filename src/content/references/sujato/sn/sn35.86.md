<!-- @segment sn35.86:1.1 -->

Seated to one side, Venerable Ānanda said to the Buddha:

<!-- @segment sn35.86:1.2 -->

“Sir, may the Buddha please teach me Dhamma in brief. When I’ve heard it, I’ll live alone, withdrawn, diligent, keen, and resolute.”

<!-- @segment sn35.86:2.1 -->

“What do you think, Ānanda?

<!-- @segment sn35.86:2.2 -->

Is the eye permanent or impermanent?”

<!-- @segment sn35.86:3.1 -->

“Impermanent, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn35.86:4.1 -->

“But if it’s impermanent, is it suffering or happiness?”

<!-- @segment sn35.86:5.1 -->

“Suffering, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn35.86:6.1 -->

“But if it’s impermanent, suffering, and perishable, is it fit to be regarded thus:

<!-- @segment sn35.86:6.2 -->

‘This is mine, I am this, this is my self’?”

<!-- @segment sn35.86:7.1 -->

“No, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn35.86:8.1 -->

“Are sights …

<!-- @segment sn35.86:9.1 -->



<!-- @segment sn35.86:10.1 -->

eye consciousness … eye contact …

<!-- @segment sn35.86:10.2 -->

The pleasant, painful, or neutral feeling that arises dependent on eye contact: is that permanent or impermanent?”

<!-- @segment sn35.86:11.1 -->

“Impermanent, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn35.86:12.1 -->

“But if it’s impermanent, is it suffering or happiness?”

<!-- @segment sn35.86:13.1 -->

“Suffering, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn35.86:14.1 -->

“But if it’s impermanent, suffering, and perishable, is it fit to be regarded thus:

<!-- @segment sn35.86:14.2 -->

‘This is mine, I am this, this is my self’?”

<!-- @segment sn35.86:15.1 -->

“No, sir.” …

<!-- @segment sn35.86:16.1 -->

“Is the ear … nose … tongue … body … mind …

<!-- @segment sn35.86:17.1 -->



<!-- @segment sn35.86:18.1 -->



<!-- @segment sn35.86:18.2 -->



<!-- @segment sn35.86:18.3 -->

The pleasant, painful, or neutral feeling that arises dependent on mind contact: is that permanent or impermanent?”

<!-- @segment sn35.86:19.1 -->

“Impermanent, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn35.86:20.1 -->

“But if it’s impermanent, is it suffering or happiness?”

<!-- @segment sn35.86:21.1 -->

“Suffering, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn35.86:22.1 -->

“But if it’s impermanent, suffering, and perishable, is it fit to be regarded thus:

<!-- @segment sn35.86:22.2 -->

‘This is mine, I am this, this is my self’?”

<!-- @segment sn35.86:23.1 -->

“No, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn35.86:24.1 -->

“Seeing this, a learned noble disciple grows disillusioned with the eye, sights, eye consciousness, and eye contact. And they grow disillusioned with the painful, pleasant, or neutral feeling that arises dependent on eye contact.

<!-- @segment sn35.86:24.2 -->

They grow disillusioned with the ear … nose … tongue … body … mind … painful, pleasant, or neutral feeling that arises dependent on mind contact.

<!-- @segment sn35.86:24.3 -->

Being disillusioned, desire fades away. When desire fades away they’re freed. When they’re freed, they know they’re freed.

<!-- @segment sn35.86:24.4 -->

They understand: ‘Rebirth is ended, the spiritual journey has been completed, what had to be done has been done, there is nothing further for this place.’”
