title: Sights, Etc.
description: The Buddha teaches Rāhula to contemplate sights, sounds, smells, tastes, touches, and ideas as impermanent, suffering, and not-self—not fit to be regarded as “mine, I am this, my self”—until disenchantment, fading of desire, and liberation arise.
qualities: learning, desire
theme: wisdom, training guideline
---

<!-- @segment sn18.2:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn18.2:1.2 -->

“What do you think, Rāhula?

<!-- @segment sn18.2:1.3 -->

Are sights permanent or impermanent?”

<!-- @segment sn18.2:1.4 -->

“Impermanent, sir.” …

<!-- @segment sn18.2:1.5 -->

“… sounds …

<!-- @segment sn18.2:1.6 -->

smells …

<!-- @segment sn18.2:1.7 -->

tastes …

<!-- @segment sn18.2:1.8 -->

touches …

<!-- @segment sn18.2:1.9 -->

Are ideas permanent or impermanent?”

<!-- @segment sn18.2:1.10 -->

“Impermanent, sir.” …

<!-- @segment sn18.2:1.11 -->

“Seeing this, a learned noble disciple grows disillusioned with sights, sounds, smells, tastes, touches, and ideas.

<!-- @segment sn18.2:1.12 -->

Being disillusioned, desire fades away. …”
