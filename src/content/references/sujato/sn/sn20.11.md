title: A Jackal

<!-- @segment sn20.11:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn20.11:1.2 -->

“Mendicants, did you hear an old jackal howling at the crack of dawn?”

<!-- @segment sn20.11:1.3 -->

“Yes, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn20.11:1.4 -->

“That old jackal has the disease called mange.

<!-- @segment sn20.11:1.5 -->

Yet it still goes where it wants,

<!-- @segment sn20.11:1.6 -->

stands where it wants,

<!-- @segment sn20.11:1.7 -->

sits where it wants,

<!-- @segment sn20.11:1.8 -->

and lies down where it wants.

<!-- @segment sn20.11:1.9 -->

And the cool breeze still blows on it.

<!-- @segment sn20.11:1.10 -->

A certain person here who claims to follow the Sakyan would be lucky to experience even such an incarnation.

<!-- @segment sn20.11:1.11 -->

So you should train like this:

<!-- @segment sn20.11:1.12 -->

‘We will stay diligent.’

<!-- @segment sn20.11:1.13 -->

That’s how you should train.”
