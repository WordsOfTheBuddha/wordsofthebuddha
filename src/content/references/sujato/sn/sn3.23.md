<!-- @segment sn3.23:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn3.23:1.2 -->

Seated to one side, King Pasenadi said to the Buddha,

<!-- @segment sn3.23:1.3 -->

“Sir, how many things arise in the world for its harm, suffering, and discomfort?”

<!-- @segment sn3.23:1.4 -->

“Great king, three things arise in the world for its harm, suffering, and discomfort.

<!-- @segment sn3.23:1.5 -->

What three?

<!-- @segment sn3.23:1.6 -->

Greed,

<!-- @segment sn3.23:1.7 -->

hate,

<!-- @segment sn3.23:1.8 -->

and delusion.

<!-- @segment sn3.23:1.9 -->

These three things arise in the world for its harm, suffering, and discomfort.”

<!-- @segment sn3.23:1.10 -->

That is what the Buddha said. …

<!-- @segment sn3.23:2.1 -->

“When greed, hate, and delusion,

<!-- @segment sn3.23:2.2 -->

originate inside oneself,

<!-- @segment sn3.23:2.3 -->

they harm a person of wicked heart,

<!-- @segment sn3.23:2.4 -->

as a bamboo is destroyed by its own fruit.”
