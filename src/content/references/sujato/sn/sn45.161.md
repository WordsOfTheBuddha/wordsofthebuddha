<!-- @segment sn45.161:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn45.161:1.2 -->

“Mendicants, there are these three searches.

<!-- @segment sn45.161:1.3 -->

What three?

<!-- @segment sn45.161:1.4 -->

The search for sensual pleasures, the search for continued existence, and the search for a spiritual path.

<!-- @segment sn45.161:1.5 -->

These are the three searches.

<!-- @segment sn45.161:1.6 -->

The noble eightfold path should be developed to directly know these three searches.

<!-- @segment sn45.161:1.7 -->

What is the noble eightfold path?

<!-- @segment sn45.161:1.8 -->

It’s when a mendicant develops right view, right purpose, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right immersion, which rely on seclusion, fading away, and cessation, and ripen as letting go.

<!-- @segment sn45.161:1.9 -->

This is the noble eightfold path that should be developed to directly know these three searches.”
