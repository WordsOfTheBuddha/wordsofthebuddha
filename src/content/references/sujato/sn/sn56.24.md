<!-- @segment sn56.24:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn56.24:1.2 -->

“Mendicants, whatever perfected ones, fully awakened Buddhas truly awaken—in the past,

<!-- @segment sn56.24:1.3 -->

future,

<!-- @segment sn56.24:1.4 -->

or present—all of them truly awaken to the four noble truths.

<!-- @segment sn56.24:2.1 -->

What four?

<!-- @segment sn56.24:2.2 -->

The noble truths of suffering, the origin of suffering, the cessation of suffering, and the practice that leads to the cessation of suffering.

<!-- @segment sn56.24:2.3 -->

Whatever perfected ones, fully awakened Buddhas truly awaken—in the past,

<!-- @segment sn56.24:2.4 -->

future,

<!-- @segment sn56.24:2.5 -->

or present—all of them truly awaken to the four noble truths.

<!-- @segment sn56.24:3.1 -->

That’s why you should practice meditation …”
