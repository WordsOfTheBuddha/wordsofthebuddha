<!-- @segment sn2.28:1.1 -->

Standing to one side, the godling Nandivisāla addressed the Buddha in verse:

<!-- @segment sn2.28:2.1 -->

“Four are its wheels, and nine its doors;

<!-- @segment sn2.28:2.2 -->

it’s stuffed full, bound with greed,

<!-- @segment sn2.28:2.3 -->

and born from a bog.

<!-- @segment sn2.28:2.4 -->

Great hero, how will I keep going?”

<!-- @segment sn2.28:3.1 -->

“Having cut the strap and harness—

<!-- @segment sn2.28:3.2 -->

wicked desire and greed—

<!-- @segment sn2.28:3.3 -->

and having plucked out craving, root and all:

<!-- @segment sn2.28:3.4 -->

that’s how you will keep going.”
