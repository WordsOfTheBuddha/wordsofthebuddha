<!-- @segment sn1.12:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn1.12:1.2 -->

Standing to one side, that deity spoke this verse in the Buddha’s presence:

<!-- @segment sn1.12:2.1 -->

“Children bring you delight!

<!-- @segment sn1.12:2.2 -->

Cattle also bring you delight!

<!-- @segment sn1.12:2.3 -->

For attachments are a man’s delight;

<!-- @segment sn1.12:2.4 -->

without attachments there’s no delight.”

<!-- @segment sn1.12:3.1 -->

“Children bring you sorrow.

<!-- @segment sn1.12:3.2 -->

Cattle also bring you sorrow.

<!-- @segment sn1.12:3.3 -->

For attachments are a man’s sorrow;

<!-- @segment sn1.12:3.4 -->

without attachments there are no sorrows.”
