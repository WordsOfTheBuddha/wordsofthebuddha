<!-- @segment sn48.51:1.1 -->

So I have heard.

<!-- @segment sn48.51:1.2 -->

At one time the Buddha was staying in the land of the Kosalans near the brahmin village of Sālā.

<!-- @segment sn48.51:1.3 -->

There the Buddha addressed the mendicants:

<!-- @segment sn48.51:1.4 -->

“Mendicants, the lion, king of beasts, is said to be the best of animals in terms of strength, speed, and courage.

<!-- @segment sn48.51:1.5 -->

In the same way, the faculty of wisdom is said to be the best of the qualities on the side of awakening; that is, for becoming awakened.

<!-- @segment sn48.51:2.1 -->

And what are the qualities on the side of awakening?

<!-- @segment sn48.51:2.2 -->

The faculties of faith,

<!-- @segment sn48.51:2.3 -->

energy,

<!-- @segment sn48.51:2.4 -->

mindfulness,

<!-- @segment sn48.51:2.5 -->

immersion,

<!-- @segment sn48.51:2.6 -->

and wisdom are qualities on the side of awakening, in that they lead to becoming awakened.

<!-- @segment sn48.51:2.7 -->

The lion, king of beasts, is said to be the best of animals in terms of strength, speed, and courage.

<!-- @segment sn48.51:2.8 -->

In the same way, the faculty of wisdom is said to be the best of the qualities on the side of awakening; that is, for becoming awakened.”
