<!-- @segment sn23.23:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn23.23:1.2 -->

Seated to one side, Venerable Rādha said to the Buddha:

<!-- @segment sn23.23:1.3 -->

“Sir, may the Buddha please teach me Dhamma in brief. When I’ve heard it, I’ll live alone, withdrawn, diligent, keen, and resolute.”

<!-- @segment sn23.23:2.1 -->

“Rādha, you should give up any desire, any greed, any desire and greed for whatever is Māra.

<!-- @segment sn23.23:2.2 -->

And what is Māra?

<!-- @segment sn23.23:2.3 -->

Form is Māra. You should give up any desire, any greed, any desire and greed for it.

<!-- @segment sn23.23:2.4 -->

Feeling …

<!-- @segment sn23.23:2.5 -->

Perception …

<!-- @segment sn23.23:2.6 -->

Choices …

<!-- @segment sn23.23:2.7 -->

Consciousness is Māra. You should give up any desire, any greed, any desire and greed for it.

<!-- @segment sn23.23:2.8 -->

You should give up any desire, any greed, any desire and greed for whatever is Māra.”

“You should give up any desire, any greed, any desire and greed for whatever is of Māra nature …”

“… impermanent …”

“… of impermanent nature …”

<!-- @segment sn23.27:1.1 -->

“… suffering …”

<!-- @segment sn23.28:1.1 -->

“… of suffering nature …”

<!-- @segment sn23.29:1.1 -->

“… not-self …”

<!-- @segment sn23.30:1.1 -->

“… of not-self nature …”

<!-- @segment sn23.31:1.1 -->

“… of a nature to end …”

<!-- @segment sn23.32:1.1 -->

“… of a nature to fall …”

<!-- @segment sn23.33:1.1 -->
