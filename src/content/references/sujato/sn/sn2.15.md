<!-- @segment sn2.15:1.1 -->

Standing to one side, the godling Candana addressed the Buddha in verse:

<!-- @segment sn2.15:2.1 -->

“Who here crosses the flood,

<!-- @segment sn2.15:2.2 -->

tireless all day and night?

<!-- @segment sn2.15:2.3 -->

Who, not standing and unsupported,

<!-- @segment sn2.15:2.4 -->

does not sink in the deep?”

<!-- @segment sn2.15:3.1 -->

“Someone who is always endowed with ethics,

<!-- @segment sn2.15:3.2 -->

wise and serene,

<!-- @segment sn2.15:3.3 -->

energetic and resolute,

<!-- @segment sn2.15:3.4 -->

crosses the flood so hard to cross.

<!-- @segment sn2.15:4.1 -->

Someone who desists from sensual perception,

<!-- @segment sn2.15:4.2 -->

has moved past the fetter of form,

<!-- @segment sn2.15:4.3 -->

and has finished with relishing and greed

<!-- @segment sn2.15:4.4 -->

does not sink in the deep.”
