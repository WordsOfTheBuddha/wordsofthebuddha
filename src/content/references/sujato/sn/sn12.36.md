<!-- @segment sn12.36:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn12.36:1.2 -->

“Ignorance is a requirement for choices.

<!-- @segment sn12.36:1.3 -->

Choices are a requirement for consciousness. …

<!-- @segment sn12.36:1.4 -->

That is how this entire mass of suffering originates.

<!-- @segment sn12.36:2.1 -->

Mendicants, you might say, ‘What are old age and death, and who do they belong to?’ Or you might say, ‘Old age and death are one thing, who they belong to is another.’ But both of these mean the same thing, only the phrasing differs.

<!-- @segment sn12.36:2.2 -->

If you have the view that the soul and the body are one and the same, there is no living of the spiritual life.

<!-- @segment sn12.36:2.3 -->

If you have the view that the soul is one thing, the body another, there is no living of the spiritual life.

<!-- @segment sn12.36:2.4 -->

Avoiding these two extremes, the Realized One teaches by the middle way:

<!-- @segment sn12.36:2.5 -->

‘Rebirth is a requirement for old age and death.’

<!-- @segment sn12.36:3.1 -->

‘What is rebirth …’

<!-- @segment sn12.36:3.2 -->

‘What is continued existence …’

<!-- @segment sn12.36:3.3 -->

‘What is grasping …’

<!-- @segment sn12.36:3.4 -->

‘What is craving …’

<!-- @segment sn12.36:3.5 -->

‘What is feeling …’

<!-- @segment sn12.36:3.6 -->

‘What is contact …’

<!-- @segment sn12.36:3.7 -->

‘What are the six sense fields …’

<!-- @segment sn12.36:3.8 -->

‘What are name and form …’

<!-- @segment sn12.36:3.9 -->

‘What is consciousness …’

<!-- @segment sn12.36:3.10 -->

You might say, ‘What are choices, and who do they belong to?’ Or you might say, ‘Choices are one thing, who they belong to is another.’ But both of these mean the same thing, only the phrasing differs.

<!-- @segment sn12.36:3.11 -->

If you have the view that the soul and the body are one and the same, there is no living of the spiritual life.

<!-- @segment sn12.36:3.12 -->

If you have the view that the soul is one thing, the body another, there is no living of the spiritual life.

<!-- @segment sn12.36:3.13 -->

Avoiding these two extremes, the Realized One teaches by the middle way:

<!-- @segment sn12.36:3.14 -->

‘Ignorance is a requirement for choices.’

<!-- @segment sn12.36:4.1 -->

When ignorance fades away and ceases with no residue left behind, then any twists, ducks, and dodges are given up:

<!-- @segment sn12.36:4.2 -->

‘What are old age and death, and who do they belong to?’ or ‘old age and death are one thing, who they belong to is another’, or ‘the soul and the body are one and the same’, or ‘the soul is one thing, the body another’.

<!-- @segment sn12.36:4.3 -->

These are all cut off at the root, made like a palm stump, obliterated, and unable to arise in the future.
