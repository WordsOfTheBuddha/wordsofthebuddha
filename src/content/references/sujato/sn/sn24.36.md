<!-- @segment sn24.36:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn24.36:1.2 -->

“Mendicants, when what exists, because of grasping what and insisting on what, does the view arise:

<!-- @segment sn24.36:1.3 -->

‘A realized one neither still exists nor no longer exists after death’?”

<!-- @segment sn24.36:1.4 -->

“Our teachings are rooted in the Buddha. …”

<!-- @segment sn24.36:2.1 -->

“When form exists, because of grasping form and insisting on form, the view arises:

<!-- @segment sn24.36:2.2 -->

‘A realized one neither still exists nor no longer exists after death.’

<!-- @segment sn24.36:2.3 -->

When feeling …

<!-- @segment sn24.36:2.4 -->

perception …

<!-- @segment sn24.36:2.5 -->

choices …

<!-- @segment sn24.36:2.6 -->

consciousness exists, because of grasping consciousness and insisting on consciousness, the view arises:

<!-- @segment sn24.36:2.7 -->

‘a realized one neither still exists nor no longer exists after death.’

<!-- @segment sn24.36:3.1 -->

What do you think, mendicants?

<!-- @segment sn24.36:3.2 -->

Is form permanent or impermanent?”

<!-- @segment sn24.36:3.3 -->

“Impermanent, sir.” …

<!-- @segment sn24.36:3.4 -->



<!-- @segment sn24.36:3.5 -->



<!-- @segment sn24.36:3.6 -->



<!-- @segment sn24.36:3.7 -->

“And so, when suffering exists, because of grasping suffering and insisting on suffering, the view arises:

<!-- @segment sn24.36:3.8 -->

‘A realized one neither still exists nor no longer exists after death.’” …

<!-- @segment sn24.36:3.9 -->

“Is feeling …

<!-- @segment sn24.36:3.10 -->

perception …

<!-- @segment sn24.36:3.11 -->

choices …

<!-- @segment sn24.36:3.12 -->

consciousness permanent or impermanent?”

<!-- @segment sn24.36:3.13 -->

“Impermanent, sir.” …

<!-- @segment sn24.36:3.14 -->

“But by not grasping what’s impermanent, suffering, and perishable, would such a view arise?”

<!-- @segment sn24.36:3.15 -->



<!-- @segment sn24.36:3.16 -->

“No, sir.”

<!-- @segment sn24.36:3.17 -->

“And so, when suffering exists, because of grasping suffering and insisting on suffering, the view arises:

<!-- @segment sn24.36:3.18 -->

‘A realized one neither still exists nor no longer exists after death.’”
