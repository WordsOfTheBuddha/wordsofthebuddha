<!-- @segment sn22.29:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn22.29:1.2 -->

“Mendicants, if you take pleasure in form, you take pleasure in suffering.

<!-- @segment sn22.29:1.3 -->

If you take pleasure in suffering, you’re not free from suffering, I say.

<!-- @segment sn22.29:1.4 -->

If you take pleasure in feeling …

<!-- @segment sn22.29:1.5 -->

perception …

<!-- @segment sn22.29:1.6 -->

choices …

<!-- @segment sn22.29:1.7 -->

consciousness, you take pleasure in suffering.

<!-- @segment sn22.29:1.8 -->

If you take pleasure in suffering, you’re not free from suffering, I say.

<!-- @segment sn22.29:1.9 -->

If you don’t take pleasure in form, you don’t take pleasure in suffering.

<!-- @segment sn22.29:1.10 -->

If you don’t take pleasure in suffering, you’re free from suffering, I say.

<!-- @segment sn22.29:1.11 -->

If you don’t take pleasure in feeling …

<!-- @segment sn22.29:1.12 -->

perception …

<!-- @segment sn22.29:1.13 -->

choices …

<!-- @segment sn22.29:1.14 -->

consciousness, you don’t take pleasure in suffering.

<!-- @segment sn22.29:1.15 -->

If you don’t take pleasure in suffering, you’re free from suffering, I say.”
