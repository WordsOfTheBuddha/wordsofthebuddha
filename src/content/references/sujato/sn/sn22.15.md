title: That Which is Impermanent

<!-- @segment sn22.15:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn22.15:1.2 -->

“Mendicants, form is impermanent.

<!-- @segment sn22.15:1.3 -->

What’s impermanent is suffering.

<!-- @segment sn22.15:1.4 -->

What’s suffering is not-self.

<!-- @segment sn22.15:1.5 -->

And what’s not-self should be truly seen with right understanding like this: ‘This is not mine, I am not this, this is not my self.’

<!-- @segment sn22.15:1.6 -->

Feeling is impermanent …

<!-- @segment sn22.15:1.7 -->



<!-- @segment sn22.15:1.8 -->



<!-- @segment sn22.15:1.9 -->



<!-- @segment sn22.15:1.10 -->

Perception is impermanent …

<!-- @segment sn22.15:1.11 -->

Choices are impermanent …

<!-- @segment sn22.15:1.12 -->

Consciousness is impermanent.

<!-- @segment sn22.15:1.13 -->

What’s impermanent is suffering.

<!-- @segment sn22.15:1.14 -->

What’s suffering is not-self.

<!-- @segment sn22.15:1.15 -->

And what’s not-self should be truly seen with right understanding like this: ‘This is not mine, I am not this, this is not my self.’

<!-- @segment sn22.15:1.16 -->

Seeing this …

<!-- @segment sn22.15:1.17 -->

They understand: ‘… there is nothing further for this place.’”
