<!-- @segment sn11.3:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn11.3:1.2 -->

There the Buddha addressed the mendicants,

<!-- @segment sn11.3:1.3 -->

“Mendicants!”

<!-- @segment sn11.3:1.4 -->

“Venerable sir,” they replied.

<!-- @segment sn11.3:1.5 -->

The Buddha said this:

<!-- @segment sn11.3:2.1 -->

“Once upon a time, mendicants, a battle was fought between the gods and the titans.

<!-- @segment sn11.3:2.2 -->

Then Sakka, lord of gods, addressed the gods of the thirty-three:

<!-- @segment sn11.3:3.1 -->

‘Good fellows, when the gods are fighting, if you get scared or terrified, just look up at my banner’s crest.

<!-- @segment sn11.3:3.2 -->

Then your fear and terror will go away.

<!-- @segment sn11.3:4.1 -->

If you can’t see my banner’s crest, then look up at the banner’s crest of the Progenitor, king of gods.

<!-- @segment sn11.3:4.2 -->

Then your fear and terror will go away.

<!-- @segment sn11.3:5.1 -->

If you can’t see his banner’s crest, then look up at the banner’s crest of Varuṇa, king of gods.

<!-- @segment sn11.3:5.2 -->

Then your fear and terror will go away.

<!-- @segment sn11.3:6.1 -->

If you can’t see his banner’s crest, then look up at the banner’s crest of Īsāna, king of gods.

<!-- @segment sn11.3:6.2 -->

Then your fear and terror will go away.’

<!-- @segment sn11.3:7.1 -->

However, when they look up at those banner’s crests their fear and terror might go away or it might not.

<!-- @segment sn11.3:8.1 -->

Why is that?

<!-- @segment sn11.3:8.2 -->

Because Sakka is not free of greed, hate, and delusion. He is fearful, scared, nervous, quick to flee.

<!-- @segment sn11.3:9.1 -->

But, mendicants, I say this:

<!-- @segment sn11.3:9.2 -->

If you’ve gone to a wilderness, or to the root of a tree, or to an empty hut and you get scared or terrified, just recollect me:

<!-- @segment sn11.3:9.3 -->

‘That Blessed One is perfected, a fully awakened Buddha, accomplished in knowledge and conduct, holy, knower of the world, supreme guide for those fit for training, teacher of gods and humans, awakened, blessed.’

<!-- @segment sn11.3:9.4 -->

Then your fear and terror will go away.

<!-- @segment sn11.3:10.1 -->

If you can’t recollect me, then recollect the teaching:

<!-- @segment sn11.3:10.2 -->

‘The teaching is well explained by the Buddha—apparent in the present life, immediately effective, inviting inspection, relevant, so that sensible people can know it for themselves.’

<!-- @segment sn11.3:10.3 -->

Then your fear and terror will go away.

<!-- @segment sn11.3:11.1 -->

If you can’t recollect the teaching, then recollect the Saṅgha:

<!-- @segment sn11.3:11.2 -->

‘The Saṅgha of the Buddha’s disciples is practicing the way that’s good, direct, systematic, and proper. It consists of the four pairs, the eight individual persons. This is the Saṅgha of the Buddha’s disciples that is worthy of offerings dedicated to the gods, worthy of hospitality, worthy of a religious donation, worthy of greeting with cupped palms, and is the supreme field of merit for the world.’

<!-- @segment sn11.3:11.3 -->

Then your fear and terror will go away.

<!-- @segment sn11.3:12.1 -->

Why is that?

<!-- @segment sn11.3:12.2 -->

Because the Realized One is free of greed, hate, and delusion. He is fearless, brave, bold, and stands his ground.”

<!-- @segment sn11.3:12.3 -->

That is what the Buddha said.

<!-- @segment sn11.3:12.4 -->

Then the Holy One, the Teacher, went on to say:

<!-- @segment sn11.3:13.1 -->

“In the wilderness, at a tree’s root,

<!-- @segment sn11.3:13.2 -->

or an empty hut, O mendicants,

<!-- @segment sn11.3:13.3 -->

recollect the Buddha,

<!-- @segment sn11.3:13.4 -->

and no fear will come to you.

<!-- @segment sn11.3:14.1 -->

If you can’t recollect the Buddha—

<!-- @segment sn11.3:14.2 -->

the eldest in the world, the captain of men—

<!-- @segment sn11.3:14.3 -->

then recollect the teaching,

<!-- @segment sn11.3:14.4 -->

emancipating, well taught.

<!-- @segment sn11.3:15.1 -->

If you can’t recollect the teaching—

<!-- @segment sn11.3:15.2 -->

emancipating, well taught—

<!-- @segment sn11.3:15.3 -->

then recollect the Saṅgha,

<!-- @segment sn11.3:15.4 -->

the supreme field of merit.

<!-- @segment sn11.3:16.1 -->

Thus recollecting the Buddha,

<!-- @segment sn11.3:16.2 -->

the teaching, and the Saṅgha, mendicants,

<!-- @segment sn11.3:16.3 -->

fear and terror

<!-- @segment sn11.3:16.4 -->

and goosebumps will be no more.”
