<!-- @segment sn1.21:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn1.21:1.2 -->

Standing to one side, that deity spoke this verse in the Buddha’s presence:

<!-- @segment sn1.21:2.1 -->

“Like they’re struck by a sword,

<!-- @segment sn1.21:2.2 -->

like their head was on fire,

<!-- @segment sn1.21:2.3 -->

a mendicant, mindful, should go forth,

<!-- @segment sn1.21:2.4 -->

to give up sensual desire.”

<!-- @segment sn1.21:3.1 -->

“Like they’re struck by a sword,

<!-- @segment sn1.21:3.2 -->

like their head was on fire,

<!-- @segment sn1.21:3.3 -->

a mendicant, mindful, should go forth,

<!-- @segment sn1.21:3.4 -->

to give up substantialist view.”
