<!-- @segment sn35.98:1.1 -->

“Mendicants, I will teach you who is restrained and who is unrestrained.

<!-- @segment sn35.98:1.2 -->

Listen …

<!-- @segment sn35.98:1.3 -->

And how is someone unrestrained?

<!-- @segment sn35.98:1.4 -->

There are sights known by the eye, which are likable, desirable, agreeable, pleasant, sensual, and arousing.

<!-- @segment sn35.98:1.5 -->

If a mendicant approves, welcomes, and keeps clinging to them, they should understand:

<!-- @segment sn35.98:1.6 -->

‘My skillful qualities are declining.

<!-- @segment sn35.98:1.7 -->

For this is what the Buddha calls decline.’

<!-- @segment sn35.98:1.8 -->

There are sounds … smells … tastes … touches …

<!-- @segment sn35.98:1.9 -->

ideas known by the mind, which are likable, desirable, agreeable, pleasant, sensual, and arousing.

<!-- @segment sn35.98:1.10 -->

If a mendicant approves, welcomes, and keeps clinging to them, they should understand:

<!-- @segment sn35.98:1.11 -->

‘My skillful qualities are declining.

<!-- @segment sn35.98:1.12 -->

For this is what the Buddha calls decline.’

<!-- @segment sn35.98:1.13 -->

This is how someone is unrestrained.

<!-- @segment sn35.98:2.1 -->

And how is someone restrained?

<!-- @segment sn35.98:2.2 -->

There are sights known by the eye, which are likable, desirable, agreeable, pleasant, sensual, and arousing.

<!-- @segment sn35.98:2.3 -->

If a mendicant doesn’t approve, welcome, and keep clinging to them, they should understand:

<!-- @segment sn35.98:2.4 -->

‘My skillful qualities are not declining.

<!-- @segment sn35.98:2.5 -->

For this is what the Buddha calls non-decline.’

<!-- @segment sn35.98:2.6 -->

There are sounds … smells … tastes … touches …

<!-- @segment sn35.98:2.7 -->

ideas known by the mind, which are likable, desirable, agreeable, pleasant, sensual, and arousing.

<!-- @segment sn35.98:2.8 -->

If a mendicant doesn’t approve, welcome, and keep clinging to them, they should understand:

<!-- @segment sn35.98:2.9 -->

‘My skillful qualities are not declining.

<!-- @segment sn35.98:2.10 -->

For this is what the Buddha calls non-decline.’

<!-- @segment sn35.98:2.11 -->

This is how someone is restrained.”
