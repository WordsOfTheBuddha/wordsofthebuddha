<!-- @segment sn55.30:1.1 -->

At one time the Buddha was staying near Vesālī, at the Great Wood, in the hall with the peaked roof.

<!-- @segment sn55.30:1.2 -->

Then Nandaka the Licchavi chief minister went up to the Buddha, bowed, and sat down to one side. The Buddha said to him:

<!-- @segment sn55.30:2.1 -->

“Nandaka, a noble disciple who has four things is a stream-enterer, not liable to be reborn in the underworld, assured, destined for awakening.

<!-- @segment sn55.30:2.2 -->

What four?

<!-- @segment sn55.30:2.3 -->

It’s when a noble disciple has experiential confidence in the Buddha …

<!-- @segment sn55.30:2.4 -->



<!-- @segment sn55.30:2.5 -->

the teaching …

<!-- @segment sn55.30:2.6 -->

the Saṅgha …

<!-- @segment sn55.30:2.7 -->

And they have the ethical conduct loved by the noble ones … leading to immersion.

<!-- @segment sn55.30:2.8 -->

A noble disciple who has these four things is a stream-enterer, not liable to be reborn in the underworld, assured, destined for awakening.

<!-- @segment sn55.30:3.1 -->

A noble disciple who has these four things is endowed with long life, both human and heavenly …

<!-- @segment sn55.30:3.2 -->

beauty …

<!-- @segment sn55.30:3.3 -->

happiness …

<!-- @segment sn55.30:3.4 -->

fame …

<!-- @segment sn55.30:3.5 -->

and sovereignty, both human and heavenly.

<!-- @segment sn55.30:3.6 -->

Now, I don’t say this because I’ve heard it from some other ascetic or brahmin. I only say it because I’ve known, seen, and realized it for myself.”

<!-- @segment sn55.30:4.1 -->

When he had spoken, a certain person said to Nandaka:

<!-- @segment sn55.30:4.2 -->

“Sir, it is time to bathe.”

<!-- @segment sn55.30:4.3 -->

“Enough now, lad, with that exterior bath.

<!-- @segment sn55.30:4.4 -->

This interior bathing will do for me, that is,

<!-- @segment sn55.30:4.5 -->

confidence in the Buddha.”
