<!-- @segment sn22.72:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn22.72:1.2 -->

Then Venerable Surādha said to the Buddha:

<!-- @segment sn22.72:1.3 -->

“Sir, how does one know and see so that the heart is rid of I-making, mine-making, and conceit for this conscious body and externally for all signs; and going beyond discrimination, it’s peaceful and well freed?”

<!-- @segment sn22.72:1.4 -->

“Surādha, one is freed by not grasping having truly seen any kind of form at all—past, future, or present; internal or external; solid or subtle; inferior or superior; far or near: <em>all</em> form—with right understanding: ‘This is not mine, I am not this, this is not my self.’

<!-- @segment sn22.72:1.5 -->

One is freed by not grasping having truly seen any kind of feeling …

<!-- @segment sn22.72:1.6 -->

perception …

<!-- @segment sn22.72:1.7 -->

choices …

<!-- @segment sn22.72:1.8 -->

consciousness at all—past, future, or present; internal or external; solid or subtle; inferior or superior; far or near: <em>all</em> consciousness—with right understanding: ‘This is not mine, I am not this, this is not my self.’

<!-- @segment sn22.72:1.9 -->



<!-- @segment sn22.72:1.10 -->



<!-- @segment sn22.72:1.11 -->



<!-- @segment sn22.72:1.12 -->

That’s how to know and see so that the heart is rid of I-making, mine-making, and conceit for this conscious body and externally for all signs; and going beyond discrimination, it’s peaceful and well freed.” …

<!-- @segment sn22.72:1.13 -->

And Venerable Surādha became one of the perfected.
