<!-- @segment sn45.37:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn45.37:1.2 -->

“Mendicants, I will teach you life as a brahmin and the fruits of life as a brahmin.

<!-- @segment sn45.37:1.3 -->

Listen …

<!-- @segment sn45.37:1.4 -->

And what is life as a brahmin?

<!-- @segment sn45.37:1.5 -->

It is simply this noble eightfold path, that is:

<!-- @segment sn45.37:1.6 -->

right view, right purpose, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right immersion.

<!-- @segment sn45.37:1.7 -->

This is called life as a brahmin.

<!-- @segment sn45.37:1.8 -->

And what are the fruits of life as a brahmin?

<!-- @segment sn45.37:1.9 -->

The fruits of stream-entry, once-return, non-return, and perfection.

<!-- @segment sn45.37:1.10 -->

These are called the fruits of life as a brahmin.”
