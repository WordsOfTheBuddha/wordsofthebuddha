<!-- @segment sn18.8:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn18.8:1.2 -->

“What do you think, Rāhula?

<!-- @segment sn18.8:1.3 -->

Is craving for sights permanent or impermanent?”

<!-- @segment sn18.8:1.4 -->

“Impermanent, sir.” …

<!-- @segment sn18.8:1.5 -->

“… craving for sounds …

<!-- @segment sn18.8:1.6 -->

craving for smells …

<!-- @segment sn18.8:1.7 -->

craving for tastes …

<!-- @segment sn18.8:1.8 -->

craving for touches …

<!-- @segment sn18.8:1.9 -->

Is craving for ideas permanent or impermanent?”

<!-- @segment sn18.8:1.10 -->

“Impermanent, sir.” …

<!-- @segment sn18.8:1.11 -->

“Seeing this, a learned noble disciple grows disillusioned with craving for sights, sounds, smells, tastes, touches, and ideas. …”

<!-- @segment sn18.8:1.12 -->
