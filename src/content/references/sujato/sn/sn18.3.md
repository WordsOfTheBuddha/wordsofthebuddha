title: Consciousness
description: The Buddha teaches Rāhula to contemplate eye, ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind consciousness as impermanent, suffering, and not-self—not fit to be regarded as “mine, I am this, my self”—until disenchantment, fading of desire, and liberation arise.
qualities: learned, desire
theme: wisdom, training guideline
---

<!-- @segment sn18.3:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn18.3:1.2 -->

“What do you think, Rāhula?

<!-- @segment sn18.3:1.3 -->

Is eye consciousness permanent or impermanent?”

<!-- @segment sn18.3:1.4 -->

“Impermanent, sir.” …

<!-- @segment sn18.3:1.5 -->

“… ear consciousness …

<!-- @segment sn18.3:1.6 -->

nose consciousness …

<!-- @segment sn18.3:1.7 -->

tongue consciousness …

<!-- @segment sn18.3:1.8 -->

body consciousness …

<!-- @segment sn18.3:1.9 -->

Is mind consciousness permanent or impermanent?”

<!-- @segment sn18.3:1.10 -->

“Impermanent, sir.” …

<!-- @segment sn18.3:1.11 -->

“Seeing this, a learned noble disciple grows disillusioned with eye consciousness, ear consciousness, nose consciousness, tongue consciousness, body consciousness, and mind consciousness.

<!-- @segment sn18.3:1.12 -->

Being disillusioned, desire fades away. …”
