title: Mortification

<!-- @segment sn4.1:1.1 -->

So I have heard.

<!-- @segment sn4.1:1.2 -->

At one time, when he was first awakened, the Buddha was staying in Uruvelā at the root of the goatherd’s banyan tree on the bank of the Nerañjarā River.

<!-- @segment sn4.1:1.3 -->

Then as he was in private retreat this thought came to his mind,

<!-- @segment sn4.1:1.4 -->

“I am truly freed from that grueling work!

<!-- @segment sn4.1:1.5 -->

Thank goodness I’m freed from that pointless grueling work.

<!-- @segment sn4.1:1.6 -->

Thank goodness I’m freed and have attained awakening.”

<!-- @segment sn4.1:2.1 -->

And then Māra the Wicked, knowing the Buddha’s train of thought, went up to him and addressed him in verse:

<!-- @segment sn4.1:3.1 -->

“You’ve left the practice of mortification

<!-- @segment sn4.1:3.2 -->

by which sons of men purify themselves.

<!-- @segment sn4.1:3.3 -->

You’re impure, but think yourself pure;

<!-- @segment sn4.1:3.4 -->

you’ve strayed from the path of purity.”

<!-- @segment sn4.1:4.1 -->

Then the Buddha, knowing that this was Māra the Wicked, replied to him in verse:

<!-- @segment sn4.1:5.1 -->

“I realized that it’s pointless;

<!-- @segment sn4.1:5.2 -->

all that endless mortification

<!-- @segment sn4.1:5.3 -->

is as futile

<!-- @segment sn4.1:5.4 -->

as oars and rudder on dry land.

<!-- @segment sn4.1:6.1 -->

Ethics, immersion, and wisdom:

<!-- @segment sn4.1:6.2 -->

by developing this path to awakening

<!-- @segment sn4.1:6.3 -->

I attained ultimate purity.

<!-- @segment sn4.1:6.4 -->

You’re beaten, terminator!”

<!-- @segment sn4.1:7.1 -->

Then Māra the Wicked, thinking, “The Buddha knows me! The Holy One knows me!” miserable and sad, vanished right there.
