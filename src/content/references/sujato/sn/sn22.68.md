<!-- @segment sn22.68:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn22.68:1.2 -->

Then a mendicant went up to the Buddha … and asked him,

<!-- @segment sn22.68:1.3 -->

“Sir, may the Buddha please teach me Dhamma in brief. When I’ve heard it, I’ll live alone, withdrawn, diligent, keen, and resolute.”

<!-- @segment sn22.68:1.4 -->

“Mendicant, give up desire for what is not-self.”

<!-- @segment sn22.68:1.5 -->

“Understood, Blessed One! Understood, Holy One!”

<!-- @segment sn22.68:2.1 -->

“But how do you see the detailed meaning of my brief statement?”

<!-- @segment sn22.68:2.2 -->

“Sir, form is not-self; I should give up desire for it.

<!-- @segment sn22.68:2.3 -->

Feeling …

<!-- @segment sn22.68:2.4 -->

Perception …

<!-- @segment sn22.68:2.5 -->

Choices …

<!-- @segment sn22.68:2.6 -->

Consciousness is not-self; I should give up desire for it.

<!-- @segment sn22.68:2.7 -->

That’s how I understand the detailed meaning of the Buddha’s brief statement.”

<!-- @segment sn22.68:3.1 -->

“Good, good, mendicant!

<!-- @segment sn22.68:3.2 -->

It’s good that you understand the detailed meaning of what I’ve said in brief like this.

<!-- @segment sn22.68:3.3 -->

Form is not-self; you should give up desire for it.

<!-- @segment sn22.68:3.4 -->

Feeling …

<!-- @segment sn22.68:3.5 -->

Perception …

<!-- @segment sn22.68:3.6 -->

Choices …

<!-- @segment sn22.68:3.7 -->

Consciousness is not-self; you should give up desire for it.

<!-- @segment sn22.68:3.8 -->

This is how to understand the detailed meaning of what I said in brief.” …

<!-- @segment sn22.68:3.9 -->

And that mendicant became one of the perfected.
