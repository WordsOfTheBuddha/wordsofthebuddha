title: Intention
description: The Buddha teaches that the arising, continuation, and manifestation of intentions regarding sights, sounds, smells, tastes, touches, and ideas is the arising of suffering, the continuation of disease, and the manifestation of old age and death—and that their cessation is the cessation of suffering.
qualities: desire, ending
theme: principle, urgency
---

<!-- @segment sn26.7:1.1 -->

At Sāvatthī.

<!-- @segment sn26.7:1.2 -->

“Mendicants, the arising of intentions regarding sights …

<!-- @segment sn26.7:1.3 -->



<!-- @segment sn26.7:1.4 -->

intentions regarding ideas … is the manifestation of old age and death.

<!-- @segment sn26.7:1.5 -->

The cessation of intentions regarding sights …

<!-- @segment sn26.7:1.6 -->



<!-- @segment sn26.7:1.7 -->

intentions regarding ideas … is the disappearance of old age and death.”
