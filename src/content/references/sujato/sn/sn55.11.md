<!-- @segment sn55.11:1.1 -->

At one time the Buddha was staying near Sāvatthī in the Royal Monastery.

<!-- @segment sn55.11:1.2 -->

Then a Saṅgha of a thousand nuns went up to the Buddha, bowed, and stood to one side. The Buddha said to them:

<!-- @segment sn55.11:2.1 -->

“Nuns, a noble disciple who has four things is a stream-enterer, not liable to be reborn in the underworld, assured, destined for awakening.

<!-- @segment sn55.11:2.2 -->

What four?

<!-- @segment sn55.11:2.3 -->

It’s when a noble disciple has experiential confidence in the Buddha …

<!-- @segment sn55.11:2.4 -->



<!-- @segment sn55.11:2.5 -->

the teaching …

<!-- @segment sn55.11:2.6 -->

the Saṅgha …

<!-- @segment sn55.11:2.7 -->

And they have the ethical conduct loved by the noble ones … leading to immersion.

<!-- @segment sn55.11:2.8 -->

A noble disciple who has these four things is a stream-enterer, not liable to be reborn in the underworld, assured, destined for awakening.”
