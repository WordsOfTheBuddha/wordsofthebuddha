<!-- @segment ud3.4:1.1 -->

So I have heard.

<!-- @segment ud3.4:1.2 -->

At one time the Buddha was staying near Sāvatthī in Jeta’s Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s monastery.

<!-- @segment ud3.4:1.3 -->

Now at that time Venerable Sāriputta was sitting not far from the Buddha, cross-legged, his body set straight, and mindfulness brought to the present.

<!-- @segment ud3.4:1.4 -->

The Buddha saw him meditating there.

<!-- @segment ud3.4:2.1 -->

Then, understanding this matter, on that occasion the Buddha expressed this heartfelt sentiment:

<!-- @segment ud3.4:3.1 -->

“As a rocky mountain

<!-- @segment ud3.4:3.2 -->

is unwavering and well grounded,

<!-- @segment ud3.4:3.3 -->

so when delusion ends,

<!-- @segment ud3.4:3.4 -->

a monk, like a mountain, doesn’t tremble.”
