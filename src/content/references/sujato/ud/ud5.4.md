<!-- @segment ud5.4:1.1 -->

So I have heard.

<!-- @segment ud5.4:1.2 -->

At one time the Buddha was staying near Sāvatthī in Jeta’s Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s monastery.

<!-- @segment ud5.4:1.3 -->

Now at that time, between Sāvatthī and the Jeta Grove, several boys were tormenting some fish.

<!-- @segment ud5.4:2.1 -->

Then the Buddha robed up in the morning and, taking his bowl and robe, entered Sāvatthī for alms.

<!-- @segment ud5.4:2.2 -->

He saw the boys tormenting the fish.

<!-- @segment ud5.4:2.3 -->

He went to them and said,

<!-- @segment ud5.4:2.4 -->

“Boys, do you fear pain? Do you dislike pain?”

<!-- @segment ud5.4:2.5 -->

“Yes, sir,” they replied. “We dislike pain.”

<!-- @segment ud5.4:3.1 -->

Then, understanding this matter, on that occasion the Buddha expressed this heartfelt sentiment:

<!-- @segment ud5.4:4.1 -->

“If you fear pain,

<!-- @segment ud5.4:4.2 -->

if you dislike pain,

<!-- @segment ud5.4:4.3 -->

don’t do bad deeds

<!-- @segment ud5.4:4.4 -->

either openly or in secret.

<!-- @segment ud5.4:5.1 -->

If you should do a bad deed,

<!-- @segment ud5.4:5.2 -->

now or in the future,

<!-- @segment ud5.4:5.3 -->

you won’t be freed from suffering,

<!-- @segment ud5.4:5.4 -->

though you fly away and flee.”
