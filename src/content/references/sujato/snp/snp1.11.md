title: Victory Over Desire for the Body
---

<!-- @segment snp1.11:1.1 -->

Walking and standing,

<!-- @segment snp1.11:1.2 -->

sitting and lying down,

<!-- @segment snp1.11:1.3 -->

extending and contracting the limbs:

<!-- @segment snp1.11:1.4 -->

these are the movements of the body.

<!-- @segment snp1.11:2.1 -->

Linked together by bones and sinews,

<!-- @segment snp1.11:2.2 -->

plastered over with flesh and hide,

<!-- @segment snp1.11:2.3 -->

and covered by the skin,

<!-- @segment snp1.11:2.4 -->

the body is not seen as it is.

<!-- @segment snp1.11:3.1 -->

It’s full of guts and belly,

<!-- @segment snp1.11:3.2 -->

liver and bladder,

<!-- @segment snp1.11:3.3 -->

heart and lungs,

<!-- @segment snp1.11:3.4 -->

kidney and spleen,

<!-- @segment snp1.11:4.1 -->

spit and snot,

<!-- @segment snp1.11:4.2 -->

sweat and fat,

<!-- @segment snp1.11:4.3 -->

blood and synovial fluid,

<!-- @segment snp1.11:4.4 -->

bile and grease.

<!-- @segment snp1.11:5.1 -->

Then in nine streams

<!-- @segment snp1.11:5.2 -->

the filth is always flowing.

<!-- @segment snp1.11:5.3 -->

There is muck from the eyes,

<!-- @segment snp1.11:5.4 -->

wax from the ears,

<!-- @segment snp1.11:6.1 -->

and snot from the nostrils.

<!-- @segment snp1.11:6.2 -->

The mouth sometimes vomits

<!-- @segment snp1.11:6.3 -->

bile and sometimes phlegm.

<!-- @segment snp1.11:6.4 -->

And from the body, sweat and waste.

<!-- @segment snp1.11:7.1 -->

Then there is the hollow head

<!-- @segment snp1.11:7.2 -->

all filled with brains.

<!-- @segment snp1.11:7.3 -->

Governed by ignorance,

<!-- @segment snp1.11:7.4 -->

the fool thinks it’s lovely.

<!-- @segment snp1.11:8.1 -->

And when it lies dead,

<!-- @segment snp1.11:8.2 -->

bloated and livid,

<!-- @segment snp1.11:8.3 -->

dumped in a charnel ground,

<!-- @segment snp1.11:8.4 -->

the relatives forget it.

<!-- @segment snp1.11:9.1 -->

It’s devoured by dogs,

<!-- @segment snp1.11:9.2 -->

by jackals, wolves, and worms.

<!-- @segment snp1.11:9.3 -->

It’s devoured by crows and vultures,

<!-- @segment snp1.11:9.4 -->

and any other creatures there.

<!-- @segment snp1.11:10.1 -->

A wise mendicant here,

<!-- @segment snp1.11:10.2 -->

having heard the Buddha’s words,

<!-- @segment snp1.11:10.3 -->

fully understands it,

<!-- @segment snp1.11:10.4 -->

for they see it as it is.

<!-- @segment snp1.11:11.1 -->

“As this is, so is that,

<!-- @segment snp1.11:11.2 -->

as that is, so is this.”

<!-- @segment snp1.11:11.3 -->

They’d blanch desire for the body

<!-- @segment snp1.11:11.4 -->

inside and out.

<!-- @segment snp1.11:12.1 -->

That wise mendicant here

<!-- @segment snp1.11:12.2 -->

rid of desire and lust,

<!-- @segment snp1.11:12.3 -->

has found the peace free of death,

<!-- @segment snp1.11:12.4 -->

extinguishment, the state that does not pass.

<!-- @segment snp1.11:13.1 -->

This two-legged body is dirty and stinking,

<!-- @segment snp1.11:13.2 -->

full of different carcasses,

<!-- @segment snp1.11:13.3 -->

and oozing all over the place—

<!-- @segment snp1.11:13.4 -->

but still it is cherished!

<!-- @segment snp1.11:14.1 -->

And if, on account of such a body,

<!-- @segment snp1.11:14.2 -->

someone prides themselves

<!-- @segment snp1.11:14.3 -->

or looks down on others—

<!-- @segment snp1.11:14.4 -->

what is that but a failure to see?
