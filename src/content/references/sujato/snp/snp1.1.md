<!-- @segment snp1.1:1.1 -->

When anger surges, they drive it out,

<!-- @segment snp1.1:1.2 -->

as with medicine a snake’s spreading venom.

<!-- @segment snp1.1:1.3 -->

Such a mendicant sheds the near shore and the far,

<!-- @segment snp1.1:1.4 -->

as a serpent its old worn-out skin.

<!-- @segment snp1.1:2.1 -->

They’ve cut off greed entirely,

<!-- @segment snp1.1:2.2 -->

like a lotus plucked flower and stalk.

<!-- @segment snp1.1:2.3 -->

Such a mendicant sheds the near shore and the far,

<!-- @segment snp1.1:2.4 -->

as a serpent its old worn-out skin.

<!-- @segment snp1.1:3.1 -->

They’ve cut off craving entirely,

<!-- @segment snp1.1:3.2 -->

drying up that swift-flowing stream.

<!-- @segment snp1.1:3.3 -->

Such a mendicant sheds the near shore and the far,

<!-- @segment snp1.1:3.4 -->

as a serpent its old worn-out skin.

<!-- @segment snp1.1:4.1 -->

They’ve swept away conceit entirely,

<!-- @segment snp1.1:4.2 -->

as a fragile bridge of reeds by a great flood.

<!-- @segment snp1.1:4.3 -->

Such a mendicant sheds the near shore and the far,

<!-- @segment snp1.1:4.4 -->

as a serpent its old worn-out skin.

<!-- @segment snp1.1:5.1 -->

In future lives they find no worth,

<!-- @segment snp1.1:5.2 -->

as an inspector of fig trees finds no flower.

<!-- @segment snp1.1:5.3 -->

Such a mendicant sheds the near shore and the far,

<!-- @segment snp1.1:5.4 -->

as a serpent its old worn-out skin.

<!-- @segment snp1.1:6.1 -->

They hide no anger within,

<!-- @segment snp1.1:6.2 -->

passed beyond any kind of existence.

<!-- @segment snp1.1:6.3 -->

Such a mendicant sheds the near shore and the far,

<!-- @segment snp1.1:6.4 -->

as a serpent its old worn-out skin.

<!-- @segment snp1.1:7.1 -->

Their mental vibrations are cleared away,

<!-- @segment snp1.1:7.2 -->

internally clipped off entirely.

<!-- @segment snp1.1:7.3 -->

Such a mendicant sheds the near shore and the far,

<!-- @segment snp1.1:7.4 -->

as a serpent its old worn-out skin.

<!-- @segment snp1.1:8.1 -->

They have not run too far nor run back,

<!-- @segment snp1.1:8.2 -->

but have gone beyond all this proliferation.

<!-- @segment snp1.1:8.3 -->

Such a mendicant sheds the near shore and the far,

<!-- @segment snp1.1:8.4 -->

as a serpent its old worn-out skin.

<!-- @segment snp1.1:9.1 -->

They have not run too far nor run back,

<!-- @segment snp1.1:9.2 -->

for they know that nothing in the world <j>is what it seems.

<!-- @segment snp1.1:9.3 -->

Such a mendicant sheds the near shore and the far,

<!-- @segment snp1.1:9.4 -->

as a serpent its old worn-out skin.

<!-- @segment snp1.1:10.1 -->

They have not run too far nor run back,

<!-- @segment snp1.1:10.2 -->

knowing nothing is what it seems, free of greed.

<!-- @segment snp1.1:10.3 -->

Such a mendicant sheds the near shore and the far,

<!-- @segment snp1.1:10.4 -->

as a serpent its old worn-out skin.

<!-- @segment snp1.1:11.1 -->

They have not run too far nor run back,

<!-- @segment snp1.1:11.2 -->

knowing nothing is what it seems, free of lust.

<!-- @segment snp1.1:11.3 -->

Such a mendicant sheds the near shore and the far,

<!-- @segment snp1.1:11.4 -->

as a serpent its old worn-out skin.

<!-- @segment snp1.1:12.1 -->

They have not run too far nor run back,

<!-- @segment snp1.1:12.2 -->

knowing nothing is what it seems, free of hate.

<!-- @segment snp1.1:12.3 -->

Such a mendicant sheds the near shore and the far,

<!-- @segment snp1.1:12.4 -->

as a serpent its old worn-out skin.

<!-- @segment snp1.1:13.1 -->

They have not run too far nor run back,

<!-- @segment snp1.1:13.2 -->

knowing nothing is what it seems, free of delusion.

<!-- @segment snp1.1:13.3 -->

Such a mendicant sheds the near shore and the far,

<!-- @segment snp1.1:13.4 -->

as a serpent its old worn-out skin.

<!-- @segment snp1.1:14.1 -->

They have no underlying tendencies at all,

<!-- @segment snp1.1:14.2 -->

and are rid of unskillful roots,

<!-- @segment snp1.1:14.3 -->

Such a mendicant sheds the near shore and the far,

<!-- @segment snp1.1:14.4 -->

as a serpent its old worn-out skin.

<!-- @segment snp1.1:15.1 -->

They have nothing born of distress at all

<!-- @segment snp1.1:15.2 -->

to make them to return to the near shore.

<!-- @segment snp1.1:15.3 -->

Such a mendicant sheds the near shore and the far,

<!-- @segment snp1.1:15.4 -->

as a serpent its old worn-out skin.

<!-- @segment snp1.1:16.1 -->

No snarl grows in them at all,

<!-- @segment snp1.1:16.2 -->

that would shackle them to a new life.

<!-- @segment snp1.1:16.3 -->

Such a mendicant sheds the near shore and the far,

<!-- @segment snp1.1:16.4 -->

as a serpent its old worn-out skin.

<!-- @segment snp1.1:17.1 -->

They’ve given up the five hindrances,

<!-- @segment snp1.1:17.2 -->

untroubled, rid of indecision, free of thorns.

<!-- @segment snp1.1:17.3 -->

Such a mendicant sheds the near shore and the far,

<!-- @segment snp1.1:17.4 -->

as a serpent its old worn-out skin.
