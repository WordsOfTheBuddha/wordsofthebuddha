title: The Wealthy Rancher

<!-- @segment snp1.2:1.1 -->

“I’ve boiled my rice and drawn my milk,”

<!-- @segment snp1.2:1.2 -->

said Dhaniya the rancher,

<!-- @segment snp1.2:1.3 -->

“I stay with my family along the bank of the Mahī.

<!-- @segment snp1.2:1.4 -->

My hut is roofed, my sacred fire kindled:

<!-- @segment snp1.2:1.5 -->

so rain forth, heavens, if you wish.”

<!-- @segment snp1.2:2.1 -->

“I boil not with anger <j>and have drawn out hard-heartedness,”

<!-- @segment snp1.2:2.2 -->

said the Buddha,

<!-- @segment snp1.2:2.3 -->

“I stay for one night along the bank of the Mahī.

<!-- @segment snp1.2:2.4 -->

My hut is wide open, my fire is quenched:

<!-- @segment snp1.2:2.5 -->

so rain forth, heavens, if you wish.”

<!-- @segment snp1.2:3.1 -->

“No gadflies or mosquitoes are found,”

<!-- @segment snp1.2:3.2 -->

said Dhaniya,

<!-- @segment snp1.2:3.3 -->

“cows graze on the lush meadow grass.

<!-- @segment snp1.2:3.4 -->

They get by even when the rain comes:

<!-- @segment snp1.2:3.5 -->

so rain forth, heavens, if you wish.”

<!-- @segment snp1.2:4.1 -->

“I bound a raft and prepared it well,”

<!-- @segment snp1.2:4.2 -->

said the Buddha,

<!-- @segment snp1.2:4.3 -->

“and with it I crossed over, went to the far shore, <j>and dispelled the flood.

<!-- @segment snp1.2:4.4 -->

Now I have no need for a raft:

<!-- @segment snp1.2:4.5 -->

so rain forth, heavens, if you wish.”

<!-- @segment snp1.2:5.1 -->

“My wife is obedient, not wanton,”

<!-- @segment snp1.2:5.2 -->

said Dhaniya,

<!-- @segment snp1.2:5.3 -->

“long have we lived together happily.

<!-- @segment snp1.2:5.4 -->

I hear nothing bad about her:

<!-- @segment snp1.2:5.5 -->

so rain forth, heavens, if you wish.”

<!-- @segment snp1.2:6.1 -->

“My mind is obedient and freed,”

<!-- @segment snp1.2:6.2 -->

said the Buddha,

<!-- @segment snp1.2:6.3 -->

“long nurtured and well-tamed.

<!-- @segment snp1.2:6.4 -->

Nothing bad is found in me:

<!-- @segment snp1.2:6.5 -->

so rain forth, heavens, if you wish.”

<!-- @segment snp1.2:7.1 -->

“I am self-employed,”

<!-- @segment snp1.2:7.2 -->

said Dhaniya,

<!-- @segment snp1.2:7.3 -->

“and my healthy children likewise.

<!-- @segment snp1.2:7.4 -->

I hear nothing bad about them:

<!-- @segment snp1.2:7.5 -->

so rain forth, heavens, if you wish.”

<!-- @segment snp1.2:8.1 -->

“I am no-one’s lackey,”

<!-- @segment snp1.2:8.2 -->

said the Buddha,

<!-- @segment snp1.2:8.3 -->

“with what I have earned I wander the world.

<!-- @segment snp1.2:8.4 -->

I have no need for wages:

<!-- @segment snp1.2:8.5 -->

so rain forth, heavens, if you wish.”

<!-- @segment snp1.2:9.1 -->

“I have heifers and sucklings,”

<!-- @segment snp1.2:9.2 -->

said Dhaniya,

<!-- @segment snp1.2:9.3 -->

“cows in calf and breeding cows.

<!-- @segment snp1.2:9.4 -->

I’ve also got a bull, captain of the herd here:

<!-- @segment snp1.2:9.5 -->

so rain forth, heavens, if you wish.”

<!-- @segment snp1.2:10.1 -->

“I have no heifers or sucklings,”

<!-- @segment snp1.2:10.2 -->

said the Buddha,

<!-- @segment snp1.2:10.3 -->

“no cows in calf or breeding cows.

<!-- @segment snp1.2:10.4 -->

I haven’t got a bull, captain of the herd here:

<!-- @segment snp1.2:10.5 -->

so rain forth, heavens, if you wish.”

<!-- @segment snp1.2:11.1 -->

“The stakes are driven in, unshakable,”

<!-- @segment snp1.2:11.2 -->

said Dhaniya,

<!-- @segment snp1.2:11.3 -->

“The grass halters are new and well-woven,

<!-- @segment snp1.2:11.4 -->

not even the sucklings can break them:

<!-- @segment snp1.2:11.5 -->

so rain forth, heavens, if you wish.”

<!-- @segment snp1.2:12.1 -->

“Like a bull I broke the bonds,”

<!-- @segment snp1.2:12.2 -->

said the Buddha,

<!-- @segment snp1.2:12.3 -->

“like an elephant I snapped the vine.

<!-- @segment snp1.2:12.4 -->

I will never lie in a womb again:

<!-- @segment snp1.2:12.5 -->

so rain forth, heavens, if you wish.”

<!-- @segment snp1.2:13.1 -->

Right then a vast cloud rained forth,

<!-- @segment snp1.2:13.2 -->

soaking the uplands and valleys.

<!-- @segment snp1.2:13.3 -->

Hearing the heavens rain down,

<!-- @segment snp1.2:13.4 -->

Dhaniya said this:

<!-- @segment snp1.2:14.1 -->

“It is no small gain for us

<!-- @segment snp1.2:14.2 -->

that we have seen the Buddha.

<!-- @segment snp1.2:14.3 -->

We come to you for refuge, Clear-eyed One.

<!-- @segment snp1.2:14.4 -->

O great sage, please be our Teacher.

<!-- @segment snp1.2:15.1 -->

My wife and I, obedient,

<!-- @segment snp1.2:15.2 -->

shall lead the spiritual life under the Holy One.

<!-- @segment snp1.2:15.3 -->

Gone beyond birth and death,

<!-- @segment snp1.2:15.4 -->

we shall make an end of suffering.”

<!-- @segment snp1.2:16.1 -->

“Children bring you delight!”

<!-- @segment snp1.2:16.2 -->

said Māra the Wicked,

<!-- @segment snp1.2:16.3 -->

“Cattle also bring you delight!

<!-- @segment snp1.2:16.4 -->

For attachments are a man’s delight;

<!-- @segment snp1.2:16.5 -->

without attachments there’s no delight.”

<!-- @segment snp1.2:17.1 -->

“Your children bring you sorrow,”

<!-- @segment snp1.2:17.2 -->

said the Buddha,

<!-- @segment snp1.2:17.3 -->

“Your cattle also bring you sorrow.

<!-- @segment snp1.2:17.4 -->

For attachments are a man’s sorrow;

<!-- @segment snp1.2:17.5 -->

without attachments there are no sorrows.”
