<!-- @segment snp5.17:1.1 -->

“I am old, feeble, and pallid,”

<!-- @segment snp5.17:1.2 -->

said Venerable Piṅgiya,

<!-- @segment snp5.17:1.3 -->

“my eyes unclear, my hearing faint—

<!-- @segment snp5.17:1.4 -->

don’t let me perish while still confused.

<!-- @segment snp5.17:1.5 -->

Explain the teaching so that I may understand

<!-- @segment snp5.17:1.6 -->

the giving up of rebirth and old age here.”

<!-- @segment snp5.17:2.1 -->

“Seeing them being harmed <j>on account of forms,”

<!-- @segment snp5.17:2.2 -->

replied the Buddha,

<!-- @segment snp5.17:2.3 -->

“those negligent folk are inflicted <j>on account of forms—

<!-- @segment snp5.17:2.4 -->

therefore, Piṅgiya, being diligent,

<!-- @segment snp5.17:2.5 -->

give up form so as not to be reborn.”

<!-- @segment snp5.17:3.1 -->

“The four quarters, the intermediate directions,

<!-- @segment snp5.17:3.2 -->

below, and above: in these ten directions

<!-- @segment snp5.17:3.3 -->

there’s nothing at all in the world

<!-- @segment snp5.17:3.4 -->

that you’ve not seen, heard, thought, or cognized.

<!-- @segment snp5.17:3.5 -->

Explain the teaching so that I may understand

<!-- @segment snp5.17:3.6 -->

the giving up of rebirth and old age here.”

<!-- @segment snp5.17:4.1 -->

“Observing mankind sunk in craving,”

<!-- @segment snp5.17:4.2 -->

replied the Buddha,

<!-- @segment snp5.17:4.3 -->

“tormented, mired in old age;

<!-- @segment snp5.17:4.4 -->

therefore, Piṅgiya, being diligent,

<!-- @segment snp5.17:4.5 -->

give up craving so as not to be reborn.”
