<!-- @segment snp5.18:1.1 -->

This was said by the Buddha while staying in the land of the Magadhans at the Pāsāṇaka Shrine.

<!-- @segment snp5.18:1.2 -->

When requested by the sixteen brahmin devotees, he answered their questions one by one.

<!-- @segment snp5.18:1.3 -->

If you understand the meaning and the teaching of each of these questions, and practice in line with the teaching, you may go right to the far shore of old age and death.

<!-- @segment snp5.18:1.4 -->

These teachings are said to lead to the far shore, which is why the name of this exposition of the teaching is “The Way to the Far Shore”.

<!-- @segment snp5.18:2.1 -->

Ajita, Tissametteyya,

<!-- @segment snp5.18:2.2 -->

Puṇṇaka and Mettagū,

<!-- @segment snp5.18:2.3 -->

Dhotaka and Upasiva,

<!-- @segment snp5.18:2.4 -->

Nanda and then Hemaka,

<!-- @segment snp5.18:3.1 -->

both Todeyya and Kappa,

<!-- @segment snp5.18:3.2 -->

and Jatukaṇṇī the astute,

<!-- @segment snp5.18:3.3 -->

Bhadrāvudha and Udaya,

<!-- @segment snp5.18:3.4 -->

and the brahmin Posāla,

<!-- @segment snp5.18:3.5 -->

Mogharāja the intelligent,

<!-- @segment snp5.18:3.6 -->

and Piṅgiya the great seer:

<!-- @segment snp5.18:4.1 -->

they approached the Buddha,

<!-- @segment snp5.18:4.2 -->

the seer of consummate conduct.

<!-- @segment snp5.18:4.3 -->

Asking their subtle questions,

<!-- @segment snp5.18:4.4 -->

they came to the most excellent Buddha.

<!-- @segment snp5.18:5.1 -->

The Buddha answered their questions

<!-- @segment snp5.18:5.2 -->

in accordance with truth.

<!-- @segment snp5.18:5.3 -->

The sage satisfied the brahmins

<!-- @segment snp5.18:5.4 -->

with his answers to their questions.

<!-- @segment snp5.18:6.1 -->

Those who were satisfied by the Clear-eyed One,

<!-- @segment snp5.18:6.2 -->

the Buddha, Kinsman of the Sun,

<!-- @segment snp5.18:6.3 -->

led the spiritual life in his presence,

<!-- @segment snp5.18:6.4 -->

the one of such outstanding wisdom.

<!-- @segment snp5.18:7.1 -->

If you practice in accordance

<!-- @segment snp5.18:7.2 -->

with each of these questions

<!-- @segment snp5.18:7.3 -->

as taught by the Buddha,

<!-- @segment snp5.18:7.4 -->

you’ll go from the near shore to the far.

<!-- @segment snp5.18:8.1 -->

Developing the supreme path,

<!-- @segment snp5.18:8.2 -->

you’ll go from the near shore to the far.

<!-- @segment snp5.18:8.3 -->

This path is for going to the far shore;

<!-- @segment snp5.18:8.4 -->

that’s why it’s called “The Way to the Far Shore”.

<!-- @segment snp5.19:1.1 -->

“I shall keep reciting the Way to the Far Shore,”

<!-- @segment snp5.19:1.2 -->

said Venerable Piṅgiya,

<!-- @segment snp5.19:1.3 -->

“which was taught as it was seen

<!-- @segment snp5.19:1.4 -->

by the immaculate one of vast intelligence.

<!-- @segment snp5.19:1.5 -->

He is desireless, unentangled, a spiritual giant:

<!-- @segment snp5.19:1.6 -->

why would he speak falsely?

<!-- @segment snp5.19:2.1 -->

Come, let me extol

<!-- @segment snp5.19:2.2 -->

in sweet words of praise

<!-- @segment snp5.19:2.3 -->

the one who’s given up stains and delusions,

<!-- @segment snp5.19:2.4 -->

conceit and contempt.

<!-- @segment snp5.19:3.1 -->

The Buddha, all-seer, dispeller of darkness,

<!-- @segment snp5.19:3.2 -->

has gone to world’s end, beyond all rebirths;

<!-- @segment snp5.19:3.3 -->

he is free of defilements, <j>and has given up all suffering,

<!-- @segment snp5.19:3.4 -->

the rightly-named one, brahmin, is revered by me.

<!-- @segment snp5.19:4.1 -->

Like a bird that flees a little copse,

<!-- @segment snp5.19:4.2 -->

to roost in a forest abounding in fruit,

<!-- @segment snp5.19:4.3 -->

I’ve left the near-sighted behind,

<!-- @segment snp5.19:4.4 -->

like a swan come to a great river.

<!-- @segment snp5.19:5.1 -->

Those who explained to me previously,

<!-- @segment snp5.19:5.2 -->

before Gotama’s teaching,

<!-- @segment snp5.19:5.3 -->

said ‘thus it was’ or ‘so it shall be’.

<!-- @segment snp5.19:5.4 -->

All that was just the testament of hearsay;

<!-- @segment snp5.19:5.5 -->

all that just fostered speculation.

<!-- @segment snp5.19:6.1 -->

Alone, the dispeller of darkness

<!-- @segment snp5.19:6.2 -->

is brilliant, a beacon:

<!-- @segment snp5.19:6.3 -->

Gotama, vast in wisdom,

<!-- @segment snp5.19:6.4 -->

Gotama, vast in intelligence.

<!-- @segment snp5.19:7.1 -->

He is the one who taught me Dhamma,

<!-- @segment snp5.19:7.2 -->

apparent in the present life, immediately effective,

<!-- @segment snp5.19:7.3 -->

the untroubled, the end of craving,

<!-- @segment snp5.19:7.4 -->

to which there is no compare.”

<!-- @segment snp5.19:8.1 -->

“Why would you dwell apart from him

<!-- @segment snp5.19:8.2 -->

even for an hour, Piṅgiya?

<!-- @segment snp5.19:8.3 -->

From Gotama, vast in wisdom,

<!-- @segment snp5.19:8.4 -->

from Gotama, vast in intelligence?

<!-- @segment snp5.19:9.1 -->

He is the one who taught you Dhamma,

<!-- @segment snp5.19:9.2 -->

apparent in the present life, immediately effective,

<!-- @segment snp5.19:9.3 -->

the untroubled, the end of craving,

<!-- @segment snp5.19:9.4 -->

to which there is no compare.”

<!-- @segment snp5.19:10.1 -->

“I never dwell apart from him,

<!-- @segment snp5.19:10.2 -->

not even for an hour, brahmin.

<!-- @segment snp5.19:10.3 -->

From Gotama, vast in wisdom,

<!-- @segment snp5.19:10.4 -->

from Gotama, vast in intelligence.

<!-- @segment snp5.19:11.1 -->

He is the one who taught me Dhamma,

<!-- @segment snp5.19:11.2 -->

apparent in the present life, immediately effective,

<!-- @segment snp5.19:11.3 -->

the untroubled, the end of craving,

<!-- @segment snp5.19:11.4 -->

to which there is no compare.

<!-- @segment snp5.19:12.1 -->

Being diligent, I see him

<!-- @segment snp5.19:12.2 -->

in my mind’s eye day and night.

<!-- @segment snp5.19:12.3 -->

I spend the night in homage to him,

<!-- @segment snp5.19:12.4 -->

hence I think I dwell with him.

<!-- @segment snp5.19:13.1 -->

My faith and joy and intent and mindfulness

<!-- @segment snp5.19:13.2 -->

never stray from Gotama’s teaching.

<!-- @segment snp5.19:13.3 -->

I bow to whatever direction

<!-- @segment snp5.19:13.4 -->

the one of vast wisdom heads.

<!-- @segment snp5.19:14.1 -->

I’m old and feeble,

<!-- @segment snp5.19:14.2 -->

so my body cannot go there,

<!-- @segment snp5.19:14.3 -->

but I always travel in my thoughts,

<!-- @segment snp5.19:14.4 -->

for my mind, brahmin, is bound to him.

<!-- @segment snp5.19:15.1 -->

Lying floundering in the mud,

<!-- @segment snp5.19:15.2 -->

I drifted from island to island.

<!-- @segment snp5.19:15.3 -->

Then I saw the Buddha,

<!-- @segment snp5.19:15.4 -->

the undefiled one who has crossed the flood.”

<!-- @segment snp5.19:16.1 -->

“Just as Vakkali was committed to faith—

<!-- @segment snp5.19:16.2 -->

Bhadrāvudha and Gotama of Āḷavī too—

<!-- @segment snp5.19:16.3 -->

so too you should commit to faith.

<!-- @segment snp5.19:16.4 -->

You will go, Piṅgiya, beyond the domain of death.”

<!-- @segment snp5.19:17.1 -->

“My confidence grows

<!-- @segment snp5.19:17.2 -->

as I hear the word of the sage,

<!-- @segment snp5.19:17.3 -->

the Buddha with veil cast off,

<!-- @segment snp5.19:17.4 -->

so kind and eloquent.

<!-- @segment snp5.19:18.1 -->

Having directly known all about the gods,

<!-- @segment snp5.19:18.2 -->

he understands all top to bottom,

<!-- @segment snp5.19:18.3 -->

the teacher who settles all questions

<!-- @segment snp5.19:18.4 -->

for those who admit their doubts.

<!-- @segment snp5.19:19.1 -->

The unfaltering, the unshakable:

<!-- @segment snp5.19:19.2 -->

that to which there is no compare.

<!-- @segment snp5.19:19.3 -->

For sure I will go there, I have no doubt of that.

<!-- @segment snp5.19:19.4 -->

Remember me as one whose mind is made up.”
