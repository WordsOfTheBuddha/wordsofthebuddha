title: A Righteous Life

<!-- @segment snp2.6:1.1 -->

A righteous life, a spiritual life,

<!-- @segment snp2.6:1.2 -->

they call this the supreme treasure.

<!-- @segment snp2.6:1.3 -->

But if someone goes forth

<!-- @segment snp2.6:1.4 -->

from the lay life to homelessness

<!-- @segment snp2.6:2.1 -->

who is of scurrilous character,

<!-- @segment snp2.6:2.2 -->

a beast and a bully,

<!-- @segment snp2.6:2.3 -->

their life gets worse,

<!-- @segment snp2.6:2.4 -->

as poison grows inside them.

<!-- @segment snp2.6:3.1 -->

A mendicant who loves to quarrel,

<!-- @segment snp2.6:3.2 -->

wrapped in delusion,

<!-- @segment snp2.6:3.3 -->

doesn’t even know what’s been explained

<!-- @segment snp2.6:3.4 -->

in the Dhamma taught by the Buddha.

<!-- @segment snp2.6:4.1 -->

Harassing those who are evolved,

<!-- @segment snp2.6:4.2 -->

governed by ignorance,

<!-- @segment snp2.6:4.3 -->

they don’t know that corruption

<!-- @segment snp2.6:4.4 -->

is the path that leads to hell.

<!-- @segment snp2.6:5.1 -->

Entering the underworld,

<!-- @segment snp2.6:5.2 -->

passing from womb to womb, <j>from darkness to darkness,

<!-- @segment snp2.6:5.3 -->

such a mendicant

<!-- @segment snp2.6:5.4 -->

falls into suffering after death.

<!-- @segment snp2.6:6.1 -->

One such as that is

<!-- @segment snp2.6:6.2 -->

like a sewer

<!-- @segment snp2.6:6.3 -->

brimful with years of filth

<!-- @segment snp2.6:6.4 -->

for it’s hard to clean one full of grime.

<!-- @segment snp2.6:7.1 -->

Mendicants, knowing that someone is like this,

<!-- @segment snp2.6:7.2 -->

attached to the domestic life,

<!-- @segment snp2.6:7.3 -->

of corrupt wishes and wicked intent,

<!-- @segment snp2.6:7.4 -->

of bad behavior and alms-resort,

<!-- @segment snp2.6:8.1 -->

then having gathered in harmony,

<!-- @segment snp2.6:8.2 -->

you should expel them.

<!-- @segment snp2.6:8.3 -->

Throw out the trash!

<!-- @segment snp2.6:8.4 -->

Get rid of the rubbish!

<!-- @segment snp2.6:9.1 -->

And sweep away the scraps—

<!-- @segment snp2.6:9.2 -->

they’re not ascetics, they just think they are.

<!-- @segment snp2.6:9.3 -->

When you’ve thrown out those of corrupt wishes,

<!-- @segment snp2.6:9.4 -->

of bad behavior and alms-resort,

<!-- @segment snp2.6:10.1 -->

dwell in communion, ever mindful,

<!-- @segment snp2.6:10.2 -->

the pure with the pure.

<!-- @segment snp2.6:10.3 -->

Then in harmony, alert,

<!-- @segment snp2.6:10.4 -->

you’ll make an end of suffering.
