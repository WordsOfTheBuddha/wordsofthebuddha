<!-- @segment iti87:1.1 -->

This was said by the Buddha, the Perfected One: that is what I heard.

<!-- @segment iti87:2.1 -->

“Mendicants, these three unskillful thoughts are destroyers of sight, vision, and knowledge. They block wisdom, they’re on the side of distress, and they don’t lead to extinguishment.

<!-- @segment iti87:2.2 -->

What three?

<!-- @segment iti87:2.3 -->

Thoughts of sensuality,

<!-- @segment iti87:2.4 -->

malice,

<!-- @segment iti87:2.5 -->

and cruelty.

<!-- @segment iti87:2.6 -->

These are the three unskillful thoughts that are destroyers of sight, vision, and knowledge. They block wisdom, they’re on the side of distress, and they don’t lead to extinguishment.

<!-- @segment iti87:3.1 -->

These three skillful thoughts are creators of sight, vision, and knowledge. They grow wisdom, they’re on the side of solace, and they lead to extinguishment.

<!-- @segment iti87:3.2 -->

What three?

<!-- @segment iti87:3.3 -->

Thoughts of renunciation,

<!-- @segment iti87:3.4 -->

good will,

<!-- @segment iti87:3.5 -->

and harmlessness.

<!-- @segment iti87:3.6 -->

These are the three skillful thoughts that are creators of sight, vision, and knowledge. They grow wisdom, they’re on the side of solace, and they lead to extinguishment.”

<!-- @segment iti87:3.7 -->

The Buddha spoke this matter.

<!-- @segment iti87:3.8 -->

On this it is said:

<!-- @segment iti87:4.1 -->

“Think the three skillful thoughts,

<!-- @segment iti87:4.2 -->

and get rid of the unskillful.

<!-- @segment iti87:4.3 -->

Quelling such thoughts and considerations,

<!-- @segment iti87:4.4 -->

like rain on the dust,

<!-- @segment iti87:4.5 -->

with a heart calmed of thought,

<!-- @segment iti87:4.6 -->

you’ll touch the state of peace right here.”

<!-- @segment iti87:5.1 -->

This too is a matter that was spoken by the Blessed One: that is what I heard.
