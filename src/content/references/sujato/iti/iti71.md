<!-- @segment iti71:1.1 -->

This was said by the Buddha, the Perfected One: that is what I heard.

<!-- @segment iti71:2.1 -->

“Mendicants, I’ve seen beings who engaged in good conduct of body, speech, and mind, who did not denounce the noble ones, who held right view and acted accordingly.

<!-- @segment iti71:2.2 -->

At the breaking up of the body, after death, they were reborn in a good destination, a heavenly realm.

<!-- @segment iti71:3.1 -->

Now, I don’t say this because I’ve heard it from some other ascetic or brahmin.

<!-- @segment iti71:3.4 -->

I only say it because I’ve known, seen, and realized it for myself.”

<!-- @segment iti71:4.3 -->

The Buddha spoke this matter.

<!-- @segment iti71:4.4 -->

On this it is said:

<!-- @segment iti71:5.1 -->

“When the mind has been directed right,

<!-- @segment iti71:5.2 -->

and words rightly spoken,

<!-- @segment iti71:5.3 -->

and right bodily deeds have been done,

<!-- @segment iti71:5.4 -->

an individual here

<!-- @segment iti71:6.1 -->

learned, doer of good deeds,

<!-- @segment iti71:6.2 -->

though their life may be short,

<!-- @segment iti71:6.3 -->

when their body breaks up, that wise person

<!-- @segment iti71:6.4 -->

is reborn in heaven.”

<!-- @segment iti71:7.1 -->

This too is a matter that was spoken by the Blessed One: that is what I heard.
