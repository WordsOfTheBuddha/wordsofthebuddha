<!-- @segment iti79:1.1 -->

This was said by the Buddha, the Perfected One: that is what I heard.

<!-- @segment iti79:2.1 -->

“These three things lead to the decline of a mendicant trainee.

<!-- @segment iti79:2.2 -->

What three?

<!-- @segment iti79:2.3 -->

It’s when a mendicant relishes work,

<!-- @segment iti79:2.4 -->

talk,

<!-- @segment iti79:2.5 -->

and sleep.

<!-- @segment iti79:2.6 -->

These three things lead to the decline of a mendicant trainee.

<!-- @segment iti79:3.1 -->

These three things don’t lead to the decline of a mendicant trainee.

<!-- @segment iti79:3.2 -->

What three?

<!-- @segment iti79:3.3 -->

It’s when a mendicant doesn’t relish work,

<!-- @segment iti79:3.4 -->

talk,

<!-- @segment iti79:3.5 -->

and sleep.

<!-- @segment iti79:3.6 -->

These three things don’t lead to the decline of a mendicant trainee.”

<!-- @segment iti79:3.7 -->

The Buddha spoke this matter.

<!-- @segment iti79:3.8 -->

On this it is said:

<!-- @segment iti79:4.1 -->

“Restless, they relish

<!-- @segment iti79:4.2 -->

work, talk, and sleep.

<!-- @segment iti79:4.3 -->

Such a mendicant is incapable

<!-- @segment iti79:4.4 -->

of experiencing the highest awakening.

<!-- @segment iti79:5.1 -->

That’s why one ought have few duties,

<!-- @segment iti79:5.2 -->

being wakeful not restless.

<!-- @segment iti79:5.3 -->

Such a mendicant is capable

<!-- @segment iti79:5.4 -->

of experiencing the highest awakening.”

<!-- @segment iti79:6.1 -->

This too is a matter that was spoken by the Blessed One: that is what I heard.
