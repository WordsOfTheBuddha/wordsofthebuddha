<!-- @segment iti56:1.1 -->

This was said by the Buddha, the Perfected One: that is what I heard.

<!-- @segment iti56:2.1 -->

“Mendicants, there are these three defilements.

<!-- @segment iti56:2.2 -->

What three?

<!-- @segment iti56:2.3 -->

The defilements of sensuality, desire to be reborn, and ignorance.

<!-- @segment iti56:2.4 -->

These are the three defilements.”

<!-- @segment iti56:2.5 -->

The Buddha spoke this matter.

<!-- @segment iti56:2.6 -->

On this it is said:

<!-- @segment iti56:3.1 -->

“Stilled, aware,

<!-- @segment iti56:3.2 -->

a mindful disciple of the Buddha

<!-- @segment iti56:3.3 -->

understands defilements,

<!-- @segment iti56:3.4 -->

the cause of defilements,

<!-- @segment iti56:4.1 -->

where they cease,

<!-- @segment iti56:4.2 -->

and the path that leads to their ending.

<!-- @segment iti56:4.3 -->

With the ending of defilements, a mendicant

<!-- @segment iti56:4.4 -->

is hungerless, quenched.”

<!-- @segment iti56:5.1 -->

This too is a matter that was spoken by the Blessed One: that is what I heard.
