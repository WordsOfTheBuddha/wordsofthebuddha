<!-- @segment iti48:1.1 -->

This was said by the Buddha, the Perfected One: that is what I heard.

<!-- @segment iti48:2.1 -->

“Mendicants, these two are bound for a place of loss, bound for hell, if they don’t give up this fault.

<!-- @segment iti48:2.2 -->

What two?

<!-- @segment iti48:2.3 -->

Someone who is unchaste, but claims to be chaste; and someone who makes a groundless accusation of unchastity against a person whose chastity is pure.

<!-- @segment iti48:2.4 -->

These are the two who are bound for a place of loss, bound for hell, if they don’t give up this fault.”

<!-- @segment iti48:2.5 -->

The Buddha spoke this matter.

<!-- @segment iti48:2.6 -->

On this it is said:

<!-- @segment iti48:3.1 -->

“A liar goes to hell,

<!-- @segment iti48:3.2 -->

as does one who denies what they did.

<!-- @segment iti48:3.3 -->

Both are equal in the hereafter,

<!-- @segment iti48:3.4 -->

those men of base deeds.

<!-- @segment iti48:4.1 -->

Many who wear a scrap of ocher cloth

<!-- @segment iti48:4.2 -->

are unrestrained and wicked.

<!-- @segment iti48:4.3 -->

Being wicked, they are reborn in hell

<!-- @segment iti48:4.4 -->

due to their bad deeds.

<!-- @segment iti48:5.1 -->

It’d be better for the immoral and unrestrained

<!-- @segment iti48:5.2 -->

to eat an iron ball,

<!-- @segment iti48:5.3 -->

scorching, like a burning flame,

<!-- @segment iti48:5.4 -->

than to eat the nation’s alms.”

<!-- @segment iti48:6.1 -->

This too is a matter that was spoken by the Blessed One: that is what I heard.
