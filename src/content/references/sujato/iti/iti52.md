<!-- @segment iti52:1.1 -->

This was said by the Buddha, the Perfected One: that is what I heard.

<!-- @segment iti52:2.1 -->

“Mendicants, there are these three feelings.

<!-- @segment iti52:2.2 -->

What three?

<!-- @segment iti52:2.3 -->

Pleasant, painful, and neutral feeling.

<!-- @segment iti52:2.4 -->

These are the three feelings.”

<!-- @segment iti52:2.5 -->

The Buddha spoke this matter.

<!-- @segment iti52:2.6 -->

On this it is said:

<!-- @segment iti52:3.1 -->

“Stilled, aware,

<!-- @segment iti52:3.2 -->

a mindful disciple of the Buddha

<!-- @segment iti52:3.3 -->

understands feelings,

<!-- @segment iti52:3.4 -->

the cause of feelings,

<!-- @segment iti52:4.1 -->

where they cease,

<!-- @segment iti52:4.2 -->

and the path that leads to their ending.

<!-- @segment iti52:4.3 -->

With the ending of feelings, a mendicant

<!-- @segment iti52:4.4 -->

is hungerless, quenched.”

<!-- @segment iti52:5.1 -->

This too is a matter that was spoken by the Blessed One: that is what I heard.
