<!-- @segment iti75:1.1 -->

This was said by the Buddha, the Perfected One: that is what I heard.

<!-- @segment iti75:2.1 -->

“Mendicants, these three individuals are found in the world.

<!-- @segment iti75:2.2 -->

What three?

<!-- @segment iti75:2.3 -->

One like a rainless cloud, one who rains locally, one who rains all over.

<!-- @segment iti75:3.1 -->

And how is an individual like a rainless cloud?

<!-- @segment iti75:3.2 -->

It’s when an individual doesn’t give to anyone at all—whether ascetics and brahmins, paupers, vagrants, supplicants, or beggars—such things as food, drink, clothing, vehicles; garlands, fragrance, and makeup; and bed, house, and lighting.

<!-- @segment iti75:3.3 -->

That’s how an individual is like a rainless cloud.

<!-- @segment iti75:4.1 -->

And how does an individual rain locally?

<!-- @segment iti75:4.2 -->

It’s when an individual gives to some but not to others—whether ascetics and brahmins, paupers, vagrants, supplicants, or beggars—such things as food, drink, clothing, vehicles; garlands, fragrance, and makeup; and bed, house, and lighting.

<!-- @segment iti75:4.3 -->

That’s how an individual rains locally.

<!-- @segment iti75:5.1 -->

And how does an individual rain all over?

<!-- @segment iti75:5.2 -->

It’s when an individual gives to everyone—whether ascetics and brahmins, paupers, vagrants, supplicants, or beggars—such things as food, drink, clothing, vehicles; garlands, fragrance, and makeup; and bed, house, and lighting.

<!-- @segment iti75:5.3 -->

That’s how an individual rains all over.

<!-- @segment iti75:5.4 -->

These are the three individuals found in the world.”

<!-- @segment iti75:5.5 -->

The Buddha spoke this matter.

<!-- @segment iti75:5.6 -->

On this it is said:

<!-- @segment iti75:6.1 -->

“They don’t share the food and drink

<!-- @segment iti75:6.2 -->

they have acquired

<!-- @segment iti75:6.3 -->

with ascetics or brahmins,

<!-- @segment iti75:6.4 -->

with paupers, vagrants, or supplicants.

<!-- @segment iti75:6.5 -->

They’re like a rainless cloud,

<!-- @segment iti75:6.6 -->

they say, the meanest of men.

<!-- @segment iti75:7.1 -->

They don’t give to some,

<!-- @segment iti75:7.2 -->

to some they provide.

<!-- @segment iti75:7.3 -->

They rain locally,

<!-- @segment iti75:7.4 -->

so say the wise.

<!-- @segment iti75:8.1 -->

Compassionate for all beings,

<!-- @segment iti75:8.2 -->

that person distributes

<!-- @segment iti75:8.3 -->

abundant food upon request,

<!-- @segment iti75:8.4 -->

saying, “Give! Give!”

<!-- @segment iti75:9.1 -->

The rain cloud rains forth,

<!-- @segment iti75:9.2 -->

having thundered and roared,

<!-- @segment iti75:9.3 -->

drenching the earth with water,

<!-- @segment iti75:9.4 -->

soaking the uplands and valleys.

<!-- @segment iti75:10.1 -->

Even so, such an individual,

<!-- @segment iti75:10.2 -->

having accumulated wealth

<!-- @segment iti75:10.3 -->

by legitimate means,

<!-- @segment iti75:10.4 -->

through their own hard work,

<!-- @segment iti75:10.5 -->

rightly satisfies with food and drink

<!-- @segment iti75:10.6 -->

those who have become supplicants.”

<!-- @segment iti75:11.1 -->

This too is a matter that was spoken by the Blessed One: that is what I heard.
