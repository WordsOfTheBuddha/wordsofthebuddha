<!-- @segment iti95:1.1 -->

This was said by the Buddha, the Perfected One: that is what I heard.

<!-- @segment iti95:2.1 -->

“Mendicants, there are these three ways of being provided with sensual pleasures.

<!-- @segment iti95:2.2 -->

What three?

<!-- @segment iti95:2.3 -->

Some sensual pleasures are simply present; some are for those who love to create; and some are for those who control what is created by others.

<!-- @segment iti95:2.4 -->

These are the three ways of being provided with sensual pleasures.”

<!-- @segment iti95:2.5 -->

The Buddha spoke this matter.

<!-- @segment iti95:2.6 -->

On this it is said:

<!-- @segment iti95:3.1 -->

“Sensual pleasures that are simply present,

<!-- @segment iti95:3.2 -->

gods who control what is imagined by others,

<!-- @segment iti95:3.3 -->

gods who love to create,

<!-- @segment iti95:3.4 -->

and others who indulge in sensual pleasures—

<!-- @segment iti95:3.5 -->

They go from this state to another,

<!-- @segment iti95:3.6 -->

but don’t escape transmigration.

<!-- @segment iti95:4.1 -->

Knowing this danger

<!-- @segment iti95:4.2 -->

in sensual indulgence, an astute person

<!-- @segment iti95:4.3 -->

would forsake all sensual pleasures,

<!-- @segment iti95:4.4 -->

both human and heavenly.

<!-- @segment iti95:5.1 -->

Having cut the stream so hard to pass,

<!-- @segment iti95:5.2 -->

that’s tied to pleasant seeming things,

<!-- @segment iti95:5.3 -->

they become fully extinguished,

<!-- @segment iti95:5.4 -->

completely transcending suffering.

<!-- @segment iti95:6.1 -->

Seers of the noble truths, knowledge masters,

<!-- @segment iti95:6.2 -->

the astute, understanding rightly,

<!-- @segment iti95:6.3 -->

directly know the ending of rebirth,

<!-- @segment iti95:6.4 -->

they come not back to future lives.”

<!-- @segment iti95:7.1 -->

This too is a matter that was spoken by the Blessed One: that is what I heard.
