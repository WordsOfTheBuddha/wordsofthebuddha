<!-- @segment iti74:1.1 -->

This was said by the Buddha, the Perfected One: that is what I heard.

<!-- @segment iti74:2.1 -->

“These three kinds of children are found in the world.

<!-- @segment iti74:2.2 -->

What three?

<!-- @segment iti74:2.3 -->

One who betters their birth, one who equals their birth, one who fails their birth.

<!-- @segment iti74:3.1 -->

And how does a child better their birth?

<!-- @segment iti74:3.2 -->

It’s when a child’s parents

<!-- @segment iti74:3.3 -->

have not gone for refuge to the Buddha, the teaching, and the Saṅgha.

<!-- @segment iti74:3.4 -->

They kill living creatures, steal, commit sexual misconduct, lie, and consume intoxicants of beer, wine, and liquor. They’re immoral, of bad character.

<!-- @segment iti74:3.5 -->

But their child

<!-- @segment iti74:3.6 -->

has gone for refuge to the Buddha, the teaching, and the Saṅgha.

<!-- @segment iti74:3.7 -->

They don’t kill living creatures, steal, commit sexual misconduct, lie, or consume intoxicants of beer, wine, and liquor. They’re ethical, of good character.

<!-- @segment iti74:3.8 -->

That’s how a child betters their birth.

<!-- @segment iti74:4.1 -->

And how does a child equal their birth?

<!-- @segment iti74:4.2 -->

It’s when a child’s parents

<!-- @segment iti74:4.3 -->

have gone for refuge to the Buddha, the teaching, and the Saṅgha.

<!-- @segment iti74:4.4 -->

They don’t kill living creatures, steal, commit sexual misconduct, lie, or consume intoxicants of beer, wine, and liquor. They’re ethical, of good character.

<!-- @segment iti74:4.5 -->

And their child

<!-- @segment iti74:4.6 -->

has gone for refuge to the Buddha, the teaching, and the Saṅgha.

<!-- @segment iti74:4.7 -->

They don’t kill living creatures, steal, commit sexual misconduct, lie, or consume intoxicants of beer, wine, and liquor. They’re ethical, of good character.

<!-- @segment iti74:4.8 -->

That’s how a child equals their birth.

<!-- @segment iti74:5.1 -->

And how does a child fail their birth?

<!-- @segment iti74:5.2 -->

It’s when a child’s parents

<!-- @segment iti74:5.3 -->

have gone for refuge to the Buddha, the teaching, and the Saṅgha.

<!-- @segment iti74:5.4 -->

They don’t kill living creatures, steal, commit sexual misconduct, lie, or consume intoxicants of beer, wine, and liquor. They’re ethical, of good character.

<!-- @segment iti74:5.5 -->

But their child

<!-- @segment iti74:5.6 -->

has not gone for refuge to the Buddha, the teaching, and the Saṅgha.

<!-- @segment iti74:5.7 -->

They kill living creatures, steal, commit sexual misconduct, lie, and consume intoxicants of beer, wine, and liquor. They’re immoral, of bad character.

<!-- @segment iti74:5.8 -->

That’s how a child fails their birth.

<!-- @segment iti74:5.9 -->

These are the three kinds of children found in the world.”

<!-- @segment iti74:5.10 -->

The Buddha spoke this matter.

<!-- @segment iti74:5.11 -->

On this it is said:

<!-- @segment iti74:6.1 -->

“The astute wish for a child

<!-- @segment iti74:6.2 -->

who betters or equals their birth;

<!-- @segment iti74:6.3 -->

not one who fails their birth,

<!-- @segment iti74:6.4 -->

disgracing their family.

<!-- @segment iti74:7.1 -->

These are the children in the world

<!-- @segment iti74:7.2 -->

who become lay devotees;

<!-- @segment iti74:7.3 -->

faithful, accomplished in ethics,

<!-- @segment iti74:7.4 -->

bountiful, rid of stinginess.

<!-- @segment iti74:7.5 -->

Like the moon freed from clouds,

<!-- @segment iti74:7.6 -->

they shine in the assemblies.”

<!-- @segment iti74:8.1 -->

This too is a matter that was spoken by the Blessed One: that is what I heard.
