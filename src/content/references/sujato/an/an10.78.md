<!-- @segment an10.78:1.1 -->

“Mendicants, Jain ascetics have ten bad qualities.

<!-- @segment an10.78:1.2 -->

What ten?

<!-- @segment an10.78:1.3 -->

They’re faithless and

<!-- @segment an10.78:1.4 -->

unethical,

<!-- @segment an10.78:1.5 -->

without conscience

<!-- @segment an10.78:1.6 -->

or prudence,

<!-- @segment an10.78:1.7 -->

and devoted to untrue persons.

<!-- @segment an10.78:1.8 -->

They glorify themselves and put others down.

<!-- @segment an10.78:1.9 -->

They’re attached to their own views, holding them tight, and refusing to let go.

<!-- @segment an10.78:1.10 -->

They’re deceptive,

<!-- @segment an10.78:1.11 -->

with corrupt wishes

<!-- @segment an10.78:1.12 -->

and bad friends.

<!-- @segment an10.78:1.13 -->

Jain ascetics possess these ten bad qualities.”
