title: Timidity

<!-- @segment an5.171:1.1 -->

So I have heard.

<!-- @segment an5.171:1.2 -->

At one time the Buddha was staying near Sāvatthī in Jeta’s Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s monastery.

<!-- @segment an5.171:1.3 -->

There the Buddha addressed the mendicants,

<!-- @segment an5.171:1.4 -->

“Mendicants!”

<!-- @segment an5.171:1.5 -->

“Venerable sir,” they replied.

<!-- @segment an5.171:1.6 -->

The Buddha said this:

<!-- @segment an5.171:2.1 -->

“A lay follower with five qualities is overcome by timidity.

<!-- @segment an5.171:2.2 -->

What five?

<!-- @segment an5.171:2.3 -->

They kill living creatures, steal, commit sexual misconduct, lie, and consume intoxicants of beer, wine, and liquor.

<!-- @segment an5.171:2.4 -->

A lay follower with these five qualities is overcome by timidity.

<!-- @segment an5.171:3.1 -->

A lay follower with five qualities is self-assured.

<!-- @segment an5.171:3.2 -->

What five?

<!-- @segment an5.171:3.3 -->

They don’t kill living creatures, steal, commit sexual misconduct, lie, or consume intoxicants of beer, wine, and liquor.

<!-- @segment an5.171:3.4 -->

A lay follower with these five qualities is self-assured.”
