<!-- @segment an10.134:1.1 -->

“Mendicants, I will teach you what is good and what is not good.

<!-- @segment an10.134:1.2 -->

Listen and apply your mind well, I will speak.”

<!-- @segment an10.134:1.3 -->

“Yes, sir,” they replied.

<!-- @segment an10.134:1.4 -->

The Buddha said this:

<!-- @segment an10.134:2.1 -->

“And what, mendicants, is not good?

<!-- @segment an10.134:2.2 -->

Wrong view, wrong purpose, wrong speech, wrong action, wrong livelihood, wrong effort, wrong mindfulness, wrong immersion, wrong knowledge, and wrong freedom.

<!-- @segment an10.134:2.3 -->

This is called what is not good.

<!-- @segment an10.134:2.4 -->

And what is good?

<!-- @segment an10.134:2.5 -->

Right view, right purpose, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, right immersion, right knowledge, and right freedom.

<!-- @segment an10.134:2.6 -->

This is called what is good.”
