title: Non-decline

<!-- @segment an4.37:1.1 -->

“Mendicants, a mendicant who has four qualities can’t decline, and has drawn near to extinguishment.

<!-- @segment an4.37:1.2 -->

What four?

<!-- @segment an4.37:1.3 -->

A mendicant is accomplished in ethics, guards the sense doors, eats in moderation, and is dedicated to wakefulness.

<!-- @segment an4.37:2.1 -->

And how is a mendicant accomplished in ethics?

<!-- @segment an4.37:2.2 -->

It’s when a mendicant is ethical, restrained in the monastic code, conducting themselves well and resorting for alms in suitable places. Seeing danger in the slightest fault, they keep the rules they’ve undertaken.

<!-- @segment an4.37:2.3 -->

That’s how a mendicant is accomplished in ethics.

<!-- @segment an4.37:3.1 -->

And how does a mendicant guard the sense doors?

<!-- @segment an4.37:3.2 -->

When a mendicant sees a sight with their eyes, they don’t get caught up in the features and details.

<!-- @segment an4.37:3.3 -->

If the faculty of sight were left unrestrained, bad unskillful qualities of covetousness and displeasure would become overwhelming. For this reason, they practice restraint, protecting the faculty of sight, and achieving restraint over it.

<!-- @segment an4.37:3.4 -->

Hearing a sound with their ears …

<!-- @segment an4.37:3.5 -->

Smelling an odor with their nose …

<!-- @segment an4.37:3.6 -->

Tasting a flavor with their tongue …

<!-- @segment an4.37:3.7 -->

Encountering a touch with their body …

<!-- @segment an4.37:3.8 -->

Knowing an idea with their mind, they don’t get caught up in the features and details.

<!-- @segment an4.37:3.9 -->

If the faculty of mind were left unrestrained, bad unskillful qualities of covetousness and displeasure would become overwhelming. For this reason, they practice restraint, protecting the faculty of mind, and achieving restraint over it.

<!-- @segment an4.37:3.10 -->

That’s how a mendicant guards the sense doors.

<!-- @segment an4.37:4.1 -->

And how does a mendicant eat in moderation?

<!-- @segment an4.37:4.2 -->

It’s when a mendicant reflects rationally on the food that they eat:

<!-- @segment an4.37:4.3 -->

‘Not for fun, indulgence, adornment, or decoration, but only to sustain this body, to avoid harm, and to support spiritual practice. In this way, I shall put an end to old discomfort and not give rise to new discomfort, and I will have the means to keep going, blamelessness, and a comfortable abiding.’

<!-- @segment an4.37:4.4 -->

That’s how a mendicant eats in moderation.

<!-- @segment an4.37:5.1 -->

And how is a mendicant dedicated to wakefulness?

<!-- @segment an4.37:5.2 -->

It’s when a mendicant practices walking and sitting meditation by day, purifying their mind from obstacles.

<!-- @segment an4.37:5.3 -->

In the first watch of the night, they continue to practice walking and sitting meditation.

<!-- @segment an4.37:5.4 -->

In the middle watch, they lie down in the lion’s posture—on the right side, placing one foot on top of the other—mindful and aware, and focused on the time of getting up.

<!-- @segment an4.37:5.5 -->

In the last watch, they get up and continue to practice walking and sitting meditation, purifying their mind from obstacles.

<!-- @segment an4.37:5.6 -->

This is how a mendicant is dedicated to wakefulness.

<!-- @segment an4.37:5.7 -->

A mendicant who possesses these four qualities can’t decline, and has drawn near to extinguishment.

<!-- @segment an4.37:6.1 -->

Established in ethics,

<!-- @segment an4.37:6.2 -->

restrained in the sense faculties,

<!-- @segment an4.37:6.3 -->

eating in moderation,

<!-- @segment an4.37:6.4 -->

and dedicated to wakefulness;

<!-- @segment an4.37:7.1 -->

a mendicant lives like this, with keen energy,

<!-- @segment an4.37:7.2 -->

tireless all night and day,

<!-- @segment an4.37:7.3 -->

developing skillful qualities,

<!-- @segment an4.37:7.4 -->

for the sake of sanctuary from the yoke.

<!-- @segment an4.37:8.1 -->

A mendicant who loves diligence,

<!-- @segment an4.37:8.2 -->

seeing fear in negligence,

<!-- @segment an4.37:8.3 -->

can’t decline,

<!-- @segment an4.37:8.4 -->

and has drawn near to extinguishment.”
