title: Failure
description: The Buddha describes the five qualities fails, and doesn’t establish themselves in the tru—a mendicant who is faithless, shameless, imprudent, and witless fails, and doesn’t establish themselves in the true teaching, and a mendicant with these five qualities fails, and doesn’t establish themselves in the true teaching.
qualities: fear of wrongdoing, faith

---

<!-- @segment an5.8:1.1 -->

“Mendicants, a mendicant with five qualities fails, and doesn’t establish themselves in the true teaching.

<!-- @segment an5.8:1.2 -->

What five?

<!-- @segment an5.8:1.3 -->

A mendicant who is faithless …

<!-- @segment an5.8:1.4 -->

shameless …

<!-- @segment an5.8:1.5 -->

imprudent …

<!-- @segment an5.8:1.6 -->

lazy …

<!-- @segment an5.8:1.7 -->

witless fails, and doesn’t establish themselves in the true teaching.

<!-- @segment an5.8:1.8 -->

A mendicant with these five qualities fails, and doesn’t establish themselves in the true teaching.

<!-- @segment an5.8:2.1 -->

A mendicant with five qualities doesn’t fail, and establishes themselves in the true teaching.

<!-- @segment an5.8:2.2 -->

What five?

<!-- @segment an5.8:2.3 -->

A mendicant who is faithful …

<!-- @segment an5.8:2.4 -->

conscientious …

<!-- @segment an5.8:2.5 -->

prudent …

<!-- @segment an5.8:2.6 -->

energetic …

<!-- @segment an5.8:2.7 -->

wise doesn’t fail, and establishes themselves in the true teaching.

<!-- @segment an5.8:2.8 -->

A mendicant with these five qualities doesn’t fail, and establishes themselves in the true teaching.”
