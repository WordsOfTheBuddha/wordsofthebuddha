<!-- @segment an7.1:1.1 -->

So I have heard.

<!-- @segment an7.1:1.2 -->

At one time the Buddha was staying near Sāvatthī in Jeta’s Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s monastery.

<!-- @segment an7.1:1.3 -->

There the Buddha addressed the mendicants,

<!-- @segment an7.1:1.4 -->

“Mendicants!”

<!-- @segment an7.1:1.5 -->

“Venerable sir,” they replied.

<!-- @segment an7.1:1.6 -->

The Buddha said this:

<!-- @segment an7.1:2.1 -->

“Mendicants, a mendicant with seven qualities is disliked and disapproved by their spiritual companions, not respected or admired.

<!-- @segment an7.1:2.2 -->

What seven?

<!-- @segment an7.1:2.3 -->

It’s when a mendicant desires material things, honor, and status. They lack conscience and prudence. They have corrupt wishes and wrong view.

<!-- @segment an7.1:2.4 -->

A mendicant with these seven qualities is disliked and disapproved by their spiritual companions, not respected or admired.

<!-- @segment an7.1:3.1 -->

A mendicant with seven qualities is liked and approved by their spiritual companions, respected and admired.

<!-- @segment an7.1:3.2 -->

What seven?

<!-- @segment an7.1:3.3 -->

It’s when a mendicant doesn’t desire material things, honor, and status. They have conscience and prudence. They have few desires and right view.

<!-- @segment an7.1:3.4 -->

A mendicant with these seven qualities is liked and approved by their spiritual companions, respected and admired.”
