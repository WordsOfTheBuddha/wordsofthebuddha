<!-- @segment an8.71:1.1 -->

“Mendicants, a mendicant is faithful but not ethical.

<!-- @segment an8.71:1.2 -->

So they’re incomplete in that respect,

<!-- @segment an8.71:1.3 -->

and should fulfill it, thinking:

<!-- @segment an8.71:1.4 -->

‘How can I become faithful and ethical?’

<!-- @segment an8.71:1.5 -->

When the mendicant is faithful and ethical,

<!-- @segment an8.71:1.6 -->

they’re complete in that respect.

<!-- @segment an8.71:2.1 -->

A mendicant is faithful and ethical, but not learned.

<!-- @segment an8.71:2.2 -->

So they’re incomplete in that respect,

<!-- @segment an8.71:2.3 -->

and should fulfill it, thinking:

<!-- @segment an8.71:2.4 -->

‘How can I become faithful, ethical, and learned?’

<!-- @segment an8.71:2.5 -->

When the mendicant is faithful, ethical, and learned,

<!-- @segment an8.71:2.6 -->

they’re complete in that respect.

<!-- @segment an8.71:3.1 -->

A mendicant is faithful, ethical, and learned, but not a Dhamma speaker. …

<!-- @segment an8.71:3.2 -->

they don’t frequent assemblies …

<!-- @segment an8.71:3.3 -->

they don’t teach Dhamma to the assembly with assurance …

<!-- @segment an8.71:3.4 -->

they don’t get the four absorptions—blissful meditations in this life that belong to the higher mind—when they want, without trouble or difficulty …

<!-- @segment an8.71:3.5 -->

they don’t realize the undefiled freedom of heart and freedom by wisdom in this very life, and live having realized it with their own insight due to the ending of defilements.

<!-- @segment an8.71:3.6 -->

So they’re incomplete in that respect,

<!-- @segment an8.71:3.7 -->

and should fulfill it, thinking:

<!-- @segment an8.71:3.8 -->

‘How can I become faithful, ethical, and learned, a Dhamma speaker, one who frequents assemblies, one who teaches Dhamma to the assembly with assurance, one who gets the four absorptions when they want, and one who lives having realized the ending of defilements?’

<!-- @segment an8.71:4.1 -->

When they’re faithful, ethical, and learned, a Dhamma speaker, one who frequents assemblies, one who teaches Dhamma to the assembly with assurance, one who gets the four absorptions when they want, and one who lives having realized the ending of defilements,

<!-- @segment an8.71:4.2 -->

they’re complete in that respect.

<!-- @segment an8.71:4.3 -->

A mendicant who possesses these eight qualities is impressive all around, and is complete in every respect.”
