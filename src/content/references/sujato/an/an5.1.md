<!-- @segment an5.1:1.1 -->

So I have heard.

<!-- @segment an5.1:1.2 -->

At one time the Buddha was staying near Sāvatthī in Jeta’s Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s monastery.

<!-- @segment an5.1:1.3 -->

There the Buddha addressed the mendicants,

<!-- @segment an5.1:1.4 -->

“Mendicants!”

<!-- @segment an5.1:1.5 -->

“Venerable sir,” they replied.

<!-- @segment an5.1:1.6 -->

The Buddha said this:

<!-- @segment an5.1:2.1 -->

“Mendicants, there are these five powers of a trainee.

<!-- @segment an5.1:2.2 -->

What five?

<!-- @segment an5.1:2.3 -->

The powers of faith, conscience, prudence, energy, and wisdom.

<!-- @segment an5.1:2.4 -->

These are the five powers of a trainee.

<!-- @segment an5.1:3.1 -->

So you should train like this:

<!-- @segment an5.1:3.2 -->

‘We will have the trainee’s powers of faith, conscience, prudence, energy, and wisdom.’

<!-- @segment an5.1:3.3 -->

That’s how you should train.”

<!-- @segment an5.1:3.4 -->

That is what the Buddha said.

<!-- @segment an5.1:3.5 -->

Satisfied, the mendicants approved what the Buddha said.
