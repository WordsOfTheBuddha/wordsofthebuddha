<!-- @segment an10.120:1.1 -->

“Mendicants, I will teach you the noble descent.

<!-- @segment an10.120:1.2 -->

Listen and apply your mind well, I will speak. …

<!-- @segment an10.120:1.3 -->

And what is the noble descent?

<!-- @segment an10.120:1.4 -->

It’s when a noble disciple reflects:

<!-- @segment an10.120:1.5 -->

‘Wrong view has a bad result

<!-- @segment an10.120:1.6 -->

in both this life and the next.’

<!-- @segment an10.120:1.7 -->

Reflecting like this, they give up wrong view,

<!-- @segment an10.120:1.8 -->

they descend from wrong view.

<!-- @segment an10.120:1.9 -->

‘Wrong purpose has a bad result …’ …

<!-- @segment an10.120:1.10 -->

‘Wrong speech …’ …

<!-- @segment an10.120:1.11 -->

‘Wrong action …’ …

<!-- @segment an10.120:1.12 -->

‘Wrong livelihood …’ …

<!-- @segment an10.120:1.13 -->

‘Wrong effort …’ …

<!-- @segment an10.120:1.14 -->

‘Wrong mindfulness …’ …

<!-- @segment an10.120:1.15 -->

‘Wrong immersion …’ …

<!-- @segment an10.120:1.16 -->

‘Wrong knowledge …’ …

<!-- @segment an10.120:1.17 -->

‘Wrong freedom has a bad result

<!-- @segment an10.120:1.18 -->

in both this life and the next.’

<!-- @segment an10.120:1.19 -->

Reflecting like this, they give up wrong freedom,

<!-- @segment an10.120:1.20 -->

they descend from wrong freedom.

<!-- @segment an10.120:1.21 -->

This is called the noble descent.”
