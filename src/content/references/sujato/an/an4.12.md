<!-- @segment an4.12:1.1 -->

“Mendicants, live by the ethical precepts and the monastic code. Live restrained in the monastic code, conducting yourselves well and resorting for alms in suitable places. Seeing danger in the slightest fault, keep the rules you’ve undertaken.

<!-- @segment an4.12:1.2 -->

When you’ve done this, what more is there to do?

<!-- @segment an4.12:2.1 -->

Suppose a mendicant has got rid of desire and ill will while walking, and has given up dullness and drowsiness, restlessness and remorse, and doubt. Their energy is roused up and unflagging, their mindfulness is established and lucid, their body is tranquil and undisturbed, and their mind is immersed in samādhi. Such a mendicant is said to be ‘keen and prudent, always energetic and resolute’ when walking.

<!-- @segment an4.12:3.1 -->

Suppose a mendicant has got rid of desire and ill will while standing …

<!-- @segment an4.12:4.1 -->

sitting …

<!-- @segment an4.12:5.1 -->

and when lying down while awake, and has given up dullness and drowsiness, restlessness and remorse, and doubt. Their energy is roused up and unflagging, their mindfulness is established and lucid, their body is tranquil and undisturbed, and their mind is immersed in samādhi and unified. Such a mendicant is said to be ‘keen and prudent, always energetic and resolute’ when lying down while awake.

<!-- @segment an4.12:6.1 -->

Carefully walking, carefully standing,

<!-- @segment an4.12:6.2 -->

carefully sitting, carefully lying;

<!-- @segment an4.12:6.3 -->

a mendicant carefully bends their limbs,

<!-- @segment an4.12:6.4 -->

and carefully extends them.

<!-- @segment an4.12:7.1 -->

Above, below, all round,

<!-- @segment an4.12:7.2 -->

as far as the planet extends;

<!-- @segment an4.12:7.3 -->

they scrutinize the rise and fall

<!-- @segment an4.12:7.4 -->

of phenomena such as the aggregates.

<!-- @segment an4.12:8.1 -->

Training in what leads to serenity of heart,

<!-- @segment an4.12:8.2 -->

always staying mindful;

<!-- @segment an4.12:8.3 -->

they call such a mendicant

<!-- @segment an4.12:8.4 -->

‘always determined’.”
