<!-- @segment an9.28:1.1 -->

“Mendicants, when a noble disciple has quelled five fears and enmities, and possesses the four factors of stream-entry, they may, if they wish, declare of themselves:

<!-- @segment an9.28:1.2 -->

‘I’ve finished with rebirth in hell, the animal realm, and the ghost realm. I’ve finished with all places of loss, bad places, the underworld. I am a stream-enterer! I’m not liable to be reborn in the underworld, and am assured, destined for awakening.’

<!-- @segment an9.28:2.1 -->

What are the five fears and enmities they have quelled?

<!-- @segment an9.28:2.2 -->

Anyone who kills living creatures brims with fear and enmity both in this life and in lives to come, and experiences mental pain and sadness. Anyone who refrains from killing living creatures does not brim with fear and enmity either in this life or in lives to come, and doesn’t experience mental pain and sadness.

<!-- @segment an9.28:2.3 -->

So that fear and enmity is quelled for anyone who refrains from killing living creatures.

<!-- @segment an9.28:3.1 -->

Anyone who steals … commits sexual misconduct … lies …

<!-- @segment an9.28:3.2 -->

Anyone who consumes intoxicants of beer, wine, and liquor creates dangers and threats both in this life and in lives to come, and experiences mental pain and sadness. Anyone who refrains from intoxicants of beer, wine, and liquor creates no dangers and threats either in this life or in lives to come, and doesn’t experience mental pain and sadness.

<!-- @segment an9.28:3.3 -->

So that danger and threat is quelled for anyone who refrains from intoxicants of beer, wine, and liquor.

<!-- @segment an9.28:3.4 -->

These are the five fears and enmities they have quelled.

<!-- @segment an9.28:4.1 -->

What are the four factors of stream-entry that they possess?

<!-- @segment an9.28:4.2 -->

When a noble disciple has experiential confidence in the Buddha …

<!-- @segment an9.28:4.3 -->



<!-- @segment an9.28:4.4 -->

the teaching …

<!-- @segment an9.28:4.5 -->

the Saṅgha …

<!-- @segment an9.28:4.6 -->

And a noble disciple’s ethical conduct is loved by the noble ones, unbroken, impeccable, spotless, and unmarred, liberating, praised by sensible people, not mistaken, and leading to immersion.

<!-- @segment an9.28:4.7 -->

These are the four factors of stream-entry that they possess.

<!-- @segment an9.28:5.1 -->

When a noble disciple has quelled these five fears and enmities, and possesses these four factors of stream-entry, they may, if they wish, declare of themselves:

<!-- @segment an9.28:5.2 -->

‘I’ve finished with rebirth in hell, the animal realm, and the ghost realm. I’ve finished with all places of loss, bad places, the underworld. I am a stream-enterer! I’m not liable to be reborn in the underworld, and am assured, destined for awakening.’”
