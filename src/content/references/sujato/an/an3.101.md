<!-- @segment an3.101:1.1 -->

“Native gold has coarse corruptions: sand, soil, and gravel.

<!-- @segment an3.101:1.2 -->

A panner or their apprentice pours it into a pan, where they wash, rinse, and clean it.

<!-- @segment an3.101:1.3 -->

When that’s been eliminated, there are medium corruptions in the native gold: fine grit and coarse sand.

<!-- @segment an3.101:1.4 -->

The panner washes it again.

<!-- @segment an3.101:1.5 -->

When that’s been eliminated, there are fine corruptions in the native gold: fine sand and black grime.

<!-- @segment an3.101:1.6 -->

The panner washes it again.

<!-- @segment an3.101:1.7 -->

When that’s been eliminated, only golden flecks remain.

<!-- @segment an3.101:1.8 -->

A goldsmith or their apprentice places the native gold in a crucible where they blow, melt, and smelt it.

<!-- @segment an3.101:1.9 -->

Still the native gold is not settled and the dross is not totally gone. It’s not pliable, workable, or radiant, but is brittle and not completely ready for working.

<!-- @segment an3.101:1.10 -->

But the goldsmith keeps on blowing, melting, and smelting it.

<!-- @segment an3.101:1.11 -->

The native gold becomes pliable, workable, and radiant, not brittle, and ready to be worked.

<!-- @segment an3.101:1.12 -->

Then the goldsmith can successfully create any kind of ornament they want, whether a bracelet, earrings, a necklace, or a golden garland.

<!-- @segment an3.101:2.1 -->

In the same way, a mendicant who is committed to the higher mind has coarse corruptions: bad bodily, verbal, and mental conduct. A sincere, capable mendicant gives these up, gets rid of, eliminates, and obliterates them.

<!-- @segment an3.101:2.2 -->

When they’ve been given up and eliminated, there are middling corruptions: sensual, malicious, or cruel thoughts. A sincere, capable mendicant gives these up, gets rid of, eliminates, and obliterates them.

<!-- @segment an3.101:2.3 -->

When they’ve been given up and eliminated, there are fine corruptions: thoughts of family, country, and status. A sincere, capable mendicant gives these up, gets rid of, eliminates, and obliterates them.

<!-- @segment an3.101:2.4 -->

When they’ve been given up and eliminated, only thoughts about the teaching are left.

<!-- @segment an3.101:2.5 -->

That immersion is not peaceful or sublime or tranquil or unified, but is held in place by forceful suppression.

<!-- @segment an3.101:2.6 -->

But there comes a time when that mind is stilled internally; it settles, unifies, and becomes immersed in samādhi.

<!-- @segment an3.101:2.7 -->

That immersion is peaceful and sublime and tranquil and unified, not held in place by forceful suppression.

<!-- @segment an3.101:2.8 -->

They extend the mind to realize by insight each and every thing that can be realized by insight; and they are capable of realizing those things, since each and every one is within range.

<!-- @segment an3.101:3.1 -->

If they wish: ‘May I wield the many kinds of psychic power: multiplying myself and becoming one again; materializing and dematerializing; going unobstructed through a wall, a rampart, or a mountain as if through space; diving in and out of the earth as if it were water; walking on water as if it were earth; flying cross-legged through the sky like a bird; touching and stroking with my hand the sun and moon, so mighty and powerful; controlling my body as far as the realm of divinity.’

<!-- @segment an3.101:3.2 -->

They are capable of realizing these things, since each and every one is within range.

<!-- @segment an3.101:4.1 -->

If they wish: ‘With clairaudience that is purified and superhuman, may I hear both kinds of sounds, human and heavenly, whether near or far.’

<!-- @segment an3.101:4.2 -->

They are capable of realizing these things, since each and every one is within range.

<!-- @segment an3.101:5.1 -->

If they wish: ‘May I understand the minds of other beings and individuals, having encompassed them with my mind.

<!-- @segment an3.101:5.2 -->

May I understand mind with greed as “mind with greed”,

<!-- @segment an3.101:5.3 -->

and mind without greed as “mind without greed”;

<!-- @segment an3.101:5.4 -->

mind with hate as “mind with hate”,

<!-- @segment an3.101:5.5 -->

and mind without hate as “mind without hate”;

<!-- @segment an3.101:5.6 -->

mind with delusion as “mind with delusion”,

<!-- @segment an3.101:5.7 -->

and mind without delusion as “mind without delusion”;

<!-- @segment an3.101:5.8 -->

constricted mind as “constricted mind”,

<!-- @segment an3.101:5.9 -->

and scattered mind as “scattered mind”;

<!-- @segment an3.101:5.10 -->

expansive mind as “expansive mind”,

<!-- @segment an3.101:5.11 -->

and unexpansive mind as “unexpansive mind”;

<!-- @segment an3.101:5.12 -->

mind that is not supreme as “mind that is not supreme”,

<!-- @segment an3.101:5.13 -->

and mind that is supreme as “mind that is supreme”;

<!-- @segment an3.101:5.14 -->

mind immersed in samādhi as “mind immersed in samādhi”,

<!-- @segment an3.101:5.15 -->

and mind not immersed in samādhi as “mind not immersed in samādhi”;

<!-- @segment an3.101:5.16 -->

freed mind as “freed mind”,

<!-- @segment an3.101:5.17 -->

and unfreed mind as “unfreed mind”.’

<!-- @segment an3.101:5.18 -->

They are capable of realizing these things, since each and every one is within range.

<!-- @segment an3.101:6.1 -->

If they wish: ‘May I recollect many kinds of past lives. That is: one, two, three, four, five, ten, twenty, thirty, forty, fifty, a hundred, a thousand, a hundred thousand rebirths; many eons of the world contracting, many eons of the world expanding, many eons of the world contracting and expanding. May I remember: “There, I was named this, my clan was that, I looked like this, and that was my food. This was how I felt pleasure and pain, and that was how my life ended. When I passed away from that place I was reborn somewhere else. There, too, I was named this, my clan was that, I looked like this, and that was my food. This was how I felt pleasure and pain, and that was how my life ended. When I passed away from that place I was reborn here.” May I recollect my many past lives, with features and details.’

<!-- @segment an3.101:6.2 -->

They are capable of realizing these things, since each and every one is within range.

<!-- @segment an3.101:7.1 -->

If they wish: ‘With clairvoyance that is purified and superhuman, may I see sentient beings passing away and being reborn—inferior and superior, beautiful and ugly, in a good place or a bad place—and understand how sentient beings pass on according to their deeds: “These dear beings did bad things by way of body, speech, and mind. They denounced the noble ones; they had wrong view; and they chose to act out of that wrong view. When their body breaks up, after death, they’re reborn in a place of loss, a bad place, the underworld, hell. These dear beings, however, did good things by way of body, speech, and mind. They never denounced the noble ones; they had right view; and they chose to act out of that right view. When their body breaks up, after death, they’re reborn in a good place, a heavenly realm.” And so, with clairvoyance that is purified and superhuman, may I see sentient beings passing away and being reborn—inferior and superior, beautiful and ugly, in a good place or a bad place. And may I understand how sentient beings pass on according to their deeds.’

<!-- @segment an3.101:7.2 -->

They are capable of realizing these things, since each and every one is within range.

<!-- @segment an3.101:8.1 -->

If they wish: ‘May I realize the undefiled freedom of heart and freedom by wisdom in this very life, and live having realized it with my own insight due to the ending of defilements.’

<!-- @segment an3.101:8.2 -->

They are capable of realizing these things, since each and every one is within range.”
