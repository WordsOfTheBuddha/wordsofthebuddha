<!-- @segment an7.12:1.1 -->

“Mendicants, the spiritual life is lived to give up and cut out these seven underlying tendencies.

<!-- @segment an7.12:1.2 -->

What seven?

<!-- @segment an7.12:1.3 -->

The underlying tendencies of sensual desire, aversion, views, doubt, conceit, desire to be reborn, and ignorance.

<!-- @segment an7.12:1.4 -->



<!-- @segment an7.12:1.5 -->



<!-- @segment an7.12:1.6 -->



<!-- @segment an7.12:1.7 -->



<!-- @segment an7.12:1.8 -->



<!-- @segment an7.12:1.9 -->

The spiritual life is lived to give up and cut out these seven underlying tendencies.

<!-- @segment an7.12:2.1 -->

When a mendicant has given up the underlying tendencies of sensual desire, repulsion, views, doubt, conceit, desire to be reborn, and ignorance—cut them off at the root, made them like a palm stump, obliterated them, so they are unable to arise in the future—

<!-- @segment an7.12:2.2 -->



<!-- @segment an7.12:2.3 -->



<!-- @segment an7.12:2.4 -->



<!-- @segment an7.12:2.5 -->



<!-- @segment an7.12:2.6 -->



<!-- @segment an7.12:2.7 -->



<!-- @segment an7.12:2.8 -->

they’re called a mendicant who has cut off craving, cast off the fetters, and by rightly comprehending conceit has made an end of suffering.”
