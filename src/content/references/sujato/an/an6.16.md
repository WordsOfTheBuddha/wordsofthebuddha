title: Nakula’s Father
---

<!-- @segment an6.16:1.1 -->

At one time the Buddha was staying in the land of the Bhaggas at Crocodile’s Bellow, in the deer park at Bhesakaḷā’s Wood.

<!-- @segment an6.16:1.2 -->

Now at that time the householder Nakula’s father was sick, suffering, gravely ill.

<!-- @segment an6.16:1.3 -->

Then the housewife Nakula’s mother said to him:

<!-- @segment an6.16:2.1 -->

“Householder, don’t pass away with concerns.

<!-- @segment an6.16:2.2 -->

Such concern is suffering,

<!-- @segment an6.16:2.3 -->

and it’s criticized by the Buddha.

<!-- @segment an6.16:2.4 -->

Householder, you might think:

<!-- @segment an6.16:2.5 -->

‘When I’ve gone, the housewife Nakula’s mother won’t be able to provide for the children and keep up the household carpets.’

<!-- @segment an6.16:2.6 -->

But you should not see it like this.

<!-- @segment an6.16:2.7 -->

I’m skilled at spinning cotton and carding wool.

<!-- @segment an6.16:2.8 -->

I’m able to provide for the children and keep up the household carpets.

<!-- @segment an6.16:2.9 -->

So householder, don’t pass away with concerns …

<!-- @segment an6.16:2.10 -->



<!-- @segment an6.16:2.11 -->



<!-- @segment an6.16:3.1 -->

Householder, you might think:

<!-- @segment an6.16:3.2 -->

‘When I’ve gone, the housewife Nakula’s mother will take another husband.’

<!-- @segment an6.16:3.3 -->

But you should not see it like this.

<!-- @segment an6.16:3.4 -->

Both you and I know that we have remained chaste while at home for the past sixteen years.

<!-- @segment an6.16:3.5 -->

So householder, don’t pass away with concerns …

<!-- @segment an6.16:3.6 -->



<!-- @segment an6.16:3.7 -->



<!-- @segment an6.16:4.1 -->

Householder, you might think:

<!-- @segment an6.16:4.2 -->

‘When I’ve gone, the housewife Nakula’s mother won’t want to see the Buddha and his Saṅgha of mendicants.’

<!-- @segment an6.16:4.3 -->

But you should not see it like this.

<!-- @segment an6.16:4.4 -->

When you’ve gone, I’ll want to see the Buddha and his mendicant Saṅgha even more.

<!-- @segment an6.16:4.5 -->

So householder, don’t pass away with concerns …

<!-- @segment an6.16:4.6 -->



<!-- @segment an6.16:4.7 -->



<!-- @segment an6.16:5.1 -->

Householder, you might think:

<!-- @segment an6.16:5.2 -->

‘The housewife Nakula’s mother won’t fulfill ethics.’

<!-- @segment an6.16:5.3 -->

But you should not see it like this.

<!-- @segment an6.16:5.4 -->

I am one of those white-robed disciples of the Buddha who fulfills their ethics.

<!-- @segment an6.16:5.5 -->

For whoever doubts this, that Blessed One, the perfected one, the fully awakened Buddha is staying in the land of the Bhaggas at Crocodile’s Bellow, in the deer park at Bhesakaḷā’s Wood. Let them approach him and ask.

<!-- @segment an6.16:5.6 -->

So householder, don’t pass away with concerns …

<!-- @segment an6.16:5.7 -->



<!-- @segment an6.16:5.8 -->



<!-- @segment an6.16:6.1 -->

Householder, you might think:

<!-- @segment an6.16:6.2 -->

‘The housewife Nakula’s mother doesn’t have internal serenity of heart.’

<!-- @segment an6.16:6.3 -->

But you should not see it like this.

<!-- @segment an6.16:6.4 -->

I am one of those white-robed disciples of the Buddha who has internal serenity of heart.

<!-- @segment an6.16:6.5 -->

For whoever doubts this, that Blessed One is staying in the land of the Bhaggas at Crocodile’s Bellow, in the deer park at Bhesakaḷā’s Wood. Let them approach him and ask.

<!-- @segment an6.16:6.6 -->

So householder, don’t pass away with concerns …

<!-- @segment an6.16:6.7 -->



<!-- @segment an6.16:6.8 -->



<!-- @segment an6.16:7.1 -->

Householder, you might think:

<!-- @segment an6.16:7.2 -->

‘The housewife Nakula’s mother has not found a footing, a firm footing, and solace in this teaching and training. She has not gone beyond doubt, got rid of indecision, and gained assurance. And she’s not independent of others in the Teacher’s instructions.’

<!-- @segment an6.16:7.3 -->

But you should not see it like this.

<!-- @segment an6.16:7.4 -->

I am one of those white-robed disciples of the Buddha who has found a footing, a firm footing, and solace in this teaching and training. I have gone beyond doubt, got rid of indecision, and gained assurance. And I am independent of others in the Teacher’s instructions.

<!-- @segment an6.16:7.5 -->

For whoever doubts this, that Blessed One is staying in the land of the Bhaggas at Crocodile’s Bellow, in the deer park at Bhesakaḷā’s Wood. Let them approach him and ask.

<!-- @segment an6.16:7.6 -->

So householder, don’t pass away with concerns.

<!-- @segment an6.16:7.7 -->

Such concern is suffering,

<!-- @segment an6.16:7.8 -->

and it’s criticized by the Buddha.”

<!-- @segment an6.16:8.1 -->

And then, as Nakula’s mother was giving this advice to Nakula’s father, his illness died down on the spot.

<!-- @segment an6.16:8.2 -->

And that’s how Nakula’s father recovered from that illness.

<!-- @segment an6.16:8.3 -->



<!-- @segment an6.16:8.4 -->

Soon after recovering, leaning on a staff he went to the Buddha, bowed, and sat down to one side. The Buddha said to him:

<!-- @segment an6.16:9.1 -->

“You’re fortunate, householder, so very fortunate,

<!-- @segment an6.16:9.2 -->

to have the housewife Nakula’s mother advise and instruct you out of kindness and sympathy.

<!-- @segment an6.16:9.3 -->

She is one of those white-robed disciples of the Buddha who fulfills their ethics.

<!-- @segment an6.16:9.4 -->

She is one of those white-robed disciples of the Buddha who has internal serenity of heart.

<!-- @segment an6.16:9.5 -->

She is one of those white-robed disciples of the Buddha who has found a footing, a firm footing, and solace in this teaching and training. She has gone beyond doubt, got rid of indecision, and gained assurance. And she is independent of others in the Teacher’s instructions.

<!-- @segment an6.16:9.6 -->

You’re fortunate, householder, so very fortunate,

<!-- @segment an6.16:9.7 -->

to have the housewife Nakula’s mother advise and instruct you out of kindness and sympathy.”
