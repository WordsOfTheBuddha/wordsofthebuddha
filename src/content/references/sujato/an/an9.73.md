title: Weaknesses in Training and Effort

<!-- @segment an9.73:1.1 -->

“Mendicants, there are these five weaknesses when you’re training.

<!-- @segment an9.73:1.2 -->

What five?

<!-- @segment an9.73:1.3 -->

Killing living creatures, stealing, sexual misconduct, lying, and intoxicants of beer, wine, and liquor.

<!-- @segment an9.73:1.4 -->

These are the five weaknesses when you’re training.

<!-- @segment an9.73:2.1 -->

To give up these five weaknesses in your training you should develop the four right efforts.

<!-- @segment an9.73:2.2 -->

What four?

<!-- @segment an9.73:2.3 -->

It’s when a mendicant generates enthusiasm, tries, makes an effort, exerts the mind, and strives so that bad, unskillful qualities don’t arise.

<!-- @segment an9.73:2.4 -->

They generate enthusiasm, try, make an effort, exert the mind, and strive so that bad, unskillful qualities that have arisen are given up.

<!-- @segment an9.73:2.5 -->

They generate enthusiasm, try, make an effort, exert the mind, and strive so that skillful qualities arise.

<!-- @segment an9.73:2.6 -->

They generate enthusiasm, try, make an effort, exert the mind, and strive so that skillful qualities that have arisen remain, are not lost, but increase, mature, and are completed by development.

<!-- @segment an9.73:2.7 -->

To give up these five weaknesses in your training you should develop these four right efforts.”
