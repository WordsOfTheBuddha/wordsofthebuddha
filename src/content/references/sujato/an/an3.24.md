<!-- @segment an3.24:1.1 -->

“Mendicants, these three individuals are very helpful to another.

<!-- @segment an3.24:1.2 -->

What three?

<!-- @segment an3.24:1.3 -->

The individual who has enabled you to go for refuge to the Buddha, the teaching, and the Saṅgha.

<!-- @segment an3.24:1.4 -->

This individual is very helpful to another.

<!-- @segment an3.24:2.1 -->

Furthermore, the individual who has enabled you to truly understand: ‘This is suffering’ … ‘This is the origin of suffering’ … ‘This is the cessation of suffering’ … ‘This is the practice that leads to the cessation of suffering’.

<!-- @segment an3.24:2.2 -->

This individual is very helpful to another.

<!-- @segment an3.24:3.1 -->

Furthermore, the individual who has enabled you to realize the undefiled freedom of heart and freedom by wisdom in this very life, and live having realized it with your own insight due to the ending of defilements.

<!-- @segment an3.24:3.2 -->

This individual is very helpful to another.

<!-- @segment an3.24:3.3 -->

These are the three individuals who are very helpful to another individual.

<!-- @segment an3.24:4.1 -->

And I say that there is no-one more helpful to another than these three individuals.

<!-- @segment an3.24:4.2 -->

And I also say that it’s not easy to repay these three individuals by bowing down to them, rising up for them, greeting them with cupped palms, and observing proper etiquette for them; or by providing them with robes, almsfood, lodgings, and medicines and supplies for the sick.”
