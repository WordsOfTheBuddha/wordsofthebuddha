<!-- @segment an7.37:1.1 -->

“Mendicants, when a friend has seven qualities you should associate with, accompany, and attend them, even if they drive you away.

<!-- @segment an7.37:1.2 -->

What seven?

<!-- @segment an7.37:1.3 -->

They’re likable, agreeable, respected, and admired. They admonish you and they accept admonishment. They speak on deep matters. And they don’t urge you to do bad things.

<!-- @segment an7.37:1.4 -->

When a friend has these seven qualities you should associate with, accompany, and attend them, even if they drive you away.

<!-- @segment an7.37:2.1 -->

They’re liked, respected, and admired,

<!-- @segment an7.37:2.2 -->

an admonisher who accepts admonishment,

<!-- @segment an7.37:2.3 -->

speaks on deep matters,

<!-- @segment an7.37:2.4 -->

and doesn’t urge you to do bad.

<!-- @segment an7.37:3.1 -->

The individual in whom

<!-- @segment an7.37:3.2 -->

these things are found is your friend.

<!-- @segment an7.37:3.3 -->

If you want to have a friend,

<!-- @segment an7.37:3.4 -->

benevolent and sympathetic,

<!-- @segment an7.37:3.5 -->

then even if they chase you away,

<!-- @segment an7.37:3.6 -->

you should keep company with that sort.”
