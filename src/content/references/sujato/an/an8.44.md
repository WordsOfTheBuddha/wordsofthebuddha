<!-- @segment an8.44:1.1 -->

At one time the Buddha was staying near Vesālī, at the Great Wood, in the hall with the peaked roof.

<!-- @segment an8.44:1.2 -->

Then the layman Vāseṭṭha went up to the Buddha, bowed, and sat down to one side. The Buddha said to him:

<!-- @segment an8.44:1.3 -->

“Vāseṭṭha, the observance of the sabbath with its eight factors is very fruitful and beneficial and brilliant and bountiful …

<!-- @segment an8.44:1.4 -->

blameless, they go to a heavenly place.”

<!-- @segment an8.44:2.1 -->

When he said this, Vāseṭṭha said to the Buddha:

<!-- @segment an8.44:2.2 -->

“If my loved ones—relatives and kin—were to observe this sabbath with its eight factors, it would be for their lasting welfare and happiness.

<!-- @segment an8.44:2.3 -->

If all the aristocrats, brahmins, peasants, and menials were to observe this sabbath with its eight factors, it would be for their lasting welfare and happiness.”

<!-- @segment an8.44:3.1 -->

“That’s so true, Vāseṭṭha! That’s so true, Vāseṭṭha!

<!-- @segment an8.44:3.2 -->

If all the aristocrats, brahmins, peasants, and menials were to observe this sabbath with its eight factors, it would be for their lasting welfare and happiness.

<!-- @segment an8.44:3.3 -->

If the whole world—with its gods, Māras, and divinities, this population with its ascetics and brahmins, gods and humans—were to observe this sabbath with its eight factors, it would be for their lasting welfare and happiness.

<!-- @segment an8.44:3.4 -->

If these great sal trees were to observe this sabbath with its eight factors, it would be for their lasting welfare and happiness—if they were sentient.

<!-- @segment an8.44:3.5 -->

How much more then a human being!”
