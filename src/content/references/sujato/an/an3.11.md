<!-- @segment an3.11:1.1 -->

“Mendicants, a well-known mendicant who has three qualities is acting for the detriment and suffering of the people, against the people, for the harm, detriment, and suffering of gods and humans.

<!-- @segment an3.11:1.2 -->

What three?

<!-- @segment an3.11:1.3 -->

They encourage deeds of body and speech, as well as principles, that aren’t in line with good qualities.

<!-- @segment an3.11:1.4 -->

A well-known mendicant who possesses these three qualities is acting for the detriment and suffering of the people, against the people, for the harm, detriment, and suffering of gods and humans.

<!-- @segment an3.11:2.1 -->

A well-known mendicant who has three qualities is acting for the welfare and happiness of the people, for the people, for the benefit, welfare, and happiness of gods and humans.

<!-- @segment an3.11:2.2 -->

What three?

<!-- @segment an3.11:2.3 -->

They encourage deeds of body and speech, as well as principles, that are in line with good qualities.

<!-- @segment an3.11:2.4 -->

A well-known mendicant who possesses these three qualities is acting for the welfare and happiness of the people, for the people, for the benefit, welfare, and happiness of gods and humans.”
