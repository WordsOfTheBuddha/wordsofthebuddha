<!-- @segment an7.43:1.1 -->

So I have heard.

<!-- @segment an7.43:1.2 -->

At one time the Buddha was staying near Kosambī, in Ghosita’s Monastery.

<!-- @segment an7.43:1.3 -->

Then Venerable Ānanda robed up in the morning and, taking his bowl and robe, entered Kosambī for alms.

<!-- @segment an7.43:1.4 -->

Then it occurred to him,

<!-- @segment an7.43:1.5 -->

“It’s too early to wander for alms in Kosambī.

<!-- @segment an7.43:1.6 -->

Why don’t I visit the monastery of the wanderers of other religions?”

<!-- @segment an7.43:1.7 -->

Then he went to the monastery of the wanderers of other religions and exchanged greetings with the wanderers there.

<!-- @segment an7.43:1.8 -->

When the greetings and polite conversation were over, he sat down to one side.

<!-- @segment an7.43:2.1 -->

Now at that time while those wanderers of other religions were sitting together this discussion came up among them,

<!-- @segment an7.43:2.2 -->

“Reverends, anyone who lives the full and pure spiritual life for twelve years is qualified to be called a ‘graduate mendicant’.”

<!-- @segment an7.43:3.1 -->

Ānanda neither approved nor rejected that statement of the wanderers of other religions.

<!-- @segment an7.43:3.2 -->

He rose from his seat, thinking,

<!-- @segment an7.43:3.3 -->

“I will learn the meaning of this statement from the Buddha himself.”

<!-- @segment an7.43:3.4 -->

Then Ānanda wandered for alms in Kosambī. After the meal, on his return from almsround, he went to the Buddha, bowed, sat down to one side, and told him what had happened, adding:

<!-- @segment an7.43:5.6 -->

“Sir, in this teaching and training can we describe a mendicant as a ‘graduate’ solely because they have completed a certain number of years?”

<!-- @segment an7.43:6.1 -->

“No, Ānanda, we cannot.

<!-- @segment an7.43:6.2 -->

These are the seven qualifications for graduation that I make known after realizing them with my own insight.

<!-- @segment an7.43:7.1 -->

What seven?

<!-- @segment an7.43:7.2 -->

It’s when someone is faithful, conscientious, prudent, learned, energetic, mindful, and wise.

<!-- @segment an7.43:7.3 -->

These are the seven qualifications for graduation that I make known after realizing them with my own insight.

<!-- @segment an7.43:7.4 -->

A mendicant who possesses these seven qualifications for graduation is qualified to be called a ‘graduate mendicant’. This is so whether they have lived the full and pure spiritual life for twelve years,

<!-- @segment an7.43:7.5 -->

twenty-four years,

<!-- @segment an7.43:7.6 -->

thirty-six years, or forty-eight years.”
