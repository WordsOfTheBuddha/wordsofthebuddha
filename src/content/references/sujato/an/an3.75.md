<!-- @segment an3.75:1.1 -->

Then Venerable Ānanda went up to the Buddha, bowed, and sat down to one side. The Buddha said to him:

<!-- @segment an3.75:2.1 -->

“Ānanda, those who you have sympathy for, and those worth listening to—friends and colleagues, relatives and family—should be encouraged, supported, and established in three things.

<!-- @segment an3.75:2.2 -->

What three?

<!-- @segment an3.75:2.3 -->

Experiential confidence in the Buddha:

<!-- @segment an3.75:2.4 -->

‘That Blessed One is perfected, a fully awakened Buddha, accomplished in knowledge and conduct, holy, knower of the world, supreme guide for those fit for training, teacher of gods and humans, awakened, blessed.’

<!-- @segment an3.75:2.5 -->

Experiential confidence in the teaching:

<!-- @segment an3.75:2.6 -->

‘The teaching is well explained by the Buddha—apparent in the present life, immediately effective, inviting inspection, relevant, so that sensible people can know it for themselves.’

<!-- @segment an3.75:2.7 -->

Experiential confidence in the Saṅgha:

<!-- @segment an3.75:2.8 -->

‘The Saṅgha of the Buddha’s disciples is practicing the way that’s good, direct, systematic, and proper. It consists of the four pairs, the eight individual persons. This is the Saṅgha of the Buddha’s disciples that is worthy of offerings dedicated to the gods, worthy of hospitality, worthy of a religious donation, worthy of greeting with cupped palms, and is the supreme field of merit for the world.’

<!-- @segment an3.75:3.1 -->

There might be change in the four principal states—

<!-- @segment an3.75:3.2 -->

earth, water, air, and fire—but a noble disciple with experiential confidence in the Buddha would never change.

<!-- @segment an3.75:3.3 -->

In this context, ‘change’ means that such a noble disciple will be reborn in hell, the animal realm, or the ghost realm: this is quite impossible.

<!-- @segment an3.75:4.1 -->

There might be change in the four principal states—

<!-- @segment an3.75:4.2 -->

earth, water, air, and fire—but a noble disciple with experiential confidence in the teaching …

<!-- @segment an3.75:4.3 -->

or the Saṅgha would never change.

<!-- @segment an3.75:4.4 -->

In this context, ‘change’ means that such a noble disciple will be reborn in hell, the animal realm, or the ghost realm: this is quite impossible.

<!-- @segment an3.75:5.1 -->

Those who you have sympathy for, and those worth listening to—friends and colleagues, relatives and family—should be encouraged, supported, and established by you in these three things.”
