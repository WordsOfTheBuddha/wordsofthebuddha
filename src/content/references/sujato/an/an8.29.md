<!-- @segment an8.29:1.1 -->

“‘Now is the time! Now is the time!’ So says an unlearned ordinary person. But they don’t know whether it’s time or not.

<!-- @segment an8.29:1.2 -->

Mendicants, there are eight lost opportunities for spiritual practice.

<!-- @segment an8.29:1.3 -->

What eight?

<!-- @segment an8.29:1.4 -->

Firstly, a Realized One has arisen in the world—perfected, a fully awakened Buddha, accomplished in knowledge and conduct, holy, knower of the world, supreme guide for those fit for training, teacher of gods and humans, awakened, blessed. He teaches the Dhamma leading to peace, extinguishment, awakening, as proclaimed by the Holy One.

<!-- @segment an8.29:1.5 -->

But an individual has been reborn in hell.

<!-- @segment an8.29:1.6 -->

This is the first lost opportunity for spiritual practice.

<!-- @segment an8.29:2.1 -->

Furthermore, a Realized One has arisen in the world.

<!-- @segment an8.29:2.2 -->

But an individual has been reborn in the animal realm. This is the second lost opportunity.

<!-- @segment an8.29:3.1 -->

Furthermore, a Realized One has arisen in the world.

<!-- @segment an8.29:3.2 -->

But an individual has been reborn in the ghost realm. This is the third lost opportunity.

<!-- @segment an8.29:4.1 -->

Furthermore, a Realized One has arisen in the world.

<!-- @segment an8.29:4.2 -->

But an individual has been reborn in one of the long-lived orders of gods. This is the fourth lost opportunity.

<!-- @segment an8.29:5.1 -->

Furthermore, a Realized One has arisen in the world.

<!-- @segment an8.29:5.2 -->

But an individual has been reborn in the borderlands, among uneducated foreigners, where monks, nuns, laymen, and laywomen do not go. This is the fifth lost opportunity …

<!-- @segment an8.29:6.1 -->

Furthermore, a Realized One has arisen in the world. And an individual is reborn in a central country. But they have wrong view and distorted perspective:

<!-- @segment an8.29:6.2 -->

‘There’s no meaning in giving, sacrifice, or offerings. There’s no fruit or result of good and bad deeds. There’s no afterlife. There’s no such thing as mother and father, or beings that are reborn spontaneously. And there’s no ascetic or brahmin who is rightly comported and rightly practiced, and who describes the afterlife after realizing it with their own insight.’ This is the sixth lost opportunity …

<!-- @segment an8.29:7.1 -->

Furthermore, a Realized One has arisen in the world.

<!-- @segment an8.29:7.2 -->

And an individual is reborn in a central country. But they’re witless, dull, idiotic, and unable to distinguish what is well said from what is poorly said.

<!-- @segment an8.29:7.3 -->

This is the seventh lost opportunity …

<!-- @segment an8.29:8.1 -->

Furthermore, a Realized One has not arisen in the world …

<!-- @segment an8.29:8.2 -->

So he doesn’t teach the Dhamma leading to peace, extinguishment, awakening, as proclaimed by the Holy One. And an individual is reborn in a central country. And they’re wise, bright, clever, and able to distinguish what is well said from what is poorly said.

<!-- @segment an8.29:8.3 -->

This is the eighth lost opportunity …

<!-- @segment an8.29:9.1 -->

There are these eight lost opportunities for spiritual practice.

<!-- @segment an8.29:10.1 -->

Mendicants, there is just one opportunity for spiritual practice.

<!-- @segment an8.29:10.2 -->

What is that one?

<!-- @segment an8.29:10.3 -->

It’s when a Realized One has arisen in the world, perfected, a fully awakened Buddha, accomplished in knowledge and conduct, holy, knower of the world, supreme guide for those fit for training, teacher of gods and humans, awakened, blessed.

<!-- @segment an8.29:10.4 -->

He teaches the Dhamma leading to peace, extinguishment, awakening, as proclaimed by the Holy One. And an individual is reborn in a central country. And they’re wise, bright, clever, and able to distinguish what is well said from what is poorly said.

<!-- @segment an8.29:10.5 -->

This is the one opportunity for spiritual practice.

<!-- @segment an8.29:11.1 -->

When you’ve gained the human state,

<!-- @segment an8.29:11.2 -->

and the true teaching has been so well proclaimed,

<!-- @segment an8.29:11.3 -->

those who don’t seize the moment

<!-- @segment an8.29:11.4 -->

are wasting their time.

<!-- @segment an8.29:12.1 -->

For many wrong times are spoken of,

<!-- @segment an8.29:12.2 -->

which obstruct the path.

<!-- @segment an8.29:12.3 -->

Only on rare occasions

<!-- @segment an8.29:12.4 -->

do Realized Ones arise.

<!-- @segment an8.29:13.1 -->

If you find yourself in their presence,

<!-- @segment an8.29:13.2 -->

so hard to find in the world,

<!-- @segment an8.29:13.3 -->

and if you’ve gained a human birth,

<!-- @segment an8.29:13.4 -->

and the teaching of the Dhamma;

<!-- @segment an8.29:13.5 -->

that’s enough to make an effort,

<!-- @segment an8.29:13.6 -->

for a personage who desires self-knowledge.

<!-- @segment an8.29:14.1 -->

How is the true teaching to be understood

<!-- @segment an8.29:14.2 -->

so that the moment doesn’t pass you by?

<!-- @segment an8.29:14.3 -->

For if you miss your moment

<!-- @segment an8.29:14.4 -->

you’ll grieve when sent to hell.

<!-- @segment an8.29:15.1 -->

If you fail to achieve

<!-- @segment an8.29:15.2 -->

certainty regarding the true teaching

<!-- @segment an8.29:15.3 -->

you’ll regret it for a long time,

<!-- @segment an8.29:15.4 -->

like the merchant in the story of the past.

<!-- @segment an8.29:16.1 -->

A man shrouded by ignorance,

<!-- @segment an8.29:16.2 -->

a failure in the true teaching,

<!-- @segment an8.29:16.3 -->

will long undergo

<!-- @segment an8.29:16.4 -->

transmigration through birth and death.

<!-- @segment an8.29:17.1 -->

Those who’ve gained the human state

<!-- @segment an8.29:17.2 -->

when the true teaching has been so well proclaimed,

<!-- @segment an8.29:17.3 -->

and have completed what the Teacher taught—

<!-- @segment an8.29:17.4 -->

or will do so, or are doing so now—

<!-- @segment an8.29:18.1 -->

have realized the right time in the world

<!-- @segment an8.29:18.2 -->

for the supreme spiritual life.

<!-- @segment an8.29:18.3 -->

You should live guarded, ever mindful,

<!-- @segment an8.29:18.4 -->

not festering with defilements,

<!-- @segment an8.29:19.1 -->

among those restrained ones

<!-- @segment an8.29:19.2 -->

who have practiced the path

<!-- @segment an8.29:19.3 -->

proclaimed by the Realized One, the Clear-eyed One,

<!-- @segment an8.29:19.4 -->

and taught by the Kinsman of the Sun.

<!-- @segment an8.29:20.1 -->

Having cut off all underlying tendencies

<!-- @segment an8.29:20.2 -->

that follow those drifting in Māra’s dominion,

<!-- @segment an8.29:20.3 -->

they’re the ones in this world <j>who’ve truly crossed over,

<!-- @segment an8.29:20.4 -->

having reached the ending of defilements.”
