<!-- @segment an6.94:1.1 -->

“Mendicants, these six things can’t be done.

<!-- @segment an6.94:1.2 -->

What six?

<!-- @segment an6.94:1.3 -->

An individual accomplished in view can’t murder their mother or father or a perfected one. They can’t maliciously shed the blood of the Realized One. They can’t cause a schism in the Saṅgha. They can’t dedicate themselves to another teacher.

<!-- @segment an6.94:1.4 -->

These are the six things that can’t be done.”
