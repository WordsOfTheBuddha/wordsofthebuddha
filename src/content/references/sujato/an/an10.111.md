<!-- @segment an10.111:1.1 -->

Then a mendicant went up to the Buddha, bowed, sat down to one side, and said to him:

<!-- @segment an10.111:2.1 -->

“Sir, they speak of the one called ‘an adept’.

<!-- @segment an10.111:2.2 -->

How is an adept mendicant defined?”

<!-- @segment an10.111:2.3 -->

“Mendicant, it’s when a mendicant has an adept’s right view, right purpose, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, right immersion, right knowledge, and right freedom.

<!-- @segment an10.111:2.4 -->

That’s how a mendicant is an adept.”
