title: The Chapter with a Basis
description: The Buddha teaches that bad unskillful qualities arise only with a basis—whether as cause, condition, or the aggregates of form, feeling, perception, and consciousness—and cease when that basis is given up.
qualities: unwholesome, giving up, wisdom, harm
theme: principle, wisdom

<!-- @segment an2.77:1.0 -->

77

<!-- @segment an2.77:1.1 -->

“Bad, unskillful qualities, mendicants, arise with a basis, not without a basis.

<!-- @segment an2.77:1.2 -->

By giving up that basis, those bad, unskillful qualities do not occur.”
