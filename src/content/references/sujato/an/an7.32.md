<!-- @segment an7.32:1.1 -->

Then, late at night, a glorious deity, lighting up the entire Jeta’s Grove, went up to the Buddha, bowed, stood to one side, and said to him:

<!-- @segment an7.32:2.1 -->

“Sir, these seven things don’t lead to the decline of a mendicant.

<!-- @segment an7.32:2.2 -->

What seven?

<!-- @segment an7.32:2.3 -->

Respect for the Teacher, for the teaching, for the Saṅgha, for the training, for immersion, for diligence, and for hospitality.

<!-- @segment an7.32:2.4 -->

These seven things don’t lead to the decline of a mendicant.”

<!-- @segment an7.32:2.5 -->

That’s what that deity said,

<!-- @segment an7.32:2.6 -->

and the teacher approved.

<!-- @segment an7.32:2.7 -->

Then that deity, knowing that the teacher approved, bowed and respectfully circled the Buddha, keeping him on his right, before vanishing right there.

<!-- @segment an7.32:3.1 -->

Then, when the night had passed, the Buddha told the mendicants all that had happened, adding:

<!-- @segment an7.32:3.2 -->



<!-- @segment an7.32:3.3 -->



<!-- @segment an7.32:3.4 -->



<!-- @segment an7.32:3.5 -->



<!-- @segment an7.32:3.6 -->



<!-- @segment an7.32:3.7 -->



<!-- @segment an7.32:3.8 -->



<!-- @segment an7.32:4.1 -->

“Respect for the Teacher and the teaching,

<!-- @segment an7.32:4.2 -->

and keen respect for the Saṅgha;

<!-- @segment an7.32:4.3 -->

respect for immersion, being energetic,

<!-- @segment an7.32:4.4 -->

and keen respect for the training.

<!-- @segment an7.32:5.1 -->

A mendicant who respects diligence

<!-- @segment an7.32:5.2 -->

and hospitality

<!-- @segment an7.32:5.3 -->

can’t decline,

<!-- @segment an7.32:5.4 -->

and has drawn near to extinguishment.”
