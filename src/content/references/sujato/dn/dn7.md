<!-- @segment dn7:1.1 -->

So I have heard.

<!-- @segment dn7:1.2 -->

At one time the Buddha was staying near Kosambī, in Ghosita’s Monastery.

<!-- @segment dn7:1.3 -->

Now at that time two renunciates—

<!-- @segment dn7:1.4 -->

the wanderer Muṇḍiya and Jāliya, the pupil of the wood-bowl ascetic—came to the Buddha and exchanged greetings with him.

<!-- @segment dn7:1.5 -->

When the greetings and polite conversation were over, they stood to one side and said to the Buddha,

<!-- @segment dn7:1.6 -->

“Reverend Gotama, are the soul and the body one and the same, or is the soul one thing, the body another?”

<!-- @segment dn7:1.7 -->

“Well then, reverends, listen and apply your mind well, I will speak.”

<!-- @segment dn7:1.8 -->

“Yes, reverend,” they replied.

<!-- @segment dn7:1.9 -->

The Buddha said this:

<!-- @segment dn7:2.1 -->

“Take the case when a Realized One arises in the world, perfected, a fully awakened Buddha …

<!-- @segment dn7:2.2 -->

That’s how a mendicant is accomplished in ethics. …

<!-- @segment dn7:2.3 -->



<!-- @segment dn7:2.4 -->

They enter and remain in the first absorption …

<!-- @segment dn7:2.5 -->

When a mendicant knows and sees like this, would it be appropriate to say of them:

<!-- @segment dn7:2.6 -->

‘The soul and the body are one and the same’ or ‘The soul is one thing, the body another’?”

<!-- @segment dn7:2.7 -->

“It would, reverend.”

<!-- @segment dn7:2.8 -->



<!-- @segment dn7:2.9 -->

“But reverends, I know and see like this.

<!-- @segment dn7:2.10 -->

Nevertheless, I do not say:

<!-- @segment dn7:2.11 -->

‘The soul and the body are one and the same’ or ‘The soul is one thing, the body another’. …

<!-- @segment dn7:3.1 -->

They enter and remain in the second absorption …

<!-- @segment dn7:3.2 -->

third absorption …

<!-- @segment dn7:3.3 -->

fourth absorption.

<!-- @segment dn7:3.4 -->

When a mendicant knows and sees like this, would it be appropriate to say of them:

<!-- @segment dn7:3.5 -->

‘The soul and the body are one and the same’ or ‘The soul is one thing, the body another’?”

<!-- @segment dn7:3.6 -->

“It would, reverend.”

<!-- @segment dn7:3.7 -->



<!-- @segment dn7:3.8 -->

“But reverends, I know and see like this.

<!-- @segment dn7:3.9 -->

Nevertheless, I do not say:

<!-- @segment dn7:3.10 -->

‘The soul and the body are one and the same’ or ‘The soul is one thing, the body another’. …

<!-- @segment dn7:4.1 -->

They project and extend the mind toward knowledge and vision …

<!-- @segment dn7:4.2 -->

When a mendicant knows and sees like this, would it be appropriate to say of them:

<!-- @segment dn7:4.3 -->

‘The soul and the body are one and the same’ or ‘The soul is one thing, the body another’?”

<!-- @segment dn7:4.4 -->

“It would, reverend.”

<!-- @segment dn7:4.5 -->



<!-- @segment dn7:4.6 -->

“But reverends, I know and see like this.

<!-- @segment dn7:4.7 -->

Nevertheless, I do not say:

<!-- @segment dn7:4.8 -->

‘The soul and the body are one and the same’ or ‘The soul is one thing, the body another’. …

<!-- @segment dn7:5.1 -->

They understand: ‘… there is nothing further for this place.’

<!-- @segment dn7:5.2 -->

When a mendicant knows and sees like this, would it be appropriate to say of them:

<!-- @segment dn7:5.3 -->

‘The soul and the body are one and the same’ or ‘The soul is one thing, the body another’?”

<!-- @segment dn7:5.4 -->

“It would not, reverend.”

<!-- @segment dn7:5.5 -->



<!-- @segment dn7:5.6 -->

“But reverends, I know and see like this.

<!-- @segment dn7:5.7 -->

Nevertheless, I do not say:

<!-- @segment dn7:5.8 -->

‘The soul and the body are one and the same’ or ‘The soul is one thing, the body another’.”

<!-- @segment dn7:5.9 -->

That is what the Buddha said.

<!-- @segment dn7:5.10 -->

Satisfied, the two renunciates approved what the Buddha said.
