<!-- @segment mn72:1.1 -->

So I have heard.

<!-- @segment mn72:1.2 -->

At one time the Buddha was staying near Sāvatthī in Jeta’s Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s monastery.

<!-- @segment mn72:2.1 -->

Then the wanderer Vacchagotta went up to the Buddha and exchanged greetings with him.

<!-- @segment mn72:2.2 -->

When the greetings and polite conversation were over, he sat down to one side and said to the Buddha:

<!-- @segment mn72:3.1 -->

“Worthy Gotama, is this your view: ‘The cosmos is eternal. This is the only truth, anything else is futile’?”

<!-- @segment mn72:3.2 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:3.3 -->

“That’s not my view, Vaccha.”

<!-- @segment mn72:3.4 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:4.1 -->

“Then is this your view: ‘The cosmos is not eternal. This is the only truth, anything else is futile’?”

<!-- @segment mn72:4.2 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:4.3 -->

“That’s not my view, Vaccha.”

<!-- @segment mn72:4.4 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:5.1 -->

“Then is this your view: ‘The cosmos is finite. This is the only truth, anything else is futile’?”

<!-- @segment mn72:5.2 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:5.3 -->

“That’s not my view, Vaccha.”

<!-- @segment mn72:5.4 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:6.1 -->

“Then is this your view: ‘The cosmos is infinite. This is the only truth, anything else is futile’?”

<!-- @segment mn72:6.2 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:6.3 -->

“That’s not my view, Vaccha.”

<!-- @segment mn72:6.4 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:7.1 -->

“Then is this your view: ‘The soul and the body are one and the same. This is the only truth, anything else is futile’?”

<!-- @segment mn72:7.2 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:7.3 -->

“That’s not my view, Vaccha.”

<!-- @segment mn72:7.4 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:8.1 -->

“Then is this your view: ‘The soul is one thing, the body another. This is the only truth, anything else is futile’?”

<!-- @segment mn72:8.2 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:8.3 -->

“That’s not my view, Vaccha.”

<!-- @segment mn72:8.4 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:9.1 -->

“Then is this your view: ‘A realized one still exists after death. This is the only truth, anything else is futile’?”

<!-- @segment mn72:9.2 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:9.3 -->

“That’s not my view, Vaccha.”

<!-- @segment mn72:9.4 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:10.1 -->

“Then is this your view: ‘A realized one no longer exists after death. This is the only truth, anything else is futile’?”

<!-- @segment mn72:10.2 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:10.3 -->

“That’s not my view, Vaccha.”

<!-- @segment mn72:10.4 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:11.1 -->

“Then is this your view: ‘A realized one both still exists and no longer exists after death. This is the only truth, anything else is futile’?”

<!-- @segment mn72:11.2 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:11.3 -->

“That’s not my view, Vaccha.”

<!-- @segment mn72:11.4 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:12.1 -->

“Then is this your view: ‘A realized one neither still exists nor no longer exists after death. This is the only truth, anything else is futile’?”

<!-- @segment mn72:12.2 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:12.3 -->

“That’s not my view, Vaccha.”

<!-- @segment mn72:12.4 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:13.1 -->

“Worthy Gotama, when asked these ten questions, you say: ‘That’s not my view.’

<!-- @segment mn72:13.2 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:13.3 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:13.4 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:13.5 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:13.6 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:13.7 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:13.8 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:13.9 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:13.10 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:13.11 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:13.12 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:13.13 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:13.14 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:13.15 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:13.16 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:13.17 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:13.18 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:13.19 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:13.20 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:13.21 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:13.22 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:13.23 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:13.24 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:13.25 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:13.26 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:13.27 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:13.28 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:13.29 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:13.30 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:13.31 -->

Seeing what drawback do you avoid all these convictions?”

<!-- @segment mn72:14.1 -->

“Each of these ten convictions is the thicket of views, the desert of views, the twist of views, the dodge of views, the fetter of views. They’re beset with suffering, distress, anguish, and fever. They don’t lead to disillusionment, dispassion, cessation, peace, insight, awakening, and extinguishment.

<!-- @segment mn72:14.2 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:14.3 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:14.4 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:14.5 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:14.6 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:14.7 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:14.8 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:14.9 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:14.10 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:14.11 -->

Seeing this drawback I avoid all these convictions.”

<!-- @segment mn72:15.1 -->

“But does the worthy Gotama have any convictions at all?”

<!-- @segment mn72:15.2 -->

“A realized one has done away with convictions.

<!-- @segment mn72:15.3 -->

For a realized one has seen:

<!-- @segment mn72:15.4 -->

‘Such is form, such is the origin of form, such is the ending of form.

<!-- @segment mn72:15.5 -->

Such is feeling, such is the origin of feeling, such is the ending of feeling.

<!-- @segment mn72:15.6 -->

Such is perception, such is the origin of perception, such is the ending of perception.

<!-- @segment mn72:15.7 -->

Such are choices, such is the origin of choices, such is the ending of choices.

<!-- @segment mn72:15.8 -->

Such is consciousness, such is the origin of consciousness, such is the ending of consciousness.’

<!-- @segment mn72:15.9 -->

That’s why a Realized One is freed with the ending, fading away, cessation, giving up, and letting go of all conceiving, all churning, and all I-making, mine-making, or underlying tendency to conceit, I say.”

<!-- @segment mn72:16.1 -->

“But worthy Gotama, when a mendicant’s mind is freed like this, where are they reborn?”

<!-- @segment mn72:16.2 -->

“‘They’re reborn’ doesn’t apply, Vaccha.”

<!-- @segment mn72:16.3 -->

“Well then, are they not reborn?”

<!-- @segment mn72:16.4 -->

“‘They’re not reborn’ doesn’t apply, Vaccha.”

<!-- @segment mn72:16.5 -->

“Well then, are they both reborn and not reborn?”

<!-- @segment mn72:16.6 -->

“‘They’re both reborn and not reborn’ doesn’t apply, Vaccha.”

<!-- @segment mn72:16.7 -->

“Well then, are they neither reborn nor not reborn?”

<!-- @segment mn72:16.8 -->

“‘They’re neither reborn nor not reborn’ doesn’t apply, Vaccha.”

<!-- @segment mn72:17.1 -->

“Worthy Gotama, when asked all these questions, you say: ‘It doesn’t apply.’

<!-- @segment mn72:17.2 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:17.3 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:17.4 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:17.5 -->

I fail to understand this point, worthy Gotama; I’ve fallen into confusion.

<!-- @segment mn72:17.6 -->

And I’ve now lost even the degree of clarity I had from previous discussions with the worthy Gotama.”

<!-- @segment mn72:18.1 -->

“No wonder you don’t understand, Vaccha, no wonder you’re confused.

<!-- @segment mn72:18.2 -->

For this principle is deep, hard to see, hard to understand, peaceful, sublime, beyond the scope of logic, subtle, comprehensible to the astute.

<!-- @segment mn72:18.3 -->

It’s hard for you to understand, since you have a different view, creed, and belief, unless you dedicate yourself to practice with the guidance of tradition.

<!-- @segment mn72:18.4 -->

Well then, Vaccha, I’ll ask you about this in return, and you can answer as you like.

<!-- @segment mn72:19.1 -->

What do you think, Vaccha?

<!-- @segment mn72:19.2 -->

Suppose a fire was burning in front of you. Would you know:

<!-- @segment mn72:19.3 -->

‘This fire is burning in front of me’?”

<!-- @segment mn72:19.4 -->

“Yes, I would, worthy Gotama.”

<!-- @segment mn72:19.5 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:19.6 -->

“But Vaccha, suppose they were to ask you:

<!-- @segment mn72:19.7 -->

‘This fire burning in front of you: what does it depend on to burn?’ How would you answer?”

<!-- @segment mn72:19.8 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:19.9 -->

“I would answer like this:

<!-- @segment mn72:19.10 -->

‘This fire burning in front of me burns in dependence on grass and logs as fuel.’”

<!-- @segment mn72:19.11 -->

“Suppose that fire burning in front of you was extinguished. Would you know:

<!-- @segment mn72:19.12 -->

‘This fire in front of me is quenched’?”

<!-- @segment mn72:19.13 -->

“Yes, I would, worthy Gotama.”

<!-- @segment mn72:19.14 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:19.15 -->

“But Vaccha, suppose they were to ask you:

<!-- @segment mn72:19.16 -->

‘This fire in front of you that is quenched: in what direction did it go—

<!-- @segment mn72:19.17 -->

east, south, west, or north?’ How would you answer?”

<!-- @segment mn72:19.18 -->

“It doesn’t apply, worthy Gotama. The fire depended on grass and logs as fuel. When that runs out, and no more fuel is added, the fire is reckoned to have become quenched due to lack of fuel.”

<!-- @segment mn72:20.1 -->

“In the same way, Vaccha, any form by which a realized one might be described has been given up, cut off at the root, made like a palm stump, obliterated, and unable to arise in the future.

<!-- @segment mn72:20.2 -->

A realized one is freed from reckoning in terms of form. They’re deep, immeasurable, and hard to fathom,

<!-- @segment mn72:20.3 -->

like the ocean.

<!-- @segment mn72:20.4 -->

‘They’re reborn’, ‘they’re not reborn’, ‘they’re both reborn and not reborn’, ‘they’re neither reborn nor not reborn’—none of these apply.

<!-- @segment mn72:20.5 -->

Any feeling …

<!-- @segment mn72:20.6 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:20.7 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:20.8 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:20.9 -->

perception …

<!-- @segment mn72:20.10 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:20.11 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:20.12 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:20.13 -->

choices …

<!-- @segment mn72:20.14 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:20.15 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:20.16 -->



<!-- @segment mn72:20.17 -->

consciousness by which a realized one might be described has been given up, cut off at the root, made like a palm stump, obliterated, and unable to arise in the future.

<!-- @segment mn72:20.18 -->

A realized one is freed from reckoning in terms of consciousness. They’re deep, immeasurable, and hard to fathom,

<!-- @segment mn72:20.19 -->

like the ocean.

<!-- @segment mn72:20.20 -->

‘They’re reborn’, ‘they’re not reborn’, ‘they’re both reborn and not reborn’, ‘they’re neither reborn nor not reborn’—none of these apply.”

<!-- @segment mn72:21.1 -->

When he said this, the wanderer Vacchagotta said to the Buddha:

<!-- @segment mn72:21.2 -->

“Worthy Gotama, suppose there was a large sal tree not far from a town or village.

<!-- @segment mn72:21.3 -->

And because it’s impermanent, its branches and foliage, bark and shoots, and softwood would fall off.

<!-- @segment mn72:21.4 -->

After some time it would be rid of branches and foliage, bark and shoots, and softwood, pure, and consolidated in the core.

<!-- @segment mn72:21.5 -->

In the same way, worthy Gotama’s dispensation is rid of branches and foliage, bark and shoots, and softwood, pure, and consolidated in the core.

<!-- @segment mn72:22.1 -->

Excellent, worthy Gotama! …

<!-- @segment mn72:22.2 -->

From this day forth, may the worthy Gotama remember me as a lay follower who has gone for refuge for life.”
