<!-- @segment mn148:1.2 -->

At one time the Buddha was staying near Sāvatthī in Jeta’s Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s monastery.

<!-- @segment mn148:1.3 -->

<!-- @segment mn148:1.4 -->

<!-- @segment mn148:1.6 -->

<!-- @segment mn148:2.1 -->

“Mendicants, I shall teach you the Dhamma that’s good in the beginning, good in the middle, and good in the end, meaningful and well-phrased. And I shall reveal a spiritual practice that’s entirely full and pure, namely,

<!-- @segment mn148:2.2 -->

the six sets of six.

<!-- @segment mn148:2.3 -->

<!-- @segment mn148:10.1 -->

If anyone says, ‘the eye is self,’ that is not tenable.

<!-- @segment mn148:10.2 -->

<!-- @segment mn148:28.2 -->

<!-- @segment mn148:28.3 -->

<!-- @segment mn148:28.4 -->

<!-- @segment mn148:28.5 -->

the underlying tendency to aversion underlies that.

<!-- @segment mn148:28.6 -->

<!-- @segment mn148:28.7 -->

the underlying tendency to ignorance underlies that.
